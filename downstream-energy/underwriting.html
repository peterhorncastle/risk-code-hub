Key Rating Factors

Factor 01

Process Complexity

FCC/hydrocracking/cracking increases hazard vs simple crude distillation. Hydrogen service at high T/P = premium loading of 10–30%.

Factor 02

Process Safety Management Quality

OSHA PSM / COMAH compliance; HAZOP completion; Baker Panel adoption; incident history. Poor PSM = +15–40% loading or referral to specialist UW.

Factor 03

Asset Age & Condition

Plant vintage vs designed life; inspection compliance; ongoing capital investment. Deferred maintenance programmes = non-renewal or significant premium loading.

Factor 04

Fire Suppression & Detection

Fixed deluge, AFFF foam, heat detection, gas detection. Sub-standard fire water supply = +10–20% loading or fire sub-limit restriction.

Factor 05

Geographic / Nat-CAT Exposure

Gulf Coast windstorm/flood: +15–35% loading. Seismic zone (Japan, Turkey, California): +10–25%. Flood plain siting: sub-limit or exclusion.

Factor 06

Operator Profile

Major IOC (Shell/Exxon) vs NOC vs independent. NOC state-managed plants in sanctioned or politically unstable regions attract significant loadings.

Factor 07

Loss Record

5-year loss run mandatory. Premium rate: loss-free risks command minimum rates; frequency accounts +25–100% or declination.

Factor 08

BI Integration & Indemnity Period

Highly integrated complexes where a single unit shutdown affects total output have higher BI risk. 36-month IP attracts loading vs 12-month.

Factor 09

Hazardous Materials Inventory

HF alkylation, methyl isocyanate, ammonium nitrate, LPG storage. Specific high-hazard inventories sub-limited or excluded by many syndicates post-2019.

Factor 10

EAR vs OAR Phase

Construction/commissioning phase (EAR) typically higher rate than operational (OAR) due to heightened cold-start and testing risks. Commissioning period sub-limits common.

ALOR Methodology: Maximum Probable Loss (MPL/PML)

Downstream energy risks are underwritten on an ALOR (Anticipated Loss On Risk) / PML (Probable Maximum Loss) basis rather than on the full Total Insured Value (TIV). Unlike total physical loss scenarios common in other classes, the underwriter assesses the maximum likely loss from a single fire, explosion or Nat-CAT event affecting the most exposed unit(s) of the complex.

MPL (Probable Maximum Loss) = TIV × Loss Scenario Factor

For a major refinery complex (TIV $3bn):
— Single Unit Fire: MPL ≈ $200–400m (7–13% of TIV)
— Major VCE (e.g. FCC explosion): MPL ≈ $600m–1.2bn (20–40% of TIV)
— Total Loss (Nat-CAT flood): MPL ≈ $1.5–2.5bn (50–80% of TIV) — rare scenario

Premium = MPL × Rate on Line (RoL)
where RoL is expressed as % of MPL (not TIV)

The MPL is determined by the risk engineer's assessment of fire zones, blast radius, explosion containment and Nat-CAT exposure. Separate MPL estimates are produced for fire/explosion scenarios, Nat-CAT scenarios (windstorm, flood, EQ), and machinery breakdown scenarios. The largest of these drives the policy structure.

Worked Example 1: Large UK Refinery (EA/EB)

Stanlow-type UK Refinery — £2.8bn TIV

Hypothetical 200,000 b/d refinery, UK North West, operational 1950s, recent modernisation programme, strong PSM record, major IOC-managed. 24-month BI indemnity period. Loss-free 5 years.

Risk Parameters

Total Insured Value (TIV)£2,800,000,000
MPL — Fire/Explosion (FCC unit, major scenario)£560,000,000 (20% of TIV)
MPL — Nat-CAT (storm/flood, UK risk — lower exposure)£420,000,000 (15% of TIV)
Policy PD Limit£560,000,000 (= MPL)
Daily Gross Profit£3,500,000/day
BI MLOP (24 months × £3.5m/day)£2,520,000,000
BI Limit (24-month indemnity, ALOR scenario)£350,000,000

Premium Calculation

EA PD Base Rate (loss-free major IOC, strong PSM)0.18% on PD limit
EA PD Base Premium (0.18% × £560m)£1,008,000
Nat-CAT loading (UK — modest)+8% → +£80,640
MB endorsement+£120,000
EA PD Sub-total£1,208,640
EB BI Rate (on BI limit £350m)0.38% on BI limit
EB BI Premium£1,330,000
TOTAL ANNUAL PREMIUM (EA + EB)~£2,538,000

Lloyd's line: typically 5–15% of programme. Lead line at 10%: £253,800. Full programme placed across 8–12 syndicates + international co-insurers.

Gulf Coast LNG Terminal — $4.5bn TIV

Hypothetical 2-train LNG liquefaction terminal, Texas Gulf Coast, 10 years operational, processing 2.5bcf/day. Strong safety record. Hurricane Harvey damage-free (elevated design). 24-month BI indemnity period. Freeport LNG event pricing reflected.

⚠️ BI Underwriting Alert: The LNG Commodity Price Problem

LNG terminal BI is particularly complex to underwrite because the daily revenue loss is driven by LNG spot prices, which can vary 5–10× over a 24-month indemnity period. The Freeport LNG outage in 2022 coincided with European gas prices at record highs ($70/mmBtu in some markets) — a "loss-in-a-loss" scenario where the insured lost revenue at precisely the moment prices were highest.

Underwriters must decide at inception whether to cover the BI on: (a) a fixed notional price (conservative — avoids price risk); (b) a market index price (fair but complex — requires commodity risk expertise); or (c) the insured's contractual price under their LNG SPAs (Sales and Purchase Agreements). Most large LNG BI policies now use SPA contract prices as the basis, with a commodity price cap/collar.
