Downstream / Onshore Energy

Insurance for refineries, petrochemical plants, pipelines, LNG terminals, gas processing and onshore power generation. The highest individual-risk values in the Lloyd's market — single assets routinely exceeding $5bn in Total Insured Value. Catastrophe exposure (Nat-CAT, fire, explosion, BI) drives underwriting discipline more than any other energy class.

What This Class Covers

🏭

Property Damage (PD)

Physical loss or damage to plant, equipment, structures, stock and inventory from any insured peril including fire, explosion, flood, storm and equipment breakdown.

📉

Business Interruption (BI)

Loss of revenue/gross profit following an insured PD event. Downstream BI values can dwarf PD — a major refinery can lose $1–5m/day in gross margin during outage.

⚙️

Machinery Breakdown (MB)

Sudden and unforeseen mechanical or electrical breakdown of plant not caused by an insured PD peril. Typically endorsed on to operational policies.

🌊

Nat-CAT

Wind, flood, earthquake and storm surge affecting onshore plant. Gulf Coast refineries face particular hurricane exposure; flood increasingly in scope globally.

🔥

Fire & Explosion

The dominant peril in downstream energy. Process fires and VCEs (vapour cloud explosions) generate the largest individual losses in the Lloyd's market.

🔧

Construction / EAR

Erection all risks for new-build or major modification projects. Covers equipment damage during installation, testing and commissioning phases.

🛢️

Pipeline & Storage (EF)

Physical damage to pipelines, terminals, pumping stations and associated equipment. Third-party pollution liability often excluded but endorsed separately.

⚡

Power Generation (PG)

Turbine and boiler all risks, CCGT combined cycle plant, transformer damage. Large transformers (EHV) can represent the largest BI exposure on a single item.

⚠️ Underwriter Alert: Accumulation & Nat-CAT Aggregation

The Gulf Coast of the United States is the world's single largest concentration of downstream energy assets — over 45% of US refining capacity within a 200-mile arc. A single major hurricane (e.g. Category 5 landfall near Houston) could trigger simultaneous PD+BI losses across dozens of policies in a Lloyd's portfolio.

Underwriters are required to monitor Gulf Coast accumulation at peer review and to apply Nat-CAT loadings of 15–35% on exposed risks. Post-Harvey (2017) the market introduced tighter flood sub-limits and mandatory 72-hour deductibles on Gulf Coast refinery risks.

How Downstream Energy Fits in Lloyd's

Downstream energy (EA/EB) and pipeline (EF) codes account for roughly 4–6% of Lloyd's total premium by market estimates. Combined with power generation (PG), the onshore energy cluster is one of the highest average-premium classes — individual placements routinely exceed $10–50m in annual premium for complex multinational programmes.

The class is heavily reinsurance-dependent: most Lloyd's syndicates writing downstream retain only 5–15% of large risks, ceding the majority to the international reinsurance market. This makes pricing discipline and cat modelling critical — adverse development from a single large loss can materially affect syndicate results for multiple years.

Class Context: EA vs EB vs EF vs PG

EA (Onshore All Risks / EAR): The cornerstone code. Covers the physical plant of refineries, petrochemical complexes, LNG terminals and gas processing. Combines construction-phase EAR and operational OAR. Average TIVs exceed $2bn; the largest complexes (Saudi Aramco, Shell Pernis, ExxonMobil Baytown) exceed $15–20bn.

EB (Onshore Business Interruption): Written as a combined PD+BI package or standalone BI. The BI section is underwritten separately with its own MLOP (Maximum Loss of Production) calculation. BI can be 2–5× the PD value for highly integrated plants.

EF (Pipeline): The specialist pipeline code. Requires a detailed line-list submission identifying each pipeline segment, diameter, contents, pressure and third-party crossing risk. Cross-country pipelines can present massive accumulation exposures — a single major trunk line rupture and fire can exceed $500m.

PG (Power Generation – Onshore): Covers conventional generation assets — gas turbines, steam turbines, CCGTs, coal plants and large diesel installations. Mechanical breakdown and transformer damage are the dominant perils. Nuclear is rarely written in the Lloyd's market (specialist pools exist).

Key Underwriting Considerations

Downstream energy underwriting requires deep technical knowledge of process engineering. Underwriters must understand how a loss at one unit can cascade through an integrated plant — for example, a fire at a crude distillation unit (CDU) can shut an entire refinery for 6–18 months while rebuilding, generating BI losses many times the original PD quantum.

The most critical single risk factor in downstream underwriting is the quality of the Process Safety Management (PSM) programme operated by the insured. US OSHA PSM regulation (29 CFR 1910.119) and EU COMAH (Control of Major Accident Hazards) provide regulatory frameworks, but compliance varies significantly. Underwriters should request PSM audit reports, PHA (Process Hazard Analysis) findings and MOC (Management of Change) procedures as standard.
