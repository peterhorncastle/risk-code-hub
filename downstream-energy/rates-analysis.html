Drivers of Current Market Dynamics (2024–2026)

The downstream energy market in 2025–2026 is in a period of relative stability following the significant hardening triggered by Harvey (2017) and the subsequent series of mid-size losses (Husky Superior, PES Philadelphia, Freeport LNG, Colonial Pipeline cyber). The key dynamics shaping pricing are:

Inflationary pressure on rebuild costs: Steel, piping, electrical equipment and construction labour costs increased 30–50% between 2020 and 2024. Many policies written at 2018–2020 TIVs are now underinsured. Underwriters are pushing for annual TIV indexation clauses on downstream risks.

LNG boom creating new premium: Post-Ukraine energy crisis, European energy security spending has driven a wave of new LNG import terminal construction (UK, Germany, Netherlands, Italy) and US LNG export terminal expansions. This is creating significant new premium volume in EA/EB for LNG construction and operational risks.

Energy transition bifurcation: Traditional refineries face stranded asset risk — insurers are increasingly cautious about long-term commitments to refinery risks scheduled for decommissioning. Deferred maintenance on "transition assets" is creating a deteriorating loss experience on aged conventional downstream risks.

Cyber exclusions hardening: Post-Colonial Pipeline (2021) and Nord Stream sabotage (2022), the scope of cyber and sabotage exclusions has tightened significantly. Many EF pipeline policies now explicitly exclude cyber-triggered physical damage, creating coverage gaps that are increasingly debated at renewal.
