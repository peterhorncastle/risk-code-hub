The Hierarchy of Downstream Risk Control

Downstream energy risk mitigation operates at multiple levels — regulatory compliance, technical engineering controls, operational management systems and emergency response. Underwriters assess all four levels, but place greatest weight on the quality of the Process Safety Management (PSM) programme, which is the single greatest determinant of long-run loss experience.

🏛️

Regulatory Compliance

OSHA PSM (US), COMAH (EU/UK), SEVESO III, EPA RMP — minimum baseline requirements. Compliance necessary but not sufficient; some of the worst losses occurred at "compliant" facilities.

⚙️

Engineering Controls

Inherently safer design, pressure relief systems, fire and gas detection, fixed suppression, emergency shutdown (ESD) systems. Hardware controls are the primary line of defence.

📋

Process Safety Management

HAZOP, PHA, LOPA, MOC, PTW, incident investigation. PSM is the management system that sustains engineering controls over the asset life. Quality of PSM is the underwriter's primary risk indicator.

🚒

Emergency Response

On-site fire brigade, mutual aid agreements, ERP quality, spill response plan. Response capability determines loss extent once an incident has occurred — mitigation of consequence.

Process Safety Management (PSM) — Key Elements for Underwriters

The US OSHA PSM Standard (29 CFR 1910.119) and UK/EU COMAH regulations define the minimum elements of a PSM programme. Underwriters should assess compliance with these elements and evaluate which are most relevant to the specific risk profile of the facility.

Mechanical Integrity: Key Inspection Requirements

⚠️ High-Temperature Hydrogen Attack (HTHA) — Critical Underwriting Issue

HTHA is the insidious degradation of carbon steel exposed to high-temperature hydrogen-rich environments (above ~260°C). The damage is invisible until catastrophic failure — the hydrogen reacts with carbon in the steel, forming methane bubbles at grain boundaries that cause fissuring and sudden rupture.

The 2010 Tesoro Anacortes explosion (7 deaths, $100m loss) was caused by HTHA in a heat exchanger that had been in hydrogen service for decades without adequate inspection. API 941 (Nelson Curves) defines the maximum operating temperature for different steel alloys in hydrogen service — underwriters should confirm that all heat exchangers in hydrogen service above 260°C are operating within their Nelson Curve limit and are subject to AUT (automated ultrasonic testing) inspection at minimum every 4 years.

Fire and Explosion Prevention — Engineering Controls

  • Gas Detection: Fixed combustible gas detectors (LEL basis) around all vapour-generating equipment; H₂S detectors in sour service areas; actionable alarm/shutdown interlocks
  • Emergency Shutdown (ESD) Systems: Layer-of-protection analysis (LOPA) completed for all major hazard scenarios; ESD system functional safety assessment (IEC 61511); regular ESD trip testing
  • Explosion Proof / Area Classification: Hazardous area classification drawings current and accessible; electrical equipment in Zone 0/1/2 certified to ATEX/IECEx
  • Spacing and Separation Distances: IP 15 (Model Code of Safe Practice) / NFPA 30 minimum separation distances between process units, storage tanks and control buildings; blast-rated control room construction
  • VCE Blast Walls and Control Room Siting: Control rooms within blast radius of major hazard scenarios should be blast-rated to withstand 0.3 bar peak overpressure. Texas City — 15 deaths in unprotected temporary offices within VCE blast radius
  • Ignition Source Control: Vehicle exclusion zones; hot work permit system; earthing and bonding of hydrocarbon containers; non-sparking tools policy
  • HF Alkylation Unit Management: Post-PES Philadelphia 2019, underwriters requiring specific risk engineering assessment for HF alkylation units. Some syndicates applying sub-limits or exclusions for HF alkylation units
  • Tank Farm Design: Independent Overflow Prevention (IOP) installed on all tanks > 10,000 m³ capacity (post-Buncefield); bund capacity ≥110% of largest tank; stormwater discharge valve management

Fixed Fire Suppression — Standards Assessment

Key Fire Protection Standards in Downstream Energy

NFPA 11

Low, medium and high expansion foam systems. AFFF application rates for hydrocarbon fires at petroleum storage tanks.

NFPA 15

Fixed water spray systems for fire protection. Deluge system design for process equipment cooling and suppression.

NFPA 30

Flammable and combustible liquid storage code. Tank spacing, bunding, overflow prevention requirements.

API 2021

Management of atmospheric storage tank fires. Fire pre-plans, foam specification, cool-down rates.

IP Model Code Part 19

UK/European petroleum industry fire protection standards. Spacing and separation, fire water supply rates.

FM Global DS 7-32

FM Global data sheet for petroleum processing. The gold standard for risk engineering assessment used by FM Global Property Loss Prevention.

IEC 61511

Functional safety standard for Safety Instrumented Systems (SIS). LOPA methodology for determining required SIL ratings for ESD systems.

COMAH SI 2015

UK Control of Major Accident Hazards Regulations. Operator Safety Report requirements for COMAH upper tier sites (refineries, chemical plants).

Nat-CAT Resilience — Gulf Coast Focus

Post-Harvey, Gulf Coast downstream risk mitigation has become a priority area for both operators and underwriters. Key engineering measures to reduce windstorm and flood vulnerability include:

  • Flood Elevation: Critical equipment (switchgear, control rooms, power transformers) elevated above 500-year flood level. Harvey demonstrated that 100-year flood standards were wholly inadequate in the Houston petrochemical corridor.
  • Winterisation (Winter Storm Uri 2021 Lesson): Instrument heat tracing on all outdoor instruments in freeze-susceptible regions; winterisation audits annually pre-Q4; emergency generator fuel supply for 72-hour minimum blackout
  • Hurricane Pre-Shutdown Procedures: Documented storm pre-shutdown procedures; inventory drawdown programmes (reduce flammable inventory before hurricane); pre-positioned fire foam supplies
  • Flood Barriers: Deployable floodgates around critical electrical infrastructure; sump pump redundancy; bund integrity assessment for flood overtopping scenarios
  • Flood Elevation Certificate: Post-Harvey, Lloyd's lead syndicates require a formal FEMA-certified flood elevation certificate for all Gulf Coast refinery and petrochemical sites at renewal. Without it, flood cover is typically sub-limited to 25–50% of PD limit.

Pipeline-Specific Risk Mitigation (EF)

  • Inline Inspection (ILI) / Smart Pig Runs: PHMSA requires ILI or equivalent for all HCA (High Consequence Area) pipeline segments. Minimum ILI interval: 7 years. Post-Colonial Pipeline, operators are accelerating to 5-year intervals. ILI data should include metal loss, dent detection and crack detection in a single run.
  • Cathodic Protection (CP): Impressed current CP systems monitored monthly; close-interval potential surveys annually; CP rectifier output records provided to underwriter on request
  • Third-Party Damage Prevention: One-call centre membership; right-of-way patrols (aerial or ground) weekly minimum for onshore pipelines; depth-of-cover surveys at road/river crossings
  • Leak Detection: SCADA-based computational pipeline monitoring (CPM) with sensitivity of ≥5% of flow rate; response time ≤4 hours; volume balance systems at pumping stations
  • Cyber Security for OT/SCADA Systems: Post-Colonial Pipeline ransomware (2021), cyber exclusions in EF policies typically exclude damage from cyber attack. Operators should implement IEC 62443 controls, network segregation between IT and OT, and incident response plans for pipeline SCADA systems.
