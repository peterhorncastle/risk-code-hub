Product recall insurance remains a relatively small but rapidly growing segment of the broader specialty insurance market. The global market was estimated at approximately $1.2 billion in gross written premium (GWP) in 2024, with food and beverage representing the single largest segment by premium volume (estimated 35–45% of total, given the sector's frequency and severity characteristics).

Market growth is being driven by several structural factors: increasing global recall frequency (3,200+ events recorded in 2024), growing regulatory stringency in the US, EU, and Asia-Pacific, heightened consumer and investor sensitivity to food safety events, and expanding distribution of food products globally. These factors collectively increase both the probability and severity of recall exposures.

Loss Ratio Analysis

Product recall insurance has historically been a relatively profitable line for carriers who have maintained underwriting discipline. The food and beverage sector exhibits a distinctive loss pattern: high frequency of small-to-medium recall events (generating attritional losses) punctuated by occasional large or catastrophic events that can dramatically impact loss ratios in any given year.

Key insight: In years with major catastrophic recall events, aggregate loss ratios for exposed portfolios can exceed 100%, pushing the market into technical underwriting loss. This is why disciplined underwriters maintain diversification across risk categories (food vs. pharma vs. automotive) and manage accumulation of exposure in high-hazard product categories.

Multi-year perspective: Across a typical 5-year cycle, a disciplined underwriter should expect approximately 2–3 favourable years, 1–2 moderate years, and 0–1 catastrophic year. On an annualised basis, a well-managed product recall portfolio targeting an average loss ratio of 45–50% should deliver a sustained ROE of 20–40% on deployed capital — making it one of the more attractive specialty lines in terms of risk-adjusted return.

📊 2026 Market Outlook

The December 2025 baby formula recalls (Nestlé / Danone / Lactalis) represent a potential market-defining event for the product recall segment. With estimated aggregate losses of up to $1.54 billion across three major brands simultaneously, this event will test the aggregate claims experience of insurers with multiple infant nutrition exposures.

For 2026, the market is expected to harden significantly in the infant nutrition category (rate increases of 30–50%+ expected). Underwriters are likely to introduce aggregation sub-limits for shared ingredient/co-packer supply chain risks. New capacity that entered the market in 2024–2025 may retreat or re-price materially, particularly for high-hazard product categories.

For well-run food manufacturers in medium-hazard categories (e.g., ambient, beverage, packaged snacks), rates should remain broadly stable as competitive pressure from newer market entrants persists. The WTW Insurance Marketplace Realities 2026 report is expected to confirm rate increases of 10–25% for the broader food recall market and 30–50%+ for infant nutrition.
