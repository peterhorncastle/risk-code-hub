1. Confidence Badge Framework

Every factual claim in this hub carries an inline confidence badge. The framework is consistent across all Risk Code Hub modules (introduced v2.0).

VER — Verified

Sourced from primary documentation: UN treaty text, FCC/ITU official filings, NASA/ESA press releases, peer-reviewed space engineering literature, or Lloyd's market circulars. High confidence.

EST — Estimate

Derived from market reports (Swiss Re sigma, Marsh/Aon annual reviews), broker presentations, industry conferences, or cross-referenced secondary sources. Directionally reliable; not primary-sourced.

ILLUS — Illustrative

Constructed for training purposes to demonstrate underwriting or programme concepts. Based on plausible market parameters but NOT actual placement data. Do not cite commercially.

GAP — Data Gap

Identified area where reliable public data is unavailable. Noted at source to signal where professional judgement or confidential market data is required.

2. Knowledge Cutoff & Currency

⏱️ Knowledge Cutoff: May 2025

This hub was compiled using information available to Claude (Anthropic) through May 2025. Space insurance is a fast-moving market — the following categories of information may be materially out of date and should be verified against current sources before professional use:

  • Launch vehicle reliability statistics (Falcon 9, Ariane 6, New Glenn, Starship flight histories)
  • Mega-constellation satellite counts (Starlink, OneWeb/Eutelsat, Amazon Kuiper)
  • FCC and OFCOM regulatory developments (5-year deorbit implementation, new licence conditions)
  • Solar Cycle 25 activity data and space weather events post-May 2025
  • Market rate movements, Lloyd's market participants, syndicate capacity changes
  • Viasat-3, ViaSat-3 Americas partial loss claim final settlement figures
  • New ASAT developments, debris creation events
  • Active debris removal (ADR) technology deployments (Astroscale ADRAS-J status)

5. Data Gap Disclosures

Identified areas where public data is insufficient to support VER-level claims. Professional market sources are required for these items.

GAP 01 — Lloyd's Space Market GWP by Code

Lloyd's does not publish granular GWP breakdowns by risk code (SC/SL/SO). The total space market GWP (approximately $700–750m globally) is a Swiss Re and Marsh estimate. The Lloyd's share cannot be precisely stated from public sources. Used where referenced: Overview (Module 01), Rates Analysis (Module 06).

GAP 02 — 1998–1999 Aggregate Loss Totals

The commonly cited figure of ~$2.5–3.5bn aggregate losses in 1998-99 is based on market practitioner estimates in broker reviews and conference presentations, not audited Lloyd's or reinsurer accounts. The figure may include non-insurance losses. Used where referenced: Overview (Module 01), Timeline (Module 03).

GAP 03 — Actual Launch Rate Benchmarks (Current)

Launch Rate on Line data is commercially sensitive and not published by operators or underwriters. The benchmarks in Module 05 (Falcon 9: 1.5–3%, Ariane 6: 3–6%, etc.) are derived from broker market commentary, practitioner interviews and secondary sources including aviation/aerospace trade press. Actual rates will differ by operator relationship, satellite value, and market conditions at placement.

GAP 04 — Viasat-3 Americas Final Settlement

The Viasat-3 Americas partial loss (2022 deployment anomaly) was reported in media as approximately $420m insured value claim. The final insurance settlement figure is confidential. The loss is included in the database at the reported insured value figure, which may differ from the settled claim amount.

GAP 05 — Mega-Constellation Blanket Insurance Pricing

No public data is available on actual premium rates or coverage structures for Starlink, OneWeb or Amazon Kuiper constellation covers. SpaceX is believed to self-insure Starlink; OneWeb/Eutelsat's insurance programme is not publicly disclosed. The constellation tier framework in Module 08 is entirely illustrative.

GAP 06 — Combined Ratio Data (Space Market, Historical)

Space market combined ratios as presented in Module 06 are reconstructed estimates based on public loss event data and estimated GWP from market reports. Actual combined ratios are not published by Lloyd's or major reinsurers at the space code level. These figures should be treated as illustrative of market cycle direction, not as accounting data.

6. Methodology & Disclaimer

This hub was compiled by Claude (Anthropic) as a training resource for Lloyd's market insurance professionals. It is intended to support the education of trainee underwriters and is not a substitute for professional market guidance, primary treaty/regulatory texts, or syndicate-specific underwriting authorities.

Space insurance is a specialist class. Rates, limits, wordings and appetite vary significantly between Lloyd's syndicates and across the market cycle. Any figures marked ILLUS are constructed for illustrative/training purposes and must not be used as reference rates for actual placements. Figures marked EST are estimates from secondary or market commentary sources and carry meaningful uncertainty.

This resource is maintained as a living document. Users are encouraged to verify all time-sensitive data (satellite counts, launch reliability statistics, regulatory rules, market rates) against current sources. The Risk Code Hub carries a version date — refer to the build tracker for the current version.
