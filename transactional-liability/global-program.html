TL Programme Tower

Transactional liability coverage is structured in layers, from top-layer contingent indemnity down to the buyer's first-loss retention. Each layer serves a distinct purpose within a multi-layered programme.

Layer 1: Contingent Risk / Specific Indemnity (CD/CE)

Limit: £/$5–200m
Per-risk cover for identified contingencies | Claims-made, 3–10 years | Covers specific identified risks (e.g., litigation, regulatory action, environmental liability)

Layer 2: Tax Liability XL (TA/TB — Upper Layer)

Limit: £/$5–50m excess of £/$5m
Aggregate or per-occurrence excess | 5–10 year term | Covers excess tax liability exposure above primary tax insurance layer

Layer 3: Tax Liability Primary (TA/TB)

Limit: £/$2–10m primary layer
Known risk (TA) or contingent tax (TB) | Claims-made, 5–10 years | Covers identified tax exposures or contingent tax positions

Layer 4: W&I / R&W — XL Layer

Limit: 15–30% of EV excess of primary
Large/complex deal excess layer | 5–7 years | Follows primary W&I, protecting against catastrophic warranty claim exposure

Layer 5: W&I / R&W — Primary Layer

Limit: 10–20% of EV
Core buyer protection | NBI standard | 5–7 years | Main warranty and representation protection covering all standard reps

Layer 6: SIR / Retention

Limit: 0.5% EV (PE standard)
Buyer's first loss | No insurance coverage | Buyer absorbs all losses below retention level
Programme Structure Note: Programmes are structured per-transaction. Not all layers are used in every deal. Tax and contingent risk policies are placed separately from W&I and may involve different insurers. The tower above represents a complex, multi-layered acquisition; smaller transactions typically use only primary W&I + retention.

Deal Structure Cards — Multi-Jurisdiction Deals

Four common structures for cross-border M&A transactions:

Cross-Border M&A — "Wrap" Structure

Single global W&I policy "wrapping" all jurisdictions in the deal. Master policy governed by English law; per-jurisdiction endorsements for local law variations. Common for: European multi-country deals, APAC acquisitions with UK holding structure. Advantage: Single insurer relationship; consistent coverage; simpler claims process. Challenge: Local law variations may require specific local endorsements.

US R&W + Non-US W&I

Largest cross-border deals often have US R&W policy (US-domiciled insurer; New York or Delaware law) for US entity representations alongside Lloyd's W&I for non-US assets. Driven by US regulatory requirements and buyer preference for US-admitted paper. Essential: Coordination clause needed between policies to avoid double-recovery or gap.

Tax Spine — Multi-Jurisdiction

Large multinational acquisitions may require separate TA/TB policies for each key tax jurisdiction. Example: UK group with German subsidiary and Irish IP holding company → three separate tax insurance placements. Lloyd's facility covers all three on shared terms. Each policy is stand-alone with separate tax counsel opinion per jurisdiction.

Seller-Side Transaction Cover (D9)

Less common but used when seller needs to cap indemnity exposure. Seller purchases D9 policy — insurer backs seller's obligations to buyer. Often used: PE fund returning capital to LPs (cannot retain reserves); listed company with litigation risk from warranty claims; family-owned business sale where seller cannot provide long escrow. Retention sits with seller; insurer covers excess.

Captive & Alternative Structures

Emerging Risk Transfer Models: Large repeat transactors (active PE funds, corporate acquirers with >5 deals/year) increasingly use captive fronts, programme facilities and multi-deal frameworks to optimise cost, speed and claims control:
  • Annual facility / multi-deal programme: Pre-agreed terms and wording for all deals within defined parameters. Binding authority at Lloyd's. Reduces transaction time from 10–14 days to 2–5 days per deal.
  • Captive fronting: Buyer captive provides first layer (SIR); Lloyd's fronts primary W&I above captive retention. Improves ROI on captive capital; maintains claims-control.
  • Synthetic R&W / NBI facility: Facility agreement pre-establishes NBI terms; seller gives nil indemnity at signing; insurer bound without individual underwriting (within pre-agreed parameters).
  • Parametric tax cover: Fixed payout on confirmed adverse tax ruling — avoids proving loss quantum. Emerging for specific HMRC/IRS assessment scenarios.

ILLUS Pan-European PE Buyout — 5-Country Technology Group

Deal Overview

PE fund acquires software/technology group with operations in UK, Germany, Netherlands, France and Sweden. EV = €420m. Seller = PE fund exiting after 5-year hold. NBI structure required.

Insurance Programme

W&I primary (D8): €84m limit (20% EV) | English law | Lloyd's | 6-year | 0.5% EV retention (€2.1m) | Premium: €84m × 2.6% = €2.184m
W&I XL (D8): €42m xs €84m | Lloyd's following line | 6-year | €0.45m
UK Tax TA (HMRC R&D credit position): £8m | 5-year | Premium: £8m × 4.0% = £320k
German Trade Tax TB (GmbH restructuring): €5m | 5-year | Premium: €5m × 5.0% = €250k
Dutch IP Holding TA: €12m | 5-year | Premium: €12m × 3.5% = €420k

Total estimated programme cost: ~€3.6m ILLUS

Lloyd's Market Advantages

  1. Largest W&I capacity globally: Single syndicate lines up to £50m; lead + follow structure for larger limits
  2. Speed: Lloyd's box market allows same-day indicative terms for familiar deal types
  3. Cross-class: TL alongside D&O (financial lines) under coordinated programme
  4. Claims expertise: Financial lines claims specialists; average W&I claim resolved 12–18 months
  5. Tax/contingent: Specialist TA/TB/CD/CE capacity developed over 30 years — few non-Lloyd's markets can match depth
