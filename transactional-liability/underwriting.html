Policy Basis & Claims-Made Structure

All TL products are written on a claims-made basis. Coverage applies when a claim is first made against the insured during the policy period, regardless of when the underlying breach occurred. Policy period typically 3–7 years for W&I; 5–10 years for tax and contingent risk. Retroactive date = completion date of the transaction. Pre-inception known issues are EXCLUDED.

W&I/R&W Rating Framework

W&I Premium = Policy Limit × Base Rate × Deal Factor × Jurisdiction Factor × Sector Factor

Where Base Rate ≈ 2–4% of policy limit (European deals); 2.5–4.5% (US/R&W)

Six Rating Factor Cards

1. Deal Type

PE buyout 1.0× | Strategic M&A 1.05–1.15× | Distressed/special 1.25–1.5× | Public-to-private 1.1–1.25× | Carve-out 1.15–1.3×

PE deals have thorough DD processes; carve-outs have incomplete financial history.

2. Jurisdiction

UK 1.0× | Northern Europe 1.0–1.1× | Southern Europe 1.2–1.35× | US/Canada 1.0–1.15× | Asia-Pacific 1.25–1.5× | Emerging markets 1.4–1.8×

Legal system quality, disclosure standards, courts sophistication.

3. Sector

General/diversified 1.0× | Technology 1.1–1.3× | Healthcare/pharma 1.2–1.4× | Financial services 1.3–1.5× | Real estate 0.9–1.1× | Manufacturing 0.95–1.05×

Complex regulatory environments, IP/data concentration, financial services regulatory exposure.

4. Deal Size

Sub-£50m EV 1.3–1.5× | £50–250m EV 1.0–1.2× | £250m–1bn EV 0.95–1.1× | £1bn+ EV 0.9–1.05×

Large deals have proportionally more DD investment; small deals may have lighter process.

5. Retention

Standard: 0.5% of EV (European PE) | 0.75–1.0% of EV (strategic/larger) | Minimum floors: £/$250k–500k. NBI: retention sits with insurer — seller gives nil indemnity. Retention reduces moral hazard; lower retention = higher premium.

6. Policy Limit

Typical W&I: 10–30% of Enterprise Value | Small deals: often 100% of deal value | US R&W: typically 10–15% of deal value | Aggregate cap including sub-limits for specific reps (financial statements, tax, etc.).

NBI Structure Explainer

No-Box Indemnity (NBI) — The Modern Standard

Traditional structure: Seller indemnifies buyer, with indemnity capped (the "escrow box") and insurance backing the seller.

NBI structure: Seller gives nil/£1 indemnity cap — buyer's SOLE recourse for warranty breach is the W&I policy. Insurer steps into seller's shoes. Benefits: Seller returns 100% capital to LPs at close; clean exit; no post-deal disputes between buyer and seller. Insurer becomes counterparty to buyer. Standard in European PE; increasingly adopted in US R&W.

Tax Liability Underwriting (TA/TB)

Distinct from W&I — tax insurer assesses probability of adverse tax outcome:

  • Tax counsel opinion required (generally "more likely than not" / 50%+ threshold)
  • Coverage only for risks where outcome is UNCERTAIN — known, certain liabilities excluded
  • Premium: typically 3–7% of policy limit (higher than W&I due to binary/specific risk)
  • Sub-types: TA (known identified risk — e.g., specific HMRC query, filed position under challenge) vs TB (broader uncertain position — e.g., transfer pricing methodology, R&D credit eligibility)
  • Key underwriting questions: has HMRC/IRS been notified? Any ongoing enquiry? Counsel opinion strength? Statutory limitation period?

Worked Examples

Example 1 — PE Buyout, UK Tech Company (W&I)

Deal Structure

£120m EV software business; 100% share acquisition; PE buyer, financial seller (PE fund)

Policy structure: Limit = £24m (20% EV); Retention = £600k (0.5% EV); NBI structure; 5-year policy

Rating Calculation

Base rate: 3.0% of limit

Adjustments: UK jurisdiction (1.0×); PE deal, good DD (1.0×); Tech sector (1.15×)

Premium Calculation

£24m × 3.0% × 1.15 = £828,000 ILLUS

Key Rep Categories Covered

Share title; financial statements; tax; employment (including contractors/IR35); IP (software ownership, open-source, data); material contracts; compliance/GDPR

Key Exclusions

Forward-looking projections; pension deficit; environmental; cyber incidents post-close; disclosed IP disputes

Example 2 — TA Tax Insurance, UK Group Relief

Risk Scenario

Target company has £8m of pre-acquisition losses claimed as group relief; HMRC challenging timing of loss crystallisation

Tax counsel opinion: "More likely than not" (55% probability) that HMRC challenge fails

Policy Structure

Type: TA insurance; Limit: £8m (full tax exposure); Premium: 4.0% of limit

Premium Calculation

£8m × 4.0% = £320,000 ILLUS

Coverage Provided

HMRC assessment, interest and penalties on challenged group relief; legal defence costs; negotiated settlement

Exclusion

Fraud; deliberate misrepresentation to HMRC; future group relief claims on same structure
