Confidence Badge Legend

This hub uses four standardised confidence levels to indicate the sourcing and verification status of each claim. Always check the badge before relying on a specific statistic or structure.

VER

Verifiable — Drawn from primary sources, regulatory filings, or widely reported public records. Information is independently verifiable or derived from official documents (e.g., Lloyd's regulatory announcements, government tax guidance, published court decisions).

EST

Estimated — Market consensus or derived from broker/adviser reports; reasonable range but not independently confirmed. Reflects industry best practice and broker estimates but not independently audited or disclosed by individual firms.

ILLUS

Illustrative — Worked examples using plausible but hypothetical figures for educational purposes. Deals, premium calculations, and programme structures are realistic but not actual transaction data. Use as reference framework only.

GAP

Data Gap — Information not available; cross-reference with primary sources before relying on this claim. This hub has identified known gaps in publicly available data. Always verify current market terms with a specialist W&I broker.

Knowledge Cutoff: May 2025

Transactional liability is a rapidly evolving market. Premium rates, retention levels, claims frequency and regulatory developments change frequently. Key areas most likely to have changed since cutoff:

  • Pillar Two UTPR implementation specifics
  • PFAS contingent risk volumes
  • AI representation policy language
  • US R&W market conditions post-rate-cycle

Always verify current market terms with a specialist W&I broker before making underwriting or purchase decisions.

Data Gap Disclosures

The following information gaps have been identified within this hub. Before relying on any of the figures below in professional or underwriting contexts, always cross-reference with a specialist W&I broker:

  • TL GWP by Code: Lloyd's does not publish GWP split between D8/D9/TA/TB/CD/CE. Market estimates of ~$3–4bn W&I GWP and ~$1–1.5bn tax insurance GWP are broker-derived and should be treated as indicative. GAP
  • W&I Claims Frequency: Individual policy claims data is not publicly disclosed. The ~20–25% claims notification rate for 2019–21 vintage is derived from broker surveys and industry conference presentations — not independently verified. GAP
  • Average Paid Claim: The ~$6–8m average paid W&I claim figure is market estimate from broker reports. Actual averages vary significantly by deal size, jurisdiction and claim type. GAP
  • Tax Insurance Claims: Tax insurance claims data is almost entirely confidential. TA/TB claim amounts in the database are reconstructions from public HMRC/IRS proceedings and academic research — individual policy claims are not disclosed. GAP
  • NBI Adoption Date: The precise year NBI became "market standard" is debated. Various industry sources cite 2010–2014 as the transition period — this hub uses 2012 as the generally cited inflection point. GAP
  • Pillar Two UTPR Insurance: As of May 2025 knowledge cutoff, UTPR-specific TA/TB policy language was still developing. Coverage terms, exclusions and premium rates for Pillar Two risks may have evolved materially since cutoff. GAP
