Market Cycle Phases

1. Niche/Formation (1990–1999)

Limited competition; premium 8–12% of limit; narrow coverage; product restricted to large UK/European deals. Few claims data points.

2. PE Adoption (2000–2007)

European PE drives volume; premium falls to 4–6%; coverage broadens; Lloyd's market GWP grows to ~£300m. Soft market driven by competition.

3. GFC Claims Reality (2008–2012)

First wave of claims; financials reps sublimited; premium rises 20–30%; some capacity exits; market re-underwriting.

4. NBI Revolution (2013–2019)

NBI structure expands market dramatically; retention compresses; premium falls to 2–2.5% by 2019; US R&W competition intensifies; Lloyd's faces margin pressure.

5. COVID Hardening (2020–2022)

Claims spike; pandemic exclusions; ESG/cyber rep exclusions added; premium rises 15–25% from trough; retention floors raised.

6. Stabilisation (2023–2026)

Claims management matures; market stable; premium 2.5–3.5%; capacity broadly adequate; Pillar Two and PFAS create new sub-product demand.
