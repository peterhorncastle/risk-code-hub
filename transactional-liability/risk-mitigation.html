Due Diligence Quality Framework

1. Financial Due Diligence

Quality of Earnings (QoE) report from independent Big 4/top-tier adviser. Normalised EBITDA reconciliation. Working capital peg methodology. Balance sheet liabilities review. Net debt definition.

Key control: Avoid management-only accounts; require 3+ years audited financial statements; W&I insurers heavily weight QoE report quality.

2. Legal Due Diligence

Material contracts review (top 10–20 customers, key suppliers). Litigation search — active claims, threatened disputes, settled matters last 5 years. Regulatory compliance — sector-specific licences, pending investigations.

Key control: IP ownership audit — registered and unregistered IP, software licences, open-source audit. Employment review — senior management contracts, TUPE, works council.

3. Tax Due Diligence

3–5 year tax return review. Open enquiries and correspondence with HMRC/IRS. Transfer pricing documentation. R&D tax credit methodology. Group structure — loss utilisation, withholding tax positions.

Key control: Post-acquisition tax structuring — Step-up elections, push-down accounting. Clean tax diligence = lower TA/TB premium.

4. IT & Cybersecurity Due Diligence

Software ownership and licence audit. GDPR/data privacy compliance assessment. Cybersecurity maturity review (ISO 27001 or equivalent). Data breach history and notification obligations.

Key control: Cloud/SaaS contractual obligations. Critical for tech-sector W&I premium.

Rep Negotiation Best Practices

Key principle: The scope and quality of warranties/representations in the SPA directly determines what W&I covers. Underwriters review every rep.

1. Broad vs Narrow Reps

Buyers prefer broad, unqualified reps (easier to claim). Sellers prefer knowledge qualifications ("to the best of the seller's knowledge") and materiality thresholds.

W&I best practice: Insurers prefer broad, clearly scoped reps with well-defined knowledge and materiality qualifications — ambiguity creates claims disputes. Agree rep scope before binding W&I policy.

2. Knowledge Qualifiers

"Actual knowledge" — hardest to claim; "actual knowledge after enquiry" — middle ground; "should have known" — broadest.

US R&W: "actual knowledge of the Seller's Knowledge Group" (defined persons). W&I insurers typically cover objective rep breach regardless of seller's actual knowledge.

3. Materiality Thresholds

Financial reps often have de minimis thresholds (e.g., "no liability exceeding £100,000 not disclosed"). These flow through to W&I coverage — claims below threshold may not trigger coverage.

Watch: Aggregated threshold vs per-item threshold.

4. Disclosure Letter Quality

Disclosure letter qualifies reps — anything disclosed is EXCLUDED from W&I. Full disclosure = clean SPA but reduces W&I coverage scope. Inadequate disclosure = broader W&I but more seller liability.

Key: Insurer reviews disclosure letter in full during underwriting.

Tax Risk Controls (TA/TB)

1. Pre-Transaction Tax Clearances

Seek HMRC/IRS advance clearance where available (UK: HMRC Non-Statutory Clearance; US: IRS Private Letter Ruling). Clearance = certain outcome = uninsurable but eliminates risk.

Outcome: Partial clearance = scope for TA insurance on remaining uncertain elements.

2. Transfer Pricing Documentation

OECD BEPS Action 13 three-tier documentation (master file, local file, CbCR). Contemporaneous documentation = strongest defence. Review arm's length comparables. Benchmark inter-company pricing.

Red flag: Lack of TP documentation = automatic amber/red flag for TB insurers.

3. Limitation Periods

UK: 4-year normal assessment period; 6-year (careless); 20-year (deliberate). US: 3-year standard; 6-year (25%+ understatement); unlimited (fraud).

Key: Tax insurance policy term should cover applicable statute of limitations. Check for extended limitation periods in target jurisdictions.

4. Tax Counsel Opinion

Required for all TA/TB placements. Standard thresholds: "more likely than not" (>50%) = insurable; "should" (>65%) = preferred; "will" (>85%) = usually uninsurable as near-certain.

Quality check: Opinion must be non-reliance waived to insurer. Counsel with HMRC/tax authority expertise preferred.

Top 10 Risk Mitigation Actions

  1. Commission full Big 4 Quality of Earnings report — most important W&I underwriting factor
  2. Ensure 3+ years audited financial statements in VDR before binding policy — minimum data requirement
  3. Seek HMRC/IRS advance clearance on identified tax uncertainties before placing TA/TB insurance — reduces tax risk premium
  4. Conduct IP ownership audit including open-source software scan before closing — critical tech-sector requirement
  5. Obtain GDPR/data privacy compliance assessment — now standard W&I requirement for all sectors
  6. Review all open litigation and regulatory correspondence; disclose fully in disclosure letter — avoids coverage disputes
  7. Engage W&I broker early (pre-signing) — insurers need 10–14 days minimum for review
  8. Negotiate broad, well-defined reps with clear knowledge qualifications in SPA — shapes insurance coverage scope
  9. For carve-outs/divisions — ensure 3-year standalone financial history — or W&I premium will load significantly
  10. Maintain contemporaneous transfer pricing documentation to OECD BEPS Action 13 standard — best defence in tax disputes
