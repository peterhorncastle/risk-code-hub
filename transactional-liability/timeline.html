Timeline of Key Events

1990
1990
First Lloyd's W&I Policy
First warranty & indemnity policy written in the London market for a continental European M&A transaction. Product niche, high-priced (~10% of limit), narrow coverage. EST
1994
1994
Uruguay Round / TRIPS Agreement
WTO TRIPS Agreement standardises IP protections globally, increasing value of IP representations in M&A — driving early demand for IP rep coverage in W&I. VER
1998
1998
BVCA Best Practice Guidelines
British Venture Capital Association publishes M&A best practice guidelines referencing W&I insurance as standard risk management tool for PE exits in UK. Catalyst for European PE adoption. EST
2002
2002
Sarbanes-Oxley Act (US)
SOX introduces CEO/CFO certification of financial statements; increases financial rep accuracy standards. Drives demand for financial statement rep coverage in W&I. VER
2007
2007
Record Pre-GFC M&A Peak
Global M&A reaches ~$4.8tn — PE buyout frenzy; W&I written on peak valuations. Lloyd's TL market GWP estimated ~$300–400m. Seeds of future claims. EST
2008
2008
Global Financial Crisis
M&A deal volumes collapse ~60%. W&I claims emerge as inflated valuations unravel — financial statement and EBITDA representation breaches dominate. First systematic claims data for W&I market. VER
2010
2010
First Significant W&I Claims Paid
Lloyd's syndicates pay first significant W&I claims ($5–30m range); market adjusts — financial statement reps sublimited or excluded; underwriting criteria tightened. EST
2012
2012
NBI Structure Emerges
"No-box indemnity" (nil seller indemnity cap) becomes standard in European PE transactions. Sellers can return 100% of deal proceeds to LPs at closing. Structural shift — W&I moves from supplementary to primary recourse. EST
2014
2014
US R&W Market Inflection
US representations & warranties market scales rapidly. AIG, Tokio Marine, Liberty Mutual expand dedicated R&W teams. Deal count grows from ~300 to ~800 in a single year. Premium rates compress to 2.5–3.5% of limit. EST
2017
2017
US Tax Cuts & Jobs Act
TCJA introduces GILTI, BEAT, FDII — immediate demand surge for TA/TB tax insurance covering historic and transitional US tax positions in cross-border M&A. VER
2018
2018
GDPR Enforcement Begins
EU GDPR takes effect May 2018. Data privacy representations become standard in W&I; cyber/data exclusions added by many insurers. First data rep claims emerge 2019–20. VER
2019
2019
Record European W&I Market
European W&I market estimated at ~$1.5–2bn GWP — Lloyd's dominant. ~90% of European PE deals use W&I. Retention at historic low (0.5% EV); premium ~2–2.5% of limit. EST
2020
2020
COVID-19 M&A Collapse
Pandemic causes ~$300bn of announced deals to collapse or be renegotiated. W&I/R&W claims spike on MAC reps, revenue warranties and supply chain reps. Insurers add pandemic exclusions. VER
2021
2021
Global M&A Record Year
Global M&A reaches ~$5.8tn — all-time record. SPAC boom: 613 SPACs raise ~$162bn. W&I/R&W deal count estimated 5,000+. Lloyd's TL market GWP exceeds $1bn for first time. VER
2021
2021
SPAC Forward-Looking Rep Problem
De-SPAC mergers include projection representations (unusual for W&I). SEC scrutiny of SPAC forecasts triggers first W&I/D&O crossover claims. Insurers exclude or sublimit SPAC projection reps. VER
2022
2022
Rate Hardening & Cyber Rep Exclusions
Premium rates rise 15–25%; cyber/data privacy reps excluded from standard W&I; retention floors increase. ESG/greenwashing representations added to SPA and W&I submissions. EST
2023
2023
All-Time High Claims Frequency
2020–21 vintage policies enter 3-year claims window. Claims frequency reaches record levels — ~20–25% of policies generate at least one notification. Financial statement reps remain #1 cause (~40%). Average paid claim ~$6–8m. EST
2023
2023
OECD Pillar Two Goes Live
OECD Pillar Two global minimum tax (15%) effective for accounting periods from Jan 2024. Creates immediate demand for TA/TB insurance covering deferred tax positions, UTPR exposure and transitional safe harbours in multinational M&A. VER
2024
2024
PFAS Contingent Risk Surge
EPA finalises PFAS MCL rules (April 2024). Chemical, automotive and textile M&A generates large CE/CD contingent risk demand. Several $50–200m PFAS contingency policies bound at Lloyd's. VER
2024
2024
AI Representations Emerge
Generative AI use by target companies creates novel IP/copyright representation risk in W&I. Insurers develop AI-specific warranty language and exclusions. First AI-related rep warranty claims begin to emerge. EST
