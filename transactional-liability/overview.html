Coverage Types

D8 — Buyer W&I

Protects buyer if seller breached representations or warranties in SPA. Nil recourse to seller (NBI structure). Limit typically 10–30% of deal value. Retention: 0.5–1.0% of EV. Premium 2–4% of limit. Covers: financial statement accuracy, title to shares/assets, compliance, IP ownership, tax, employment, environmental.

D9 — Seller W&I

Less common. Allows seller to cap liability under indemnity. Insurer steps in for valid claims. Used when seller needs clean exit (PE funds returning capital). Often structured as "back-to-back" with D8.

TA — Tax Liability: Known

Covers specific identified tax risks documented in due diligence. Examples: step-up elections, deferred tax disputes, group loss utilisation, VAT on property. Underwritten against tax counsel opinion. Indemnity structure; typically £5–100m per risk.

TB — Tax Liability: Contingent

Broader uncertain tax positions. Transfer pricing challenges by HMRC/IRS. R&D tax credit exposure. Cross-border withholding tax disputes. State/local tax nexus issues. Often multi-year run-off coverage.

CD — Contingent Liability

Known legal/regulatory contingency that could materialise. Examples: pending litigation, regulatory investigations, IP infringement claims, employment class actions. Requires legal assessment and probability opinion.

CE — Contingent Risk: General

Catch-all for unresolved SPA risks. PFAS/environmental contamination contingency. Pension deficit contingency. Earn-out dispute cover. Post-close regulatory approvals.

Transaction Structure

Typical W&I Deal Flow

  1. SPA negotiation: Seller and buyer agree on representations, warranties, and indemnity mechanics.
  2. W&I broker appointed: Deal team engages specialist broker to structure and market insurance.
  3. NDA + underwriting submission: Broker obtains executed SPA, VDR access, and due diligence reports.
  4. Insurer reviews: Underwriter scrutinises SPA, VDR, reports; identifies coverage questions.
  5. Q&A with deal advisers: Underwriter and buyer counsel/advisers clarify rep scope, exclusions, baskets.
  6. Policy bound at/before closing: Insurer issues binding quote; policy takes effect at or just before closing.
  7. Buyer holds policy (NBI structure): Buyer owns policy; seller has no rights or recourse to insurer.

Key concept: No-box indemnity (NBI): seller gives nil or minimal indemnity cap; buyer's sole recourse is insurer.

Key Underwriting Considerations

Critical Factors in W&I & Tax Liability Underwriting

  • Claims-made basis: Coverage triggered at time of claim notification (not occurrence). Policy must remain in force at claim time.
  • SPA quality and rep scope: Narrow, specific reps easier to underwrite; broad "basket catch-alls" attract higher retention or exclusions.
  • VDR completeness and adviser quality: Well-organised data rooms and credible advisers (Big 4 accounting, top-tier law) reduce underwriting uncertainty.
  • Deal type (PE vs strategic): PE deals typically smaller retention, faster underwriting. Strategic deals larger, more complex reps.
  • Jurisdiction (US, UK, Europe, ROW): US deals highest premium (~3–4%); UK/Europe moderate (~2.5–3%); emerging markets higher (~4–5%+).
  • Industry sector: Tech, healthcare, pharma attract higher rates due to IP/regulatory complexity. Manufacturing/basic industries lower.
  • Deal size: Large deals ($500m+) better pricing; smaller deals ($50–150m) attract higher rates due to fixed underwriting cost.
  • Retention negotiation: Brokers target 0.5–1.0% EV for European PE; US market floor typically $500k–$1m regardless of deal size.
  • Exclusions for disclosed/known issues: Any risk item disclosed pre-close or documented in VDR typically excluded from coverage (unless specifically insured as TA/TB/CD).
