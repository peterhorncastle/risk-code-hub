EPLI Reserve Development: A Shorter Tail Than D&O

Unlike D&O securities class actions which can take 4–7 years to settle, the majority of individual EPLI claims resolve within 18–36 months. EEOC charge-to-right-to-sue letter typically takes 6–18 months; subsequent litigation typically settles within 12–24 months of filing. This shorter tail means EPLI reserves are less sensitive to investment return assumptions — but also means less investment income opportunity compared to longer-tail lines.

The exception is class action EPLI, which mirrors D&O in its development pattern. The Goldman Sachs pay equity class action (filed 2010, settled 2022) ran for 12 years. Class action reserves should be treated as long-tail and held at conservative loss development assumptions. Underwriters who price class action exposure as short-tail will systematically under-reserve.

IBNR (Incurred But Not Reported) is a significant issue for EPLI. Employees who experience discrimination or harassment may not file an EEOC charge immediately — the Lilly Ledbetter Act means pay discrimination charges can be filed years after the original act, and the 180-day filing window resets with each paycheck. Late-reported EEOC charges on multi-year pay discrimination patterns require substantial IBNR allowances.
