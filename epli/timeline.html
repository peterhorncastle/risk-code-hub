1986Supreme Court
Meritor Savings Bank v. Vinson — Harassment Defined
Supreme Court unanimously holds that sexual harassment creating a hostile work environment violates Title VII. Employer liability doctrine for supervisor harassment established. This decision is the foundation for the multi-billion dollar sexual harassment EPLI sub-class.
1991Statutory
Civil Rights Act 1991 — EPLI Market Created
Introduces compensatory and punitive damages and jury trial rights for Title VII intentional discrimination. Within 24 months, the first standalone EPLI policies appear. AIG, Chubb and Lloyd's syndicates launch initial products. The market begins with Fortune 500 clients at high premiums.
1991Cultural Shift
Anita Hill / Clarence Thomas Hearings
Senate confirmation hearings televised nationally. Anita Hill's testimony about workplace harassment raises public awareness dramatically. EEOC sexual harassment charges nearly double between 1991 and 1993. Corporate HR departments begin investing in formal harassment policies for the first time.
1994Major Settlement
Denny's Restaurants — Racial Discrimination
Denny's settles two racial discrimination class actions for $54.4m — at the time the largest civil rights settlement under Title II and Title VII. Evidence of systematic denial of service to Black customers alongside discriminatory employment practices. Reputational damage triggers major corporate culture reforms.
$54.4m settlement
1998Supreme Court
Faragher v. Boca Raton & Burlington Industries v. Ellerth
Twin Supreme Court decisions establish the Faragher/Ellerth affirmative defence: an employer can escape or limit vicarious liability for supervisor harassment if (1) it exercised reasonable care to prevent and correct harassment, and (2) the employee unreasonably failed to use the employer's complaint procedures. These decisions become the template for EPLI risk mitigation — driving investment in anti-harassment policies and training programmes worldwide.
1998Major Settlement
Mitsubishi Motors — Sexual Harassment Class Action
Mitsubishi Motors' Normal, Illinois plant settles EEOC sexual harassment class action. A pattern of systematic harassment of women on the factory floor, combined with corporate retaliation against complainants, produced a landmark settlement. One of the first major EPLI class actions to establish that factory-floor culture claims could reach corporate D&O and board accountability.
$34m settlement
2000Major Settlement
Coca-Cola — Racial Discrimination Class Action
Coca-Cola settles racial discrimination class action by Black employees alleging systematic pay, promotion and performance review discrimination. At the time the largest civil rights settlement in US corporate history. Settlement included a task force with unprecedented power to monitor and direct HR practices for four years. Triggered industry-wide pay equity analysis initiatives.
$192.5m settlement
2004Major Settlement
Morgan Stanley — Gender Discrimination (Schieffelin)
Morgan Stanley settles sex discrimination class action brought by Allison Schieffelin and other female bond saleswomen alleging glass ceiling discrimination in promotions and compensation. Consent decree included monitoring and mandatory reporting. Glass ceiling claims in financial services become a key underwriting concern for D6 financial institution risks.
$54m settlement
2009Statutory
Lilly Ledbetter Fair Pay Act
First legislation signed by President Obama. Resets the 180-day EEOC filing window with each discriminatory paycheck — overturning the Supreme Court's Ledbetter v. Goodyear (2007) decision. Dramatically extends the potential tail of pay discrimination claims. Equal pay analysis becomes a standard EPLI underwriting requirement for large D6 risks.
2010International
UK Equality Act 2010
Consolidates and harmonises nine major UK anti-discrimination statutes. Introduces protected characteristics framework (age, disability, gender reassignment, marriage, pregnancy, race, religion, sex, sexual orientation). No cap on discrimination awards (unlike unfair dismissal). Enhanced victimisation protections. Key D7 statutory framework for all UK employment risks.
2017Major Settlement
Sterling Jewelers / Signet — Gender Pay Class Action
Signet Jewelers settles pay equity and sexual harassment class action by approximately 70,000 current and former female employees. Evidence of systematic pay discrimination and a culture of sexual harassment by male managers. One of the largest EPLI class actions in jewellery retail history. EPLI rates for retail sector increase sharply in 2017–2018.
$175m settlement
2017Cultural Shift
#MeToo Movement
Harvey Weinstein exposés by New York Times and New Yorker (October 2017) trigger a global reckoning. Within 90 days, EEOC sexual harassment charges surge; major entertainment, media, and tech companies face concurrent claims. EPLI rates increase 30–50% in affected sectors. Underwriters impose new standalone harassment warranties, mandatory training requirements, and executive conduct disclosures. Fox News had already settled Roger Ailes claims for $20m+ and Bill O'Reilly claims for $32m+ before the broader movement emerged.
2019Major Settlement
McDonald's CEO Easterbrook — Misconduct & Clawback
McDonald's terminates CEO Steve Easterbrook for consensual relationships with employees, violating company policy. McDonald's later sues Easterbrook for $105m clawback after discovering he had concealed further relationships. Case illustrates: (1) EPLI / D&O overlap at senior executive level; (2) clawback provisions as an underwriting tool; (3) board's duty to investigate misconduct allegations independently of management.
$105m clawback (2020)
2020Supreme Court
Bostock v. Clayton County — LGBTQ+ Protection
Supreme Court 6-3: Title VII's prohibition on sex discrimination covers sexual orientation and gender identity. An employer who fires an employee for being gay or transgender violates Title VII. Immediately adds a fully established new EPLI claim vector affecting ~5% of the US workforce. EPLI policies must now expressly cover LGBTQ+ claims under the sex discrimination umbrella.
2020Regulatory / EEOC
COVID-19 — FMLA & ADA Retaliation Surge
Mass furloughs across hospitality, retail, aviation, and travel trigger the largest single-year EEOC charge volume for wrongful termination and retaliation since 2008–2009. FMLA retaliation claims by workers needing COVID-related or caregiver leave surge. ADA accommodation failures for employees with COVID-related disabilities generate multi-employer class actions. EEOC issues guidance on COVID-related EPLI obligations.
2022Major Settlement
Google — Gender Pay Equity Class Action
Google settles class action by approximately 15,500 current and former female employees in California alleging systematic pay discrimination and barriers to promotion. Settlement includes independent monitoring and mandatory pay equity audits. Technology sector EPLI rates, already elevated post-#MeToo, remain firm. Pay transparency laws passed in California, New York, Colorado, and Illinois in 2022–2023 directly follow from this litigation wave.
$118m settlement
2023International
Australia — Sex Discrimination Positive Duty Obligation
Australian Sex Discrimination Act amendments introduce a positive duty requiring employers to proactively eliminate sexual harassment, sex discrimination and victimisation — rather than merely responding to complaints. Australian Human Rights Commission gains new compliance powers. D7 underwriting for Australian risks requires evidence of proactive prevention measures, not merely reactive complaint procedures.
2024Regulatory
EEOC Guidance on AI Hiring Tools
EEOC issues guidance clarifying that employers are liable under Title VII for discriminatory outcomes of AI hiring and performance management tools, regardless of whether the employer designed the tool. Resume screening algorithms, video interview AI, and automated scoring tools that produce disparate impact are a Title VII violation. Algorithmic bias becomes a new EPLI underwriting factor — underwriters begin asking about AI tool usage and bias auditing.
