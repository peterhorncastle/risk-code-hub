Coverage Overview — R2 (Operational) and R4 (Construction)

💨

Onshore Wind — Physical Damage

Turbines, towers, blades, nacelles, gearboxes, generators, transformers, substation, grid connection cables. Lightning, storm, mechanical/electrical breakdown.

☀️

Solar PV — Physical Damage

Modules, mounting/racking, inverters, combiners, cables, substation. Perils: hail (primary), fire, flood, wind, PID degradation, module manufacturing defect.

🔋

BESS — Physical Damage

Battery modules, battery management systems (BMS), inverters, containers, thermal management. Thermal runaway, DC arc flash, fire propagation between cells/racks.

🌿

Biomass & Hydro

Fuel storage, boiler, turbine, generator for biomass. Civil structures, turbine/runner, penstock, dam for hydro. Fire (biomass) and flood/debris (hydro) primary perils.

📉

ALOR — Advanced Loss of Revenue

Revenue loss following physical damage. Sum insured: P50 annual revenue × (indemnity months / 12). Typical waiting period: 30–60 days (wind), 45 days (solar). Basis: CfD, PPA, or merchant.

🏗️

R4 — Contract Works / EAR

Erection All Risk (EAR) for construction phase. All works at peak TIV including turbines/panels pre-commissioning. DSU coverage for delayed commissioning following physical damage.

🛡️

Third-Party Liability

Bodily injury and property damage to third parties. Includes ice throw (wind), panel debris (solar), BESS explosion blast/fire spread to adjacent property. Typically £25–250m per occurrence.

🔧

Machinery Breakdown

Sudden and accidental breakdown of machinery components not covered under standard property. Gearbox, generator, transformer, inverter. Often sub-limited (e.g., £10m/turbine gearbox).

⚡ BESS — The Emerging Market Challenge

Battery Energy Storage Systems are the fastest-growing risk class in onshore renewable energy insurance. The global BESS market has grown from ~5GW in 2018 to over 200GW in 2025 — and the insurance market is still calibrating exposure. Thermal runaway events — where heat generation within a cell cascades uncontrollably across an entire battery pack — have produced multi-million-pound losses at APS Arizona (2019), Victorian Big Battery (2021), and Moss Landing, California (2025, est. $700m+).

Key underwriting considerations: battery chemistry (NMC vs LFP vs NaS), thermal management system specification, fire suppression adequacy, container inter-spacing, BMS quality, and grid connection type. Rates for BESS risks are 2–5× those for equivalent solar PV TIV due to the severity of thermal runaway fire losses.

Market Context — Why This Class Matters

Onshore renewable energy (R2/R4) is now one of the largest and fastest-growing classes in Lloyd's by total insured value. Global solar PV alone has grown from under 10GW installed in 2007 to over 1.6TW by 2025 — a 160× increase in 18 years. The pace of new installations (350GW added in 2023 alone) means Lloyd's underwriters are pricing risks on technologies that did not exist a decade ago, in geographies with limited loss history, against a backdrop of climate change increasing severe weather frequency.

Three structural shifts define the current R2/R4 market: (1) US hail exposure has emerged as the dominant solar loss driver, with losses estimated at £1bn+ annually across the class; (2) BESS thermal runaway has created a new accumulation risk that aggregates with adjacent solar and wind assets; (3) the Inflation Reduction Act (2022) has driven a US capacity surge that is rapidly expanding the addressable premium pool but concentrating exposure in hail-prone states.
