Rating Factors by Technology

Onshore renewable rating factors vary significantly by technology sub-class. Each factor below can move the base rate materially — BESS chemistry alone is worth a 2–4× rate multiplier.

💨 Wind — OEM & Turbine Model

OEM loss history, turbine model, installed fleet size, and known serial defect exposure. GE 1.5MW (ageing fleet) +15–25% loading. First-of-kind turbine models +30–50%. Vestas and Siemens Gamesa modern platforms closest to base rate.

💨 Wind — Turbine Age

Age loading applies beyond 10 years. Bearing failure rates increase with age. 10–15 years: +10–15%. 15–20 years: +25–40%. 20+ years: individual engineering survey required. CMS installation can reduce age loading by 10–15%.

💨 Wind — Lightning Exposure

Ground flash density (GFD) at site drives lightning loading. High-GFD sites (e.g., Florida, central Europe, tropical): +20–35%. IEC 61400-24 lightning protection compliance reduces loading. Blade lightning receptor service records required.

☀️ Solar — Hail Zone

ANSI/SPRI hail zone classification for US sites. Zone 1–2 (low hail): base rate. Zone 3–4 (moderate): +30–50% + hail deductible. Zone 5 (Texas/Oklahoma): +80–120% + 3–5% TIV hail deductible + hail sub-limit. Dual-glass module specification: −20–30% hail loading.

☀️ Solar — Module Technology

Module type affects PID, fire, and hail risk profile. Monofacial crystalline silicon: standard. Bifacial mono-PERC (most common): hail loading unless dual-glass rear. Cadmium telluride (First Solar): different thermal performance; lower hail vulnerability. Thin film: lower fire risk.

☀️ Solar — Tracker vs Fixed-Tilt

Single-axis tracker systems (dominant in flat-terrain US solar) add mechanical complexity and storm/hail vulnerability (trackers at intermediate stow angle exposed to hail). Fixed-tilt systems simpler; lower O&M risk. Tracker stow protocol for severe weather is an underwriting requirement.

🔋 BESS — Battery Chemistry

Chemistry is the primary BESS rating driver. NMC (nickel-manganese-cobalt): highest thermal runaway risk; base BESS rate ×2.0–4.0 vs NMC benchmark. LFP (lithium iron phosphate): lower thermal runaway risk; rate ×1.0 (benchmark). NaS (sodium-sulfur): high operating temperature; specialist assessment required.

🔋 BESS — Thermal Management

Active liquid cooling: preferred. Passive air cooling: +20–30% loading. Thermal management system must include remote temperature monitoring with automatic isolation. BMS quality (response time to thermal event) is critical — systems with sub-second thermal isolation trigger: base rate. >5 second response: +30–50%.

🔋 BESS — Fire Suppression & Separation

Active fire suppression (FM200, IG-541, or equivalent) within battery containers: required. Container-to-container separation ≥3m: required. Thermal barrier walls between container clusters: −15% loading where fitted. Open-air rack-mounted BESS (no containment): specialist assessment, typically +50–80%.

🌿 Biomass — Fuel Storage

Wood pellet spontaneous combustion is primary attritional risk. CO monitoring in enclosed fuel stores: required. Temperature alarm system: required. Moisture content monitoring: required (>10% moisture content = high risk). Outdoor vs indoor storage: outdoor lower risk. Annual fire survey required for risks >£50m TIV.

📍 Location — Windstorm / CAT

Sites in hurricane zones (US Gulf/Atlantic coast): mandatory CAT deductible (5% TIV min). European windstorm zones (Pyrenees, Atlantic coast): additional windstorm loading. Wildfire proximity (US West, Australia): vegetation management plan required; defensible space of 30m minimum.

📋 O&M Quality

In-house O&M with manufacturer-trained technicians: base rate. Third-party ISP (Independent Service Provider): +5–10%. No formal O&M contract: +20–30%. CMS data sharing with insurer: −10%. Annual inspection records provided: −5%. Condition-based maintenance programme: −10–15%.

ALOR Methodology — Onshore Renewable

ALOR Sum Insured Calculation

Formula: ALOR SI = P50 Annual Revenue × (Indemnity Period Months ÷ 12)

The revenue basis depends on whether the asset operates under a subsidy mechanism:

  • CfD (Contract for Difference) — UK: Revenue = P50 generation (MWh) × CfD strike price (£/MWh). Highly predictable; low revenue risk. Indemnity period typically 12 months.
  • PPA (Power Purchase Agreement) — US/EU: Revenue = P50 generation (MWh) × PPA contracted rate (£/MWh). Fixed price; revenue risk low during PPA term. Indemnity period 12–18 months.
  • Merchant (no subsidy): Revenue = P50 generation (MWh) × forward power price (£/MWh) at time of policy. Volatile; sum insured may need annual review. Indemnity period 12 months (longer periods require basis clause).
  • BESS grid services: Revenue = contracted capacity payment (£/MW/year) + energy arbitrage revenue. Highly bespoke; requires grid services contract review.

Waiting period: 30–60 days for wind; 45 days for solar; 30 days for BESS. Claims paid on days 31–(indemnity period end).

⚡ BESS Underwriting — Key Additional Requirements (post-Moss Landing)

Following the Moss Landing catastrophe (2025), Lloyd's syndicates active in BESS have introduced additional mandatory requirements for all new and renewing BESS risks:

  • Battery chemistry declaration — NMC, LFP, NaS, or other; rate applied accordingly
  • Thermal management system specification (active liquid cooling preferred; passive air cooling — additional survey)
  • Container-to-container physical separation ≥3m (or equivalent thermal barrier)
  • BMS auto-isolation response time <1 second from thermal event detection
  • Remote 24/7 thermal monitoring with escalation protocol to emergency responders
  • First-responder pre-planning and dedicated BESS emergency response procedure
  • Maximum per-location BESS TIV: £200m (most syndicates); larger BESS require co-insurance tower
  • Grid connection protection: dedicated protection relay with anti-islanding; grid fault isolation time <0.5 seconds

Information Requirements — R2 Renewal Submission

  • Asset details: site name, location (lat/long), installed capacity (MW), TIV, commissioning date
  • Technology: OEM/manufacturer and model for turbines, modules, inverters, battery
  • 5-year loss history including near-misses and warranty claims
  • O&M contract details: provider, scope, response time SLA
  • Annual inspection and blade/module condition reports
  • CMS/SCADA data — alert history for wind; soiling and clipping data for solar; temperature logs for BESS
  • ALOR: P50 annual generation (MWh), revenue mechanism (CfD/PPA/merchant), contracted rate (£/MWh)
  • For BESS: battery chemistry, thermal management specification, BMS specification, fire suppression details
  • For solar (US): hail zone classification, module specification (dual-glass or monofacial), tracker stow protocol

⚠ US Solar — Hail Deductible Must Be Disclosed to Insured

A 3% TIV hail deductible on a 300MW solar farm represents £9.3m of retention per storm event. This is a material deductible that must be clearly communicated and agreed with the insured at placement — not buried in policy conditions. Underwriters should confirm the insured has adequate financial reserves or a captive to fund the retention. Several disputes have arisen where solar farm owners were unaware of the quantum of their hail deductible until a total-loss hail event occurred.
