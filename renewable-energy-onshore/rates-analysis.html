ROE Scenarios — R2/R4 Portfolio (April 2026)

Three scenarios based on a £100m GWP onshore renewable portfolio (50% wind, 30% solar, 20% BESS by premium) with £180m capital deployed. Reflects current rate environment post-IRA surge and Moss Landing repricing.

Bull Case

17.8%

Low cat year (no major US hail season), BESS losses within pricing assumptions, wind attritional at 50% LR. Combined ratio 74%. ROE: 17.8% on £180m capital.

Base Case

7.2%

Average US hail season, one moderate BESS event (£30m), wind stable. Combined ratio 88%. ROE: 7.2% on £180m capital. Meets Lloyd's minimum return threshold.

Bear Case

−18.4%

Major US hail season (£800m industry loss), large BESS event (£200m industry), GE bearing cluster. Combined ratio 148%. ROE: −18.4% on capital. Plausible worst-case 2026 scenario.

Key message: The R2/R4 class is emerging from its worst pricing period (2015–2019 solar trough, 2019–2023 BESS repricing delay) and approaching technical adequacy in most sub-classes for the first time. Wind remains the most stable sub-class. US solar and BESS remain the primary volatility drivers — a single severe hail season or major BESS catastrophe can push the class into significant net loss even at current rates. Underwriters with disciplined geographic and technology concentration limits are best positioned to generate sustainable returns.
