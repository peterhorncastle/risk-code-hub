Section A: MIG/FM Underwriting

MIG Premium Rating Formula
MIG Premium = LTV Excess Amount × Base Rate × Property Risk Factor × Portfolio Adjustment

Rating Components

LTV Excess: Amount of loan above lender threshold (e.g. above 75%) VER
Base Rate: 0.25–2.5% of excess per annum EST
Property Risk Factor: 0.8–1.5× (property type, location, new-build premium) EST
Portfolio Adjustment: 0.85–1.2× (lender quality, arrears history, mix) EST

LTV Band

75–80%: Standard rate, minimal premium.

80–90%: Elevated risk; rate loading 1.2–1.5×.

90–95%: High risk; rate loading 1.8–2.5×. Tighter lender approval.

Property Type

Standard residential: 1.0× factor.

New-build: 1.1–1.3× premium (completion risk, defect risk).

Flats & ex-RTB: 1.2–1.5× (collective leasehold risk, older defects).

Borrower Quality

Income verification: PAYE vs self-employed; recent payslips required.

Credit score: Minimum floor typically 620–680.

Affordability: Stress-rate test; debt-to-income ratio limits.

Regional Market

London/SE: Lower long-run default; 0.85–0.95× factor.

Northern regions: Higher price volatility; 1.0–1.2× factor.

Portfolio limits: Geographic concentration caps (e.g. max 30% in single region).

Section B: Title Insurance (TT) Underwriting

Title insurance is fundamentally different from risk-rated products: premiums are one-time, paid at close, and based on property or loan value rather than annual risk assessment.

Title Insurance Premium Formula
Title Premium = (Property or Loan Value / 1,000) × Rate per $1,000

Rate Guidance

Owner's policy (USA): $3.00–6.00 per $1,000 (varies by state) VER
Lender's policy (USA): $1.50–4.00 per $1,000 VER
UK premium: typically 0.1–0.5% of property value EST
Simultaneous issue discount: typically 20–30% when both purchased together VER

Title Risk Assessment

Risk Factor Low Risk Higher Risk
Title history Clean registered title Unregistered, multiple transfers
Property type Standard freehold residential Leasehold, listed building, flying freehold
Transaction type Straightforward purchase Remortgage after recent sale, developer transfer
Fraud indicators Verified ownership, physical inspection Remote transaction, urgent sale, below-market price
Planning/building regs Fully permitted Retrospective permissions, extensions without consent
Cladding/BSA 2022 EWS1 certificate obtained No EWS1, unknown cladding type
UK-Specific Title Risks
• Conveyancing fraud: Wire transfer hijacking, spoofed emails from solicitors, diverted payments
• Leasehold reform uncertainty: Leasehold reform bill (2024) introduces new statutory rights; existing deed restrictions may become unenforceable
• Restrictive covenant defects: Historic covenants (e.g. "no trade use") may reactivate with new owner; indemnity insurance required
• Missing planning consents: Extensions or conversions executed without consent; enforcement action possible up to 10 years
• Chancel repair liability: Ancient obligation to fund parish church repairs; affects older properties in some regions
• HS2 compulsory purchase zones: Properties in CPO routes face uncertainty; title insurance exclusion common

Section C: Surety (SB) Underwriting

Surety is fundamentally a credit analysis product, not a risk-rated insurance product. Surety underwriters assess contractor/principal financial strength first; pricing follows from credit tier.

Bond Premium Formula
Bond Premium = Contract Value × Rate × Tenor Factor

Premium Components

Performance bond rate: 0.5–3.0% of contract value VER
Payment bond (typically alongside): additional 0.2–0.75% VER
Tenor factor: 1.0 (1 year), 1.4–1.6 (2 years), 1.8–2.2 (3+ years) EST

1. Financial Strength

Net worth, working capital, backlog-to-equity ratio, current ratio. Key ratio: backlog/equity < 10:1 EST

2. Work Type

Building: Low risk 0.5–0.8%. Civil/Infrastructure: Medium 0.8–1.5%. Process/Industrial: Higher 1.2–2.0%. Specialist/International: Highest 1.5–3.0% EST

3. Completion Record

% projects completed on time, prior bond losses, references from principals. Strong track record reduces rate.

4. Management Quality

Experience, succession planning, key-person dependency, subcontractor management. Weak management triggers rate loading or declination.

5. Project-Specific Risks

Size vs annual turnover (< 25% safer), fixed-price vs cost-plus, design responsibility, supply chain dependencies EST

6. Co-Surety/Reinsurance

Large bonds often co-sured; lead surety typically 50–70% with one or two co-sureties. Reinsurance obligatory above single risk retention limits EST

4-Class Risk Matrix (Contractor Quality vs Project Complexity)

Contractor / Project Complexity Low Complexity High Complexity
Low Quality (higher risk contractor) 1.0–1.5% 2.0–3.0%+
High Quality (strong contractor) 0.5–0.7% 0.8–1.2%

8-Step Surety Underwriting Process

  1. Financial analysis: 3 years audited accounts, management accounts, tax returns. Ratio analysis and trend.
  2. Work program review: Current backlog, pipeline contracts, projected cash flows, project-to-turnover concentration.
  3. Reference checks: Prior bond experience with other sureties, principal feedback on contractor performance.
  4. Contract review: Fixed-price risk, design liability, liquidated damages caps, subcontracting limits.
  5. Site visit: Conducted for major bonds (> £5m). Assess site management, safety culture, resource allocation.
  6. Principal indemnity agreement: Personal indemnity from directors and shareholders. Cross-guarantees within group.
  7. Rating & pricing: Credit tier assigned; rate set per tier and project factors. Co-surety placement if required.
  8. Co-surety/reinsurance placement: For bonds exceeding lead surety single risk limit. Negotiate lead/co-surety split, lines, and commissions.

Section D: Financial Guarantee (FG) Underwriting

Financial Guarantee is structurally different from traditional insurance: it wraps a specific debt obligation. Underwriting focuses on credit analysis of the underlying issuer and obligation structure.

Financial Guarantee Annual Premium Formula
FG Annual Premium = Insured Par × (Credit Spread − AAA Reference Rate) × Coverage Fraction

Pricing Guidance

Infrastructure FG: Typically 20–60 bps per annum of insured par EST
Tenor: 10–30 years common for infrastructure EST
Payment structure: Up-front or annual premium depending on contract structure VER

Key Underwriting Criteria

  • Issuer credit quality: Investment-grade minimum (BBB− floor); sovereign or quasi-sovereign support preferred.
  • Security structure: Revenue pledge, reserve fund requirements, debt service coverage ratio covenants (DSCR ≥ 1.2×).
  • Legal opinion: Validity and enforceability of underlying obligation; strength of security package.
  • Monitoring rights: Ongoing covenant compliance reporting; trigger events for enhanced monitoring or cure rights.
  • Portfolio concentration limits: Geographic concentration (no single country > 20% of insured par); sector concentration (no single sector > 25%).

Section F: Worked Examples

Example 1: Performance Bond (Surety) ILLUS

Contract: £8m hospital wing construction, 18-month completion
Contractor: Mid-tier regional contractor, net worth £2.5m, 8-year trading history, no prior bond claims
Rating analysis:
  • Work type: Building (0.8% base rate)
  • Complexity factor: Medium (×1.1)
  • Track record discount: Clean history (×0.9)
Bond sizing: Performance bond = 10% of contract = £800,000
Performance bond premium: £800,000 × 0.8% × 1.1 × 0.9 = ~£6,336 (approx 0.08% of contract value)
Payment bond: £800,000 × 0.3% = £2,400
Total bonding cost: ~£8,736 for full performance + payment package

Example 2: Title Insurance (Title) ILLUS

Property: £650,000 residential property in London, leasehold flat, recent change in ground rent structure, no EWS1 for external wall system
Owner's policy: £650,000 / 1,000 × £4.50 = £2,925
Lender's policy (simultaneous issue): £520,000 loan / 1,000 × £2.50 × 0.75 (discount) = £975
Leasehold/cladding risk loading: +30% on combined = +£1,170
Total: ~£5,070 for combined owner's + lender's cover package with risk loading
