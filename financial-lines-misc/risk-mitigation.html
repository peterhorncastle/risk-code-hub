Section A: MIG/FM Risk Controls

1. LTV Limits & Stress Testing

Maximum LTV thresholds by product type and lender tier. Stress test all portfolios to 20–30% property value decline and concurrent 3% rate rise. Regional portfolio concentration limits (e.g. max 30% in single region). New-build LTV discounts to reflect post-completion value stabilisation risk.

2. Affordability Assessment

Income multiples (e.g. max 4.5× gross income). Stress-rate affordability test (SVR + 3%). Employment type analysis (PAYE vs self-employed; self-employed typically require 2 years audited accounts). Credit score minimum thresholds (typically 620–680 range). Debt-to-income ratio caps (e.g. max 45%).

3. Arrears Monitoring & Early Intervention

Flag accounts at first missed payment. Forbearance protocol (payment holidays, term extension, temporary rate reduction). SPOC (Single Point of Contact) for borrowers in difficulty VER — FCA requirement. Regular portfolio arrears reporting and trend analysis.

4. Property Valuation Controls

Independent RICS-qualified valuation at origination. Automated valuation model (AVM) cross-check for standardisation. New-build premium caps (e.g. max 10% above open market value). Regular portfolio re-valuation (e.g. annually). Fraud flag triggers: below-market price, quick resale patterns, non-standard mortgages.

Section B: Title Insurance Risk Controls

1. Title Search & Examination

Comprehensive title plant search (USA) or Land Registry OS1 official search (UK). Verify registered owner via Land Registry and Companies House. Check for pending applications, cautions, restrictions. Review historical title for defects and prior claims against property.

2. Identity Verification

Government-issued ID + proof of address. For high-value transactions: in-person verification or video-link notarised. HMLR Practice Guide 67 compliance VER. Warning signs: remote sale only, urgent timeline, price below market, power of attorney being used, foreign beneficial owner.

3. Conveyancing Fraud Prevention

Verify bank account details independently via confirmed firm letterhead (never via email alone). Use CHAPS/Faster Payments only to previously verified accounts. Call client on known number to confirm payment details before transferring funds. SRA guidance on wire fraud VER. Monitor for deepfake video/audio identity spoofing.

4. Post-Completion Monitoring

Register promptly at Land Registry. Monitor for fraudulent registered dispositions using Land Registry Property Alert service VER. Title insurer underwriting exclusions review for known defects already disclosed at inception. Update exclusions after major policy changes (e.g. BSA 2022 cladding, leasehold reform 2024).

Section C: Surety Risk Controls

1. Financial Analysis Rigour

3 years audited financials plus current management accounts (within 3 months of underwriting). Key ratios: current ratio > 1.5, backlog/equity < 10:1, working capital > 5% of annual revenue. Monitor quarterly. Trigger covenant: ratio breach = automatic bond review and potential rate increase or cancellation.

2. Principal Indemnity Agreements (PIAs)

Personal indemnity from directors and shareholders VER. Cross-guarantees within group if contractor is subsidiary. Collateral security (bank guarantee, charge over assets) for marginal credits. Indemnity from parents/guarantors where contractor is subsidiary. Legal opinion on enforceability in relevant jurisdiction.

3. Contract-Specific Review

Review contract terms: fixed-price vs cost-plus (fixed-price higher risk), design liability, liquidated damages caps. Flag high-risk contracts: contracts > 25% of annual turnover, overseas contracts, first-entry-to-market types, back-to-back subcontracting with tight margins, novel technologies or supply chain dependencies.

4. Claims Management & Completing Bonds

On contractor default: investigate within 30 days. Three completion options: finance contractor to complete (preferred if < 50% complete), tender for replacement contractor, pay bond penalty. Completion bond is preferred where project is > 50% complete. Aggressive subrogation against defaulted principal and personal indemnitors.

Section D: Financial Guarantee Risk Controls

1. Credit Analysis & Minimum Standards

Investment-grade underlying credit (BBB− minimum floor). Sovereign or quasi-sovereign support for infrastructure preferred. Legal opinion on validity and enforceability of obligation. Structural protections: debt service reserve fund ≥ 6 months coverage, rate covenant ≥ 1.2× DSCR EST. Stress scenarios on project cash flows.

2. Portfolio Concentration Limits

Geographic concentration: no single country > 20% of insured par. Sector concentration: no single sector > 25% (e.g. utilities, toll roads, water). Single obligor limit (typically < 5% of par). Stressed scenario modelling across portfolio under economic downturn and credit event scenarios.

3. Monitoring & Covenant Surveillance

Quarterly financial covenants reported by issuer. Trigger events activate enhanced monitoring or accelerated cure rights. Rating agency surveillance alignment (use same agencies as capital market investors). Annual legal review of security package. Monitor for refinancing risk and changes in obligor credit quality.

4. The Lessons of 2008

Correlation risk: the 2008 crisis proved AAA-rated structures can be highly correlated (all RMBS in same housing cycle). Model risk: Gaussian copula underestimated tail risk. Concentration: exposure to single asset class (US RMBS) proved fatal. Key post-2008 discipline: no structured credit wraps, concentration limits, conservative stress scenarios. Avoid monoline consolidation to single obligor.

Section E: Top 10 Risk Actions for Underwriters

  1. Surety: never issue a bond without full financial analysis — pricing alone does not compensate for insolvency risk.
  2. Title: treat any remote/urgent/below-market transaction as higher fraud risk — follow standard ID verification regardless of time pressure.
  3. MIG: stress test all portfolios to 20–30% HPI fall and 3% rate rise simultaneously before capacity allocation.
  4. Surety: require personal indemnity as standard — subrogation without personal indemnity is often worthless against insolvent contractor.
  5. Title: use Land Registry Property Alert for all insured properties to detect post-completion fraud.
  6. MIG: monitor new-build LTVs carefully — new-build premium inflates LTV; post-completion value drops are common (5–15% decline typical).
  7. FG: never wrap structured credit — the 2008 lesson is definitive; maintain investment-grade single-name focus only.
  8. Surety: flag contracts where size exceeds 25% of contractor annual turnover — outsized project risk triggers senior review.
  9. Title: update exclusion schedules after every policy change in relevant area (BSA 2022 cladding, leasehold reform 2024).
  10. All lines: review reinsurance panel credit quality — FG/surety losses can be severe; reinsurer insolvency in stress scenario is an amplifier.
