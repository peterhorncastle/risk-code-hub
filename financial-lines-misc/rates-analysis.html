Section C: Market Cycle Narrative

1. 1999–2003: US Refi Boom

Low interest rates drove unprecedented refinance volume. Title premium volume nearly doubled (index 100 → 225). Surety remained stable during housing construction boom. Title insurers expanded rapidly; premium competition intensified.

2. 2003–2007: Housing Bubble

Title market stabilised at elevated levels (index 220–232). Surety highly profitable (CR 65–72%) from construction demand. MIG/PMI grew rapidly as lenders pushed higher LTVs and stated-income lending. Underwriting standards deteriorated across all lines.

3. 2007–2010: Financial Crisis

FG monolines (AMBAC, MBIA) collapsed on RMBS exposure. PMI/MIG suffered catastrophic losses on housing downturn. Title declined sharply with transaction volume collapse (index 160 → 120). Surety CR spiked (2009: 102) as contractor defaults surged.

4. 2010–2015: Slow Recovery

Title recovered with housing market stabilisation (index 120 → 168). Surety CR returned to 65–70% range (very profitable). FG market remained dormant; only Assured Guaranty survived as active municipal monoline. MIG cautious recovery; lender LTV discipline tightened.

5. 2016–2021: Bull Market Peak

Surety benefited from construction boom and infrastructure investment. Title reached 2021 peak (index 390, ~$26bn market) from unprecedented refi volume. MIG stabilised at modest LTV thresholds; arrears rates remained low. FG limited to municipal guarantee business.

6. 2022–Present: Normalisation

Title contracted sharply on rate rises and mortgage volume collapse (index 390 → 170). Surety: infrastructure boom vs contractor insolvency risk tension. CR pressured by 2023–24 contractor failures but not yet at crisis levels. MIG UK cautious due to rising arrears; FG minimal new issuance.

Section E: ROE Analysis

Three scenarios on a combined $500m hypothetical book (mix of title, surety, MIG, FG). All figures illustrative ILLUS.

Scenario Note Title low-claims year, surety profitable Base cycle mid-market Contractor default wave + title fraud spike

Bull scenario (CR 77%): Achieves when surety backlog strong, title refi volume recovers, and MIG arrears stay benign. Requires market tailwind on rates and housing sentiment. Pre-tax ROE of 24% is near peak-cycle return.

Base scenario (CR 94%): Typical mid-cycle outcome. Title and surety each profitable; MIG stable. Investment income offsets modest underwriting losses. 11% pre-tax ROE is acceptable but below cost of capital for most institutions.

Bear scenario (CR 119%): Triggers during contractor insolvency wave + conveyancing fraud spike. FG exposure to credit events also assumes losses. Negative ROE implies capital erosion; business model stress-tested to breaking point.
