Section B: Lloyd's Market Role — Firm/Segment Cards

1. UK Residential Title

Lloyd's syndicates (Hiscox, CNA) provide capacity for specialist UK residential title risks (leasehold, BSA cladding, historic defects) not covered by standard Land Registry indemnity. Underwriting focuses on defect-specific risk assessment. ~£50–100m Lloyd's title GWP estimated. GAP

2. Construction Surety Co-Surety

Lloyd's participates as co-surety on large UK/European infrastructure bonds (HS2, Hinkley Point, Network Rail). Lead typically Zurich, Liberty, Aviva; Lloyd's joins as following market (10–20% lines). Specialist expertise in complex construction risks and novel contract forms. EST

3. International Surety

Lloyd's active in markets where local capacity is thin: Middle East, Latin America, Africa. Cross-border infrastructure with political risk overlay. Provides currency and sovereign risk expertise. Often co-insures with local captives to manage political exposure. EST

4. Specialist MIG/PMI Reinsurance

Lloyd's reinsurers provide excess-of-loss capacity above retained MIG layers for lender captives. Especially relevant for challenger banks and building societies without A-rated fronting insurer. Provides portfolio capacity for lenders with narrow capital bases. EST

Section E: Case Study — Large UK Infrastructure Surety Programme

Scenario ILLUS

£200m rail upgrade contract (NEC4 ECC), 3-year completion, public sector client (Network Rail), mid-tier UK contractor (annual turnover £180m, net worth £12m).

Contract Size Assessment: £200m contract is 111% of annual turnover — significant over-exposure flag. Requires senior management meeting, 3-year projected cash flows, detailed project plan review.
Bond Structure: Performance bond amount = 10% of contract value = £20m. Payment bond also £20m (standard).
Rating Factors:
  • Work type: Railway infrastructure (1.4% base — elevated risk vs standard building)
  • Size/turnover loading: 1.2× (contract exceeds 25% of turnover threshold)
  • First-time with principal: 1.1× (new client relationship, no track record)
Premium Calculation: Performance bond premium = £20m × 1.4% × 1.2 × 1.1 = £369,600
Payment Bond: £20m × 0.5% × 1.2 = £120,000
Additional Requirements:
  • Personal indemnity: Directors and parent company (cross-guarantee)
  • Quarterly financial reporting covenant
  • Project bank account with restricted draw-down
  • Co-surety arranged for performance bond (contract exceeds single surety limit)
Total Annual Bonding Cost: ~£490,000 (approximately 0.24% of contract value) ILLUS
Lloyd's Participation: Second co-surety line of ~£5m (following market behind lead and first co-surety).
Placement Summary: Lead surety retains £12m, first co-surety (AXA XL) £5m, Lloyd's syndicates £3m, facultative reinsurance £5m. Tight placement reflecting contractor's limited net worth relative to contract size and infrastructure sector risk.
