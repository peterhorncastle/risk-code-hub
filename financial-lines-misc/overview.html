Coverage Types

🏠 Mortgage Indemnity Guarantee (FM)

Protects mortgage lenders when a borrower defaults and the property sale proceeds fall short of the outstanding loan. Typically triggered on LTV > 75–80%. Once paid by insurer, subrogation rights against borrower.

📋 Title Insurance — Owner's Policy (TT)

Protects property buyers against title defects existing at the date of policy: unknown liens, encumbrances, boundary disputes, deed forgery, missing heir claims. One-time premium; coverage for as long as owner holds interest.

🏦 Title Insurance — Lender's Policy (TT)

Required by most mortgage lenders. Covers the lender's interest up to the loan amount. Typically purchased alongside owner's policy at discounted simultaneous issue rate. Coverage reduces as loan is paid down.

🔨 Performance & Payment Bonds (SB)

Guarantees contract completion and payment to subcontractors/suppliers. Performance bond: principal obligated to complete or pay damages. Payment bond: protects sub-tier against non-payment. Core to public procurement (Miller Act US, OJEU EU).

📄 Licence, Court & Misc Surety (SB)

Wide range including: licence bonds (professional/trade), court bonds (appeals, guardianship), customs bonds, environmental closure bonds, oil & gas plugging bonds, tax bonds. Typically shorter-term, lower premium intensity.

🏛️ Financial Guarantee / Bond Insurance (FG)

Unconditional guarantee of timely principal and interest payments on debt instruments. Used historically on municipal bonds (AAA-wrap), ABS, CDOs. Near-extinct for structured finance post-2008; surviving on infrastructure and public finance.

Product Differentiation

Key Distinctions Within This Hub

MIG (FM) is credit insurance for lenders on residential mortgages; charged upfront or built into mortgage rate.

Title (TT) is a legal indemnity product covering pre-existing defects rather than future risks; unique in that it covers known threats (fraud, forged deeds) and unknown threats (missing heirs, undiscovered liens).

Surety (SB) is a three-party obligation (principal–obligee–surety), not traditional insurance; the surety can pursue the principal for recovery after paying a claim.

Financial Guarantee (FG) is an unconditional wrap of scheduled debt payments; historically used to upgrade credit ratings, now mostly confined to public finance and infrastructure.

Each has distinct regulatory, reserving and capital treatment under IFRS, IASB, and local regulators.
