D&O Rates & ROE Analysis

D&O rates for US public companies tripled between 2019 and 2021 — the most severe hardening in the class's history — driven by the SPAC explosion, COVID-related claims and ESG litigation. The 2022–2024 market has softened materially, particularly on non-US and private company risks, while US public company rates remain elevated by historical standards.

The Long Tail Problem: Why D&O Reserving Is So Difficult

D&O claims are notoriously slow to develop. A securities class action filed in 2024 may not settle until 2028–2030. Regulatory investigations filed in 2022 may not produce final orders until 2026. The average time from filing to settlement for US securities class actions is approximately 3–4 years; for DOJ/SEC enforcement actions it can be 5–8 years. This means that a D&O underwriter's 2020 portfolio is still developing in 2025.

The long tail creates two structural problems. First, reserving: initial IBNR reserves set at policy expiry may be significantly understated — a phenomenon known as "reserve development." Lloyd's syndicates that wrote D1 business in 2019–2020 were still taking adverse development on those years in 2023–2024 as SPAC-related claims settled. Second, investment income: the long claims tail means premium funds are invested for longer, providing a timing benefit that partially offsets the reserving uncertainty.

The most sophisticated D&O underwriters maintain a "claims development triangle" showing settlement patterns by year of account, helping calibrate IBNR reserves against actual historical development. This is standard actuarial practice for long-tail casualty lines but requires years of data accumulation to be reliable — making it particularly difficult for new D&O entrants to price accurately.
