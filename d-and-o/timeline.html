D&O Major Loss Events

Twenty years of securities class actions, corporate fraud, financial crisis, SPAC litigation and ESG claims — the events that shaped D&O pricing, policy form development and underwriter appetite from 2001 to 2024.

2001 D&O Settlement ~$300m+
Enron Corporation Collapse
Energy trading giant collapsed in December 2001 after systematic accounting fraud involving off-balance-sheet SPVs. CEO Jeffrey Skilling and CFO Andrew Fastow convicted. Largest US bankruptcy at the time ($63bn assets). D&O insurers paid out over $300m; Chubb and Zurich among largest payers. Triggered Sarbanes-Oxley legislation within months.
2002 D&O Settlement ~$50m+
WorldCom / MCI Accounting Fraud
WorldCom CEO Bernie Ebbers and CFO Scott Sullivan orchestrated $11bn in accounting fraud — the largest at the time. Bankruptcy in July 2002 ($107bn assets) surpassed Enron. Ebbers sentenced to 25 years. D&O settlements reached approximately $50m from insurance; State Street Bank separately settled for $9m. SEC obtained $2.25bn in disgorgement from WorldCom.
2002–2003 D&O ~$50m (Tyco)
Tyco International / HealthSouth / Adelphia
Multiple simultaneous corporate fraud scandals: Tyco CEO Dennis Kozlowski looted $600m; HealthSouth CEO Richard Scrushy orchestrated $2.7bn accounting fraud; Adelphia CEO John Rigas looted $2.3bn. Combined D&O industry losses from the 2001–2003 fraud wave exceeded $1bn market-wide and drove the first major 21st-century D&O hardening cycle.
2004 AIG Fine $1.64bn
NY AG Spitzer — Insurance Industry Investigations
New York Attorney General Eliot Spitzer launched investigations into bid-rigging and contingent commission practices across the insurance industry. Marsh & McLennan paid $850m; AIG under Hank Greenberg faced accounting fraud allegations and paid $1.64bn settlement. Greenberg resigned. Accelerated D&O scrutiny of financial institution governance and insurance market conflicts of interest.
2006–2007 Multiple settlements $25m–$250m
Stock Options Backdating Scandal
Over 200 companies were investigated for backdating stock options to more favourable prices — effectively undisclosed compensation to executives. Apple (Steve Jobs), Broadcom, Brocade, Comverse Technology and UnitedHealth Group were among the most prominent. CEO Gregory Reyes of Brocade was convicted. The wave generated extensive D&O derivative litigation; UnitedHealth's CEO William McGuire forfeited $600m in compensation.
2007–2008 D&O ~$450m (AIG alone)
Global Financial Crisis — Bear Stearns, Lehman, AIG, Washington Mutual
The GFC produced the most concentrated D&O loss event in history. Bear Stearns hedge fund failure (June 2007) and Bear's collapse (March 2008); Lehman Brothers bankruptcy ($639bn assets, September 2008); AIG bailout ($182bn); Washington Mutual seizure (September 2008, $307bn assets). D&O insurance paid approximately $450m on AIG alone; total financial sector D&O losses exceeded $2bn. FDIC pursued directors of WaMu and IndyMac aggressively.
2008 Restitution $17bn (Madoff)
Bernie Madoff Ponzi Scheme Collapse
$65bn Ponzi scheme — the largest in US history — collapsed after Madoff turned himself in. Primarily a fiduciary liability (DD) event: feeder fund directors and fund-of-fund managers who failed to conduct due diligence faced claims from investors. HSBC, JP Morgan and various feeder fund directors paid hundreds of millions in settlements. SEC enforcement failure also attracted regulatory investigation of oversight shortcomings.
2010 Securities settlements ~$200m+
BP Deepwater Horizon — Securities Fraud Claims
The April 2010 Macondo blowout triggered not only $65bn+ in cleanup and settlement costs, but securities class actions alleging BP's executives made materially false statements about the company's safety record and blowout preventer reliability before the disaster. BP's D&O/securities settlement with US institutional investors reached approximately $175m. Case established the template for "safety disclosure" securities claims in highly regulated industries.
2015 VW settlements $30bn+
Volkswagen Dieselgate — Governance and Securities Claims
Revelations that Volkswagen installed "defeat devices" to cheat US emissions tests wiped 30% off VW's share price in two days. German and US shareholder claims followed. While German court procedures differ from US class actions, VW ultimately agreed to a €9bn shareholder settlement in Germany (2021). Key D&O lesson: emissions, ESG and regulatory concealment are securities disclosure issues — the same day the cover-up is revealed becomes the trigger date for investor loss calculations.
2018–2019 D&O ~$100m+
Theranos / Elizabeth Holmes Securities Fraud
Elizabeth Holmes and Theranos President Ramesh "Sunny" Balwani convicted of defrauding investors of $945m by misrepresenting blood-testing technology capabilities. Board members including George Shultz (former US Secretary of State) avoided personal liability through the Business Judgment Rule but faced significant reputational scrutiny. Holmes sentenced to 11 years. Total investor/patient settlement exposure exceeded $926m. Emblematic of Silicon Valley "fake it till you make it" governance risk.
2019 Reputational / IPO collapse
WeWork IPO Collapse — CEO Governance Failure
WeWork's August 2019 IPO prospectus revealed extraordinary governance failings: CEO Adam Neumann had sold $700m of shares, leased properties to WeWork personally, trademarked the word "We" and sold it back to the company for $5.9m, and given himself supervoting shares. The IPO was withdrawn; SoftBank bailed out the company. Neumann ultimately received $1.7bn in a contested settlement. The case became the textbook example for governance disclosures in prospectuses.
2019–2020 Boeing settlements $2.5bn+
Boeing 737 MAX — Securities and Derivative Claims
Two fatal 737 MAX crashes (October 2018, March 2019, 346 deaths) triggered securities class actions alleging Boeing executives concealed MCAS safety system flaws from investors. Boeing agreed to a $237m D&O derivative settlement in 2021 (Delaware) and a separate $2.5bn DOJ settlement. The case clarified that board-level safety oversight failures constitute securities disclosure violations — enormously important for industrial and aviation D&O underwriting.
2020–2022 100+ class actions filed
SPAC Wave — Blank-Cheque Company Litigation Explosion
600+ SPACs launched in 2021 alone, many merging with early-stage companies that provided wildly optimistic revenue projections in investor presentations (not subject to the normal IPO due diligence standard). Post-merger stock collapses triggered a wave of securities class actions. High-profile SPAC D&O losses: Lordstown Motors (EV trucks), Clover Health, MultiPlan, Lottery.com, Atlis Motor Vehicles. D&O market losses from SPACs were concentrated in 2021–2023; some syndicates exited SPAC business entirely.
2022 $8bn+ investor losses
FTX Collapse — Crypto Governance Catastrophe
FTX cryptocurrency exchange collapsed in November 2022 after revelations that approximately $8bn of customer funds had been transferred to affiliated trading firm Alameda Research. CEO Sam Bankman-Fried convicted on 7 counts of wire fraud, securities fraud and conspiracy — sentenced to 25 years. FTX directors and investors (including Sequoia, SoftBank, Ontario Teachers' Pension Plan) faced claims. D&O tail coverage from FTX's sparse pre-collapse insurance programme was rapidly exhausted. Largest crypto governance failure to date.
2023 FDIC D&O claims ongoing
Silicon Valley Bank Collapse — Interest Rate Risk & Insider Trading
SVB Financial collapsed in March 2023 — the second-largest US bank failure ($209bn assets). SVB's CEO Gregory Becker sold $3.6m of shares two weeks before the collapse; CFO also sold shares. FDIC and class action plaintiffs alleged SVB's board failed to manage interest rate risk in its bond portfolio while disclosing "well-capitalised" status. D5 code event. Signature Bank and First Republic Bank failures the same month generated further FI D&O exposure.
2023–2024 Emerging claims
AI Governance Claims — Emerging Frontier
Early AI-related D&O claims emerged against technology companies alleged to have overstated AI capability in investor communications. Cases included claims against companies stating AI tools were "live" when still in development, and board disclosure failures regarding AI regulatory risk. The SEC's enhanced focus on AI disclosure accuracy and the EU AI Act's governance requirements signal that AI-related D&O claims will become a sustained claims category through the late 2020s. First filed cases still in early stages as of 2024.
