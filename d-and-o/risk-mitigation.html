Module 06 — Risk Mitigation

D&O Risk Mitigation & Corporate Governance

The best D&O mitigation is strong corporate governance — independent boards, transparent disclosure, quality audit and proactive regulatory engagement. For underwriters, governance quality is both a direct rating factor and an indicator of the broader cultural environment in which claims arise.

Corporate Governance Best Practices

Board Independence

Majority of board members should be independent non-executive directors (INEDs). Audit, compensation and nominating committees should be 100% independent. Separate CEO and Chairman roles strongly preferred. Lead independent director required where roles are combined.

D&O Rate Credit: 5–15%

Board Diversity & Expertise

Sector-relevant expertise (industry, finance, technology, legal) among non-executive directors. Gender and ethnic diversity correlates with better oversight outcomes and reduces ESG securities litigation risk. Board skills matrix disclosed in annual report.

D&O Rate Credit: 5–10%

Audit Committee Quality

All-independent audit committee; at least one member with financial expertise (CFO/CPA background). Quarterly meetings with external auditor in camera (without management). SOX 404 material weakness remediation tracked by committee. Big 4 auditor preferred.

D&O Rate Credit: 5–10%

Compliance & Ethics Programme

Formal code of conduct with annual certification. Whistleblower hotline with board-level reporting. Anti-corruption (FCPA/UK Bribery Act) programme. Regular compliance training for executives. Demonstrated consequence management for violations.

D&O Rate Credit: 5–10%

Accurate ESG Disclosure

ESG commitments proportionate to verifiable performance. Climate disclosures (TCFD/SEC climate rules) reconciled to actual emissions data. ESG KPIs tied to executive compensation only if measurable. Avoid commitments that create securities fraud exposure if unmet.

Litigation Risk Reduction

Director Education & D&O Awareness

Annual board training on directors' duties, securities disclosure obligations and personal liability. D&O policy annual review with coverage counsel. Directors should understand: their policy's retro date, run-off obligations, disclosure requirements and conduct exclusions.

Risk Awareness

Insider Trading Controls

Formal insider trading policy with pre-clearance requirements. Blackout periods around earnings releases and material events. 10b5-1 trading plans for executive equity sales must comply with post-2022 SEC rules (cooling-off periods, single-plan limitations). Share sales near disclosures are the most visible red flag in securities litigation.

Litigation Prevention

Cyber Governance (post-SEC 2023)

Board-level cybersecurity oversight responsibility formally assigned (audit committee or dedicated cyber committee). CISO reporting line to board, not just CIO. Annual board cyber education. Material incident disclosure process established pre-incident (4-day SEC clock).

D&O Rate Credit: 3–8%

Securities Disclosure Risk Reduction

The majority of D1 and D3 securities class actions are triggered by a share price drop following a corrective disclosure — information that reveals a prior statement was inaccurate or misleading. The plaintiff's theory is that the prior statement was a fraud and the company is liable for the investor's losses. The best mitigation is scrupulous disclosure accuracy.

EPL Risk Mitigation (DA / DB)

Employment practices claims are largely preventable through proactive HR governance. Post-#MeToo, underwriters specifically ask about harassment reporting mechanisms, training programmes and documented investigation processes. EEOC charge rates, settlement history and formal diversity reporting are all assessed.

⚠️ Fiduciary Liability (DD): ERISA Excessive Fee Claims

Since the Supreme Court's 2015 Tibble v. Edison decision, university and corporate 401(k)/403(b) plan sponsors face a continuing duty to monitor and replace imprudent investments. Plaintiff firms targeting plan sponsors typically allege: (i) the plan offered actively managed mutual funds when comparable index funds were available at a fraction of the cost; and (ii) the plan's recordkeeping fees were excessive relative to benchmarks.

Best practices for DD mitigation: conduct annual investment fee benchmarking and document the process; replace funds that persistently underperform net of fees; issue competitive RFPs for recordkeeping services every 3–5 years; document all Investment Committee decisions in formal minutes; ensure the Investment Committee includes at least one member with investment expertise. Companies with documented, rigorous Investment Committee governance pay materially less for DD insurance and have consistently better litigation outcomes when claims are filed.

The "Storm Warnings" Doctrine — When to Disclose Early

One of the most counterintuitive aspects of D&O risk management is that earlier, more complete disclosure of problems — even before they fully crystallise — typically reduces D&O liability compared to delayed disclosure. The "storm warnings" doctrine in US securities law holds that once a company has internal awareness of a developing problem, continuing to make positive statements creates a duty to disclose the contrary information.

Silicon Valley Bank's management sold shares and described the bank as "well-capitalised" in February–March 2023, weeks before disclosure of the capital raise that triggered the run. The FDIC's D&O claims centre on exactly this sequence. By contrast, companies that disclose problems proactively — issuing "cautionary statements" before investors buy in reliance on overly positive guidance — typically avoid the largest securities fraud claims, even if the stock falls sharply on the disclosure.

The practical governance implication: when internal signals are negative, legal counsel and the disclosure committee should be convened immediately to assess disclosure obligations. The cost of a precautionary disclosure (temporary share price pressure) is almost always lower than the cost of a securities class action filed after a delayed disclosure.
