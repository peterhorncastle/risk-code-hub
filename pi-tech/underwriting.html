Claims-Made Basis & Coverage Trigger

Technology E&O is universally written on a claims-made basis. Coverage applies when a claim is first made against the insured during the policy period, regardless of when the negligent act or omission occurred. Full prior acts coverage is standard for established firms.

Key trigger: The claimant must suffer a quantifiable ECONOMIC LOSS as a direct result of the insured's failure to perform professional technology services to the required standard. Bodily injury and property damage are excluded (covered by General Liability). Pure financial loss from software errors, project failures and service outages is the core insuring agreement.

F4 Technology E&O Rating Formula

Tech E&O Premium = Annual Revenue × Base Rate × Service Type Factor × Client Concentration Factor × Contract Liability Factor

Base rate range: 0.3–2.5% of annual technology services revenue (varies widely by risk profile)

Rating Factors

1. Service Type

  • Custom software development (1.3–1.8×)
  • System integration (1.2–1.6×)
  • SaaS platform (1.0–1.5×)
  • IT consulting (0.8–1.2×)
  • Managed services/MSP (1.2–1.7×)
  • Data/analytics services (1.0–1.4×)
  • IT staffing/body shop (0.5–0.8×)

Reason: Custom code and integration have highest failure probability; staffing has low PI exposure as client manages output.

2. Client Sector

  • Critical infrastructure (utilities, healthcare, aviation, defence): 1.5–2.5×
  • Financial services (banking, trading): 1.4–2.0×
  • General enterprise (manufacturing, retail): 1.0–1.3×
  • Small/medium business (SMB): 0.8–1.1×
  • Government/public sector: 1.1–1.6×

Reason: System failure consequences proportional to client sector criticality.

3. Largest Single Client Revenue Concentration

  • Top client <10% of revenue: 0.9×
  • 10–25%: 1.0×
  • 25–40%: 1.2×
  • 40–60%: 1.4×
  • >60%: 1.6–2.0× or possible declination

Reason: Single-client concentration means one large PI claim could be catastrophic relative to premium base.

4. Contract Liability Cap

  • Capped at 100%+ of contract value: 0.9×
  • Capped at 50–100%: 1.0×
  • Capped at 12 months' fees: 1.15×
  • Capped at 6 months' fees: 1.25×
  • Uncapped: 1.4–1.8×

Reason: Contract caps significantly limit PI exposure; uncapped contracts create catastrophic scenario potential.

5. Development Methodology & Testing

  • Agile with CI/CD, automated testing, security scanning: 0.9×
  • Waterfall with UAT, staged rollout: 1.0×
  • No documented methodology, minimal testing: 1.3–1.5×
  • Deploying to financial/safety-critical without test environment: 1.5–2.0×

Reason: Testing discipline is primary risk reduction tool — CrowdStrike event exemplifies consequences of deploying without adequate content validation testing.

6. Claims History

  • Clean 5+ years: 0.85×
  • 1 minor claim (<$100k): 1.05×
  • 1 significant claim ($100k–$1m): 1.2–1.4×
  • Multiple claims or pattern: 1.5–2.0×
  • Adverse trend (frequency): potential declination

F5 Telecom PI Rating Considerations

Telecom PI carries unique exposures requiring separate underwriting consideration. Network availability, billing system accuracy, and emergency services routing represent distinct liability vectors.

Telecom Risk Categories

Service F4 or F5 Base Rate (% revenue) Key Exposure
Mobile network operator F5 0.4–0.8% Network outage, 999/911 failure, billing errors
Fixed-line/broadband ISP F5 0.3–0.6% Service level breach, business interruption
MVNO/virtual operator F5 0.5–1.0% Upstream network dependency, billing platform
Managed WAN/SD-WAN F4/F5 0.6–1.2% Enterprise network failure, SLA breach
Telecom billing software F4 0.8–1.5% Mass billing error (systemic aggregate)
Emergency services network F5 Negotiated — highest 999/112/911 access failure

Emergency services uplift: Any telecom provider with a role in emergency call routing faces 2–4× loading on relevant revenue. A single 999 failure resulting in death creates catastrophic liability potential.

Contract Liability Analysis

Limitation of liability (LoL) clauses are the single most important underwriting factor in tech E&O. Most technology contracts include LoL caps — typically 12 months' fees or 100% of contract value. These create a ceiling on indemnifiable loss. However: CrowdStrike-type mass-casualty events, government contracts (often with no LoL or unlimited caps), and consumer-facing products without B2B contracts may face uncapped exposure.

Contract Type Typical LoL Underwriting Implication
Standard B2B software contract 12 months' fees or £/$100k Moderate — cap limits max PI exposure
Government/public sector contract Often 100–200% contract value or uncapped High — can be very large absolute amounts
Critical national infrastructure Unlimited or capped very high Very high — potential for catastrophic loss
Consumer SaaS (Terms of Service) Often no liability beyond subscription refund Low for direct consumer claims; class action risk higher
Open source software No liability (licences disclaim) Low direct; indirect if incorporated into commercial product
Algo/financial trading software Unlimited or very high caps Extremely high — Knight Capital scenario

Premium: £8m × 0.9% × 1.1 × 1.0 × 1.0 × 0.9 × 0.88 = ~£62,200

Limit: £5m each and every claim; £10m aggregate

Example 2: IT Systems Integrator (F4) — Financial Services Sector ILLUS

Profile: £25m annual revenue; core business = systems integration for UK banks and investment firms; largest client = 35% of revenue (major retail bank); contracts — 50% have uncapped LoL (negotiated out); experienced underwriting team; 1 claim settled at £220k in year 3

Rating Calculation (Underwriter Negotiated):

Base rate:
1.5% (integration, financial services)
Service type (integration):
×1.4
Client sector (financial services):
×1.6
Concentration (35%):
×1.2
Contract LoL (50% uncapped):
×1.4
Development methodology:
×1.0
Claims (1 significant):
×1.25

Full rate would be too high — underwriter negotiates: higher retention (£500k), requirement to cap liability in new contracts, risk management undertaking

Negotiated Premium: £25m × 1.5% × 1.4 × 1.6 × 1.2 × 1.4 × 1.0 × 1.25 = ~£1,411,200 — AFTER risk management discount and £500k SIR: ~£620,000

This illustrates why financial services integration is the most expensive tech E&O sub-class
