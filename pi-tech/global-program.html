Tech E&O Programme Tower

A typical technology professional indemnity programme is structured in layers from top (catastrophe reinsurance) to bottom (insured's deductible). Each layer has distinct placement logic, market, and pricing relative to the primary rate.

Layer 1: Catastrophe XL / Top Excess
$50–500m+ | Reinsurance-backed Large tech vendors and critical infrastructure providers only. Negotiated terms; post-CrowdStrike pricing reset.
Layer 2: Upper Excess Layers
$25m xs $25m → $50m xs $50m Lloyd's follow market. Rate: 20–35% of primary rate per layer.
Layer 3: Middle Excess Layers
$10m xs $15m → $15m xs $25m Lloyd's lead + follow. Rate: 35–55% of primary.
Layer 4: First Excess Layer
$5m xs $5m → $10m xs $10m Lloyd's or admitted carrier. Rate: 55–70% of primary.
Layer 5: Primary Layer
$1–10m Admitted carrier (US) or Lloyd's/London (UK/global). All underwriting conditions set; sub-limits for specific perils.
Layer 6: SIR / Deductible
$25k–$500k+ (varies by firm size) Buyer's first loss. No insurance; insured absorbs.

Tech E&O Tower Sizing Logic

Tech E&O towers are sized relative to the insured's largest single client exposure, not total revenue. A $50m revenue SaaS business serving a single $500m banking client may need a $25m+ programme. Post-CrowdStrike (July 2024), catastrophic/systemic update risk is being specifically underwritten — vendors with auto-update mechanisms face mandatory risk management questionnaires.

US vs UK/Lloyd's Market Comparison

Tech E&O underwriting differs significantly between admitted US markets and the London market. Understanding these differences is critical for risk placement and claims outcomes.

Firm Type Programme Structures

Programme design varies significantly by firm size, client concentration, sector, and regulatory exposure. Below are four archetypal structures.

Big Tech Vendor

Profile: Hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP), major software vendors (Microsoft, Oracle, Salesforce).

Limits: $100m–$1bn+ — primarily captive/self-insured. Lloyd's market provides: top excess layers ($100m–$500m+); finite risk structures; catastrophe covers.

Post-CrowdStrike: Vendors with large auto-update install bases face specific systemic loss modelling.

Programme: Captive ($50m SIR) → Lloyd's excess $50m xs $50m → CAT XL → reinsurance.

Mid-Size SaaS/Software Firm

Profile: Revenue $10–100m; 50–500 enterprise clients; limited captive capacity.

Standard Programme: US admitted primary $5m (Travelers/Beazley) + Lloyd's first excess $5m xs $5m + second excess $10m xs $10m. Combined tech E&O + cyber growing in this segment.

SIR: $50k–$250k. Annual premium: $80k–$500k depending on sector.

Key Issue: Contract LoL alignment — policy limit should exceed largest single contract value.

Government IT Contractor

Profile: Accenture, IBM, DXC, Fujitsu-type firms. Government contracts: large absolute values; limited LoL; long project tails.

Programmes: Very large limits ($50–250m+); specific project professional indemnity.

Lloyd's Market: Specialist government contractor PI facilities. Run-off: government projects create long-tail exposure requiring extended reporting periods.

Case Examples: NHS NPfIT / Post Office Horizon illustrate catastrophic long-tail potential.

Telecoms Operator (F5)

Profile: Mobile network operators, ISPs, MVNOs. F5 PI typically written alongside F4 if tech services also provided.

Emergency Services: Emergency services liability (999/112/911) requires specific sublimit or dedicated layer.

Regulatory: Regulatory exposure (Ofcom/FCC fines) generally excluded from PI; separate D&O/regulatory coverage needed.

Lloyd's Facility: Experienced PI syndicates with F5 expertise. MVNO note: MVNO has limited control over underlying network — policy coordination with host MNO critical.

Cyber & Tech E&O Programme Coordination

The cyber/tech E&O interface requires careful programme design. The same event can trigger both policies simultaneously — or fall between them.

Coverage Coordination

Best practice: separate tech E&O and cyber policies with explicit coordination clause defining which policy pays first in dual-trigger scenarios. Avoid 'combined' products for risks over $10m.

Illustrative Case Study: US Healthcare SaaS Provider

ILLUS Case Study

$60m ARR Healthcare SaaS Provider

Profile: US-based clinical decision support SaaS; $60m annual recurring revenue; 85 hospital clients; largest client = 22% of ARR; Microsoft Azure cloud-hosted; contracts have $5m LoL cap per incident; HIPAA-compliant; SOC 2 Type II certified; 3-year clean history; automated CI/CD with full test coverage.

Programme Structure:

• Primary Tech E&O (F4): $10m — Beazley (US admitted) | Premium: ~$432,000 ILLUS
Calculation: $60m × 1.2% × healthcare sector × certification discount

• First Excess Tech E&O: $10m xs $10m — Lloyd's | ~$180,000 ILLUS

• Cyber (CY): $15m — CNA (coordinated with tech E&O) | ~$280,000 ILLUS

• SIR: $100,000 (all policies)

• Total Programme: ~$892,000 ILLUS for $25m combined limit

Lloyd's Advantages:

  • Largest tech PI excess capacity globally — single syndicate lines to $25m; towers to $250m+ for hyperscalers
  • Specialist underwriters: dedicated tech E&O teams with sector expertise (fintech, health tech, critical infrastructure)
  • Speed: Lloyd's electronic placement (PPL) enables same-day binding for clean tech E&O risks under $10m
  • Post-CrowdStrike expertise: Lloyd's market has developed systemic update risk questionnaires and specific coverage frameworks faster than admitted market
  • Cross-class: F4/F5 alongside CY/CZ under coordinated financial lines programme — single claims team, no coverage gap
