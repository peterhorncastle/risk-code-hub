Confidence Badge Legend

All data points in this hub are tagged with one of four confidence levels. Use these to assess data reliability and understand where verification or industry expertise may be required.

VER — Verified

VER

From regulatory filings, published company reports, or court documents. Data is fact-checkable and subject to public disclosure or legal scrutiny.

EST — Estimated

EST

From broker reports, analyst consensus, or calculated via reasonable market assumptions. Directionally reliable but subject to revision as markets evolve.

ILLUS — Illustrative

ILLUS

Worked example or case study for teaching purposes only. Numbers are plausible but not sourced from a specific insured's actual programme. Use for scenario planning, not as pricing guidance.

GAP — Data Gap

GAP

Known limitation: required data is unavailable, confidential, or rapidly evolving. Marked to flag where specialist advice is necessary.

Knowledge Cutoff Warning

May 2025 Knowledge Cutoff

Technology and telecom PI E&O is the most rapidly evolving insurance class. Key areas most likely to have changed since this hub was created:

CrowdStrike Litigation Outcomes: Delta lawsuit ongoing as of May 2025. Actual policy claim payouts and settlement patterns will reshape market expectations.

EU AI Act Implementation: Regulatory framework is being operationalised. AI E&O policy language and exclusion patterns are expected to shift.

AI E&O Coverage Language: No market-standard tech E&O wording for AI/generative AI liability yet exists. Insurer-specific endorsements are developing rapidly.

MSP and Supply-Chain Exclusions: Exclusion wording and prevalence are evolving. Check current market papers for latest restrictions.

CrowdStrike-Related Market Pricing: Post-July 2024, premium and underwriting appetite changes are still in motion.

Always consult a specialist technology PI broker for current market terms.

Data Gap Disclosures

The following six areas represent known limitations in data availability, confidentiality constraints, or rapid evolution. These are flagged throughout the hub with GAP badges.

1

F4/F5 GWP Breakdown

No published data splits Lloyd's tech PI between F4 (technology) and F5 (telecom). Market-wide estimates of $15bn+ global tech E&O GWP include US admitted and surplus lines markets — not separately verified. GAP

2

CrowdStrike Insured Losses

The $800m–$1bn insured loss estimate for CrowdStrike July 2024 is from analyst and broker reports. Delta litigation is ongoing; actual policy claim payouts across the market will not be known for years. The $10bn total economic loss is widely cited but methodologically variable. GAP

3

Delta v CrowdStrike Outcome

As of May 2025 knowledge cutoff, Delta Air Lines' $500m lawsuit against CrowdStrike was active. Outcome unknown. CrowdStrike's contract LoL clause (liability capped at fees paid — approximately $10m) is central to the dispute. GAP

4

NHS NPfIT Contractor Liability

NHS NPfIT total cost (~£9.8bn wasted) is publicly reported, but individual contractor liability settlements (iSOFT, Fujitsu, BT, CSC) are almost entirely confidential under commercial settlement agreements. GAP

5

AI E&O Policy Language

As of May 2025, there was no market-standard tech E&O wording for AI/generative AI liability. Insurer-specific endorsements were developing rapidly. The coverage position for AI hallucination errors, bias claims and EU AI Act non-compliance is unsettled. GAP

6

MSP Supply Chain Exclusions

Post-Kaseya and post-CrowdStrike, many insurers added MSP supply-chain software exclusions. The specific wording, scope and prevalence of these exclusions varies significantly between insurers and is not standardised. GAP
