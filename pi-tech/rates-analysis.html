Market Cycles

Y2K Hard Market
(1999–2002)

Y2K claims and dot-com IT failures harden market sharply. Capacity withdraws. Premiums peak at ~65% above 1995 levels. Few specialist underwriters willing to write tech E&O above £5m limits.

Post-Dot-Com Softening
(2003–2010)

Claims resolve; new capacity enters; market softens consistently. US insurers (AIG, Chubb, Travelers) compete for Lloyd's business. Premium falls ~40% from 2001 peak. Market increasingly views tech E&O as benign class.

SaaS/Cloud Disruption
(2011–2018)

Cloud adoption creates new risk profile — ongoing service vs discrete project. Combined tech E&O + cyber products emerge (SME market). Premium continues to soften. No systemic losses; market complacent. Rate at historic low 2016–2018.

Cyber Interface Hardening
(2019–2022)

Separation of tech E&O and cyber products. NotPetya/SolarWinds/Kaseya events drive awareness of tech liability. Market hardens 25–40% 2020–2022. Specific exclusions added: cyber war, ransomware, supply chain software.

CrowdStrike Recalibration
(2023–2025)

July 2024 CrowdStrike event forces market-wide recalibration of software update risk. Vendors with auto-update mechanisms face significant reunderwriting. Market moderately hardens. AI/ML liability exclusions under development.

AI Era — Emerging Underwriting
(2025–)

EU AI Act and AI hallucination liability create new sub-class of tech PI. Insurers developing specific AI E&O endorsements. Premium for AI-heavy tech firms expected to increase 20–40% over cycle. Market actively developing policy language.
