1. Confidence Badge Framework

Every factual claim in this hub carries an inline confidence badge. The framework is consistent across all Risk Code Hub modules (introduced v2.0).

VER — Verified

Sourced from primary documentation: IMO Convention text, UK statute (MIA 1906), Lloyd's Acts, Lloyd's Register publications, class society rules, PSC MOU official reports, or industry treaty text. High confidence.

EST — Estimate

Derived from market reports (IUMI, Swiss Re, Marsh/Aon annual reviews), broker commentary, industry conferences, or cross-referenced secondary sources. Directionally reliable; not primary-sourced.

ILLUS — Illustrative

Constructed for training purposes to demonstrate underwriting, programme or claims concepts. Based on plausible market parameters but NOT actual placement data. Do not cite commercially.

GAP — Data Gap

Identified area where reliable public data is unavailable. Noted at source; professional market data or Lloyd's internal data required for precision.

2. Knowledge Cutoff & Currency

⏱️ Knowledge Cutoff: May 2025

This hub was compiled using information available to Claude (Anthropic) through May 2025. The marine hull market is dynamic — the following categories are particularly time-sensitive and should be verified against current sources before professional use.

  • Red Sea / Houthi war risk situation — JWC listed area status, current additional premium levels
  • Black Sea war risk — Russia-Ukraine conflict status; vessel seizure / mine risk
  • LMA/JWC cyber exclusion clause developments post-May 2025
  • EV battery fire regulatory requirements for RoRo/car carrier underwriting
  • Lloyd's Decile 10 / market remediation outcomes for marine hull lines
  • Final Dali / Francis Scott Key Bridge insurance settlement figures
  • Autonomous vessel / smart shipping regulatory developments (IMO MSC)
  • MARPOL Annex VI CII (Carbon Intensity Indicator) implementation and underwriting impact
  • Any IMO amendments to SOLAS or ISM Code
  • PSC blacklist / whitelist changes (Paris MOU, Tokyo MOU annual reports)

5. Data Gap Disclosures

Identified areas where public data is insufficient to support VER-level claims. Professional market sources or Lloyd's internal data are required.

GAP 01 — Lloyd's Marine Hull GWP by Risk Code (B / T / TS / TX)

Lloyd's does not publish granular GWP breakdowns by individual marine risk code. The total global ocean hull market GWP (~$7bn) is an IUMI estimate aggregated from member returns. The Lloyd's market share and the split between codes T, TS and TX cannot be precisely stated from public sources. Used in: Overview (Module 01), Rates Analysis (Module 05).

GAP 02 — Actual Hull Rate on Line Benchmarks

Hull rate on line (ROL) data is commercially sensitive and not published by Lloyd's, syndicates or brokers. The benchmark rates presented in the Underwriting and Rates modules (e.g. VLCC 0.25–0.45%) are derived from broker market commentary, IUMI/AGCS reports and practitioner interviews reported in trade press (Lloyd's List, Marine Insurance International). Actual rates at placement will differ materially based on fleet quality, market conditions, and negotiation.

GAP 03 — Costa Concordia Final Insured Loss Breakdown

The total insured loss from Costa Concordia (hull + wreck removal + P&I + third-party liability) is widely cited at approximately $2.0–2.5 billion but no audited final settlement figure is publicly available. The hull value ($512m) is better documented; the wreck removal figure (~$1.5bn) is from Costa Cruises press releases and Italian government announcements. The split between hull and P&I at final settlement is not publicly available. Used in: History (Module 02), Timeline (Module 03), Claims Database (Module 04).

GAP 04 — Red Sea War Risk — Final 2024 Premium Volume

The total additional war risk premium generated by the Red Sea crisis in 2024 (~$400m annualised estimate) is based on market commentary and broker estimates. No audited premium data has been published. The JWC listed area additional premium rates (0.5–1%+ per voyage) are from broker market reports and trade press, not from JWC official publications. The situation remains dynamic; premiums have fluctuated significantly with each incident.

GAP 05 — Dali / Francis Scott Key Bridge — Final Insurance Settlement

The Francis Scott Key Bridge collapse (26 March 2024) is one of the most complex maritime casualty events in US modern history. At the knowledge cutoff (May 2025), litigation is ongoing between the Dali's owners/P&I club and various claimants including the State of Maryland (bridge reconstruction) and commercial interests affected by port closure. Final settlement figures are unknown. The $2bn+ total liability figure referenced in this hub is based on media reports and analyst estimates, not court findings.

GAP 06 — EV Battery Fire — Underwriting Loading Data for Car Carriers

Following the Felicity Ace (2022) and increasing EV vehicle volumes on RoRo vessels, underwriters have applied significant loadings to car carrier hull rates. The specific quantum of the EV-related loading (cited as 15–30% in this hub) is based on trade press reports and broker commentary rather than published tariff data. Individual underwriter loadings vary significantly; no industry-wide standardised EV surcharge exists. This is a rapidly evolving underwriting issue.

6. Methodology & Disclaimer

This hub was compiled by Claude (Anthropic) as a training resource for Lloyd's market insurance professionals. It is intended to support the education of trainee underwriters and is not a substitute for professional market guidance, primary treaty / regulatory texts, or syndicate-specific underwriting authorities.

Marine hull insurance is a specialist class with significant individual underwriter judgement. Rates, limits, deductibles, wordings and appetite vary substantially between Lloyd's syndicates and across the market cycle. Any figures marked ILLUS are constructed for illustrative/training purposes and must not be used as reference rates for actual placements. Figures marked EST are estimates from secondary or market commentary sources and carry meaningful uncertainty.

This resource is maintained as a living document. Users are encouraged to verify all time-sensitive data — war risk listed areas, hull rates, PSC statistics, IMO regulatory developments — against current sources before use. The Risk Code Hub carries a version date; refer to the build tracker for the current version.
