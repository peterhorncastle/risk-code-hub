Marine Hull Insurance

Physical damage cover for oceangoing vessels — tankers, bulk carriers, containerships, passenger ships and their machinery. Includes Builders' Risk (B), Hull Time (T), Total Loss Only (TS), and Loss of Hire / Additional Perils (TX). One of the oldest classes of insurance, governed by the Marine Insurance Act 1906 and the Institute Time Clauses (Hulls) 1983 / International Hull Clauses 2003.

Lloyd's Risk Code Structure

CodeClassCoverage ScopePolicy BasisTypical Limit
B Builders' Risk Physical damage to a vessel during construction at a shipyard. Covers hull plating, machinery, outfitting equipment from keel-laying through sea trials and delivery. Includes third-party liability for damage to dry-dock, yard equipment, or adjacent vessels. Also covers faulty workmanship damage to the vessel itself (subject to exclusions). All-risks; typically on a "builder's risk" policy basis aligned to the shipbuilding contract value $5m – $500m+ per vessel
T Hull & Machinery (Time) All-risks physical damage cover for the hull, machinery and equipment of an operational vessel. Written on a time basis (typically 12 months). Principal perils: collision, grounding, stranding, fire, explosion, piracy, negligence of crew or master. Covers 3/4 collision liability (with P&I covering the remaining 1/4). Standard Lloyd's hull slip; worded on ITC Hulls 1983 or IHC 2003 clauses. Institute Time Clauses (Hulls) 1/11/83 or International Hull Clauses 1/11/03 (IHC 2003) $10m – $500m+ per vessel
TS Total Loss Only (TLO) Restricted form of hull cover paying only on total loss (actual or constructive). Does not respond to partial losses, machinery breakdown, or collision damage below CTL threshold. Typically used for older vessels where full H&M cover is uneconomic or unavailable. May be combined with a separate machinery breakdown policy. Often written with a higher excess than full H&M. ITC Hulls TLO Clauses or manuscript basis; constructive total loss threshold typically >75–100% insured value $2m – $100m per vessel
TX Loss of Hire / Additional Perils TX covers consequential losses arising from an insured hull event. Loss of Hire (LOH): daily hire rate for period vessel is off-hire due to an insured damage. Additional Perils: optional extensions to the standard H&M policy — sue and labour, salvage, towage, scrap/wreck removal, freight interest. Cyber or parametric hull extensions also placed under TX in some syndicates. ITC Loss of Hire Clauses 20/7/87; written as a separate slip supplementary to the H&M policy LOH: $5,000–$50,000/day × 90–180 days

Coverage Architecture

⚓ Hull & Machinery (H&M) — Core

  • Physical damage to hull, machinery and equipment
  • Collision (3/4 RDC — Running Down Clause)
  • Grounding, stranding, sinking
  • Fire, explosion, lightning
  • Perils of the sea — weather damage
  • Negligence of master, crew or pilots
  • Latent defect causing loss (Sue & Labour)

🏗️ Builders' Risk (B)

  • All-risks during shipyard construction
  • Keel-laying through sea trials & delivery
  • Third-party liability — yard / dry-dock damage
  • Faulty workmanship (to resultant damage only)
  • Launching accidents
  • Transit of components (pre-keel)
  • Typically follows Lloyds SG/MAR form + institute clauses

📉 Total Loss Only (TS)

  • Actual total loss (ATL): vessel completely destroyed/lost
  • Constructive total loss (CTL): repair cost exceeds insured value
  • Missing vessel: presumed lost after reasonable time
  • No partial loss recovery
  • No machinery breakdown cover
  • More economic for older/smaller vessels
  • Often layered with separate P&I and FD&D

⏱️ Loss of Hire (TX)

  • Daily hire rate while vessel under repair (insured peril)
  • Deductible: typically 14–30 day waiting period
  • Maximum indemnity: 90–180 days (typical)
  • Covers temporary repair delays
  • Complementary to H&M — same insured peril trigger
  • Dry-dock delays at agreed daily rate
  • Parametric LOH structures emerging (post-2020)

💰 Increased Value (IV)

  • Covers difference between insured value and market value
  • Responds only on total loss (actual or constructive)
  • Separate policy supplementary to H&M
  • Freight interest IV also available
  • Typically 25% of H&M insured value maximum
  • Important for vessels with strong second-hand market

🔧 Additional Perils (AP)

  • Extends ITC/IHC cover for excluded perils
  • Breakdown of machinery / boiler explosion
  • Crew negligence extensions
  • Wreck removal / scrap costs
  • Towage and salvage extensions
  • Cyber risk extensions (post-BIMCO 2019)

⚓ The P&I Club Interface — What Hull Does NOT Cover

Marine hull (T/TS/TX) and Protection & Indemnity (P&I) club cover are complementary and deliberately non-overlapping. Understanding the boundary is critical for underwriters and brokers:

  • Hull covers: Physical damage to the insured vessel; 3/4 collision liability (Running Down Clause — RDC); salvage of the vessel; Sue & Labour charges; GA (General Average) contributions.
  • P&I covers: 1/4 collision liability (not in hull); cargo damage/shortage liability; crew injury/death/repatriation; wreck removal and pollution (MARPOL); passenger and stevedore liability; war/strike-related crew liability.
  • General Average (GA): When a deliberate sacrifice saves the common venture (e.g. jettisoning cargo), costs are shared between hull and cargo underwriters according to GA rules (York-Antwerp Rules 2016). VER
  • The 1/4 collision issue: Standard ITC Hull only covers 3/4 of collision liability for damage to third-party vessels. The remaining 1/4 sits with P&I. Some hull policies extend to 4/4 RDC by endorsement — critical to check on large container vessel covers.

Market Participants

The Lloyd's marine hull market is one of the oldest and most internationally competitive insurance markets. London remains a dominant pricing centre for large, complex and unusual tonnage, but faces competition from Scandinavian and Asian markets for standard tonnage. EST
