1. Confidence Badge System

Every claim, figure, or assertion in this hub is tagged with one of four confidence badges. These badges appear inline throughout the hub pages as small coloured chips. The classification system is documented in full at the Risk Code Hub Methodology & Sources page.

VER — VERIFIED

Sourced from a named public authority, regulatory document, or industry body publication that can be cited and verified. High confidence.

EST — ESTIMATE

A professional market estimate derived from published data ranges, broker guidance, or synthesised industry data. Directionally reliable; exact figures should be confirmed from primary sources.

ILLUS — ILLUSTRATIVE

A constructed example or hypothetical scenario built for educational purposes. Based on plausible market parameters but not reflecting any actual risk, policy, or transaction.

GAP — DATA GAP

A known unknown — a figure or fact that would be material to the analysis but for which no reliable public data is available. Flagged for awareness.

Knowledge cutoff: This hub was built using information available up to May 2025 (AI training knowledge cutoff). Market data, regulatory changes, loss figures, and Flood Re cession rates should be verified against current sources. The hub will be updated as new versions are published — check the footer version stamp.

2. Source List

Regulatory & Statutory Sources

  • VER
    FCA PS21/5 — General Insurance Pricing Practices
    FCA Policy Statement on GIPP reform; effective January 2022. Primary source for loyalty pricing statistics (6m customers, £1.2bn aggregate penalty, 3m home insurance customers).
    fca.org.uk/publications/policy-statements/ps21-5-general-insurance-pricing-practices
  • VER
    Water Act 2014 — Flood Re Statutory Framework
    UK statute establishing Flood Re Ltd and its mandate; including the 2039 transition requirement. Flood Re eligibility criteria (pre-2009 buildings) are set by secondary legislation under this Act.
    legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/2014/21/contents
  • VER
    Flood Re — Scheme Rules & Quinquennial Review 2022
    Flood Re scheme documentation including current cession bands, eligibility criteria, PFR commitment, and transition roadmap to 2039. Source for council tax band ceded premiums, £250 excess, and PFR strategy.
    floodre.co.uk/industry/
  • VER
    Environment Agency — Flood Map for Planning
    EA definitive flood zone mapping (Zones 1, 2, 3a, 3b) used in HP underwriting. Free public access tool for postcode/property-level flood zone assessment.
    flood-map-for-planning.service.gov.uk
  • VER
    HMRC — Insurance Premium Tax
    IPT at 12% standard rate. Source for current tax rate applied to UK household policies.
    gov.uk/guidance/insurance-premium-tax

Industry Bodies & Association Data

  • VER
    Association of British Insurers (ABI) — Market Statistics
    ABI published UK household market statistics; subsidence claims data (£3bn cumulative 1988–1995); summer 2007 floods (£3bn, 55,000 homes); Great Storm 1987 (£2bn); Burns Day 1990; winter 2013–14 storms (£1.1bn). ABI is the primary source for verified UK household market loss figures.
    abi.org.uk/data-and-resources/tools-and-resources/claims/
  • VER
    BCIS (Building Cost Information Service) — Rebuilding Cost Index
    BCIS Rebuilding Cost Index and BCIS House Rebuilding Cost Index. Source for +28% rebuild cost increase 2021–2023; automated rebuild cost assessment tools referenced in underwriting module.
    bcis.co.uk
  • VER
    British Geological Survey — National Shrink-Swell Susceptibility Dataset
    BGS GeoSure subsidence hazard datasets; Zones S1–S4 (low to very high). Developed in response to 1989–1991 subsidence crisis. Core tool for HP subsidence loading. Free public access via BGS National Geoscience Data Centre.
    bgs.ac.uk/datasets/national-geoscience-data-centre/
  • VER
    Lloyd's of London — Market Results & Franchise Standards
    Lloyd's annual reports, market results (combined ratios by class), Syndicate Business Forecasting parameters, and Central Fund financial security rating. AM Best rating A (Excellent) sourced from Lloyd's published investor relations.
    lloyds.com/about-lloyds/annual-report

Research, Academic & Specialist Sources

  • EST
    Swiss Re sigma — UK P&C Market Data
    Swiss Re sigma World Insurance Reports; UK P&C combined ratio estimates; storm loss event summaries (Eunice, Babet, Ciaran). Used for combined ratio trend data and event loss verification where ABI data is pending. Note: sigma figures are published with a lag and may not reflect final settled loss.
    swissre.com/institute/research/sigma-research.html
  • EST
    JBA Risk Management — UK Flood Data
    JBA high-resolution UK flood hazard datasets (1m resolution); used by Lloyd's syndicates for flood zone scoring beyond EA flood map classifications. Source of per-property flood return period estimates referenced in underwriting module.
    jbarisk.com
  • EST
    CIRIA C790 — Guidance on the Use of Property Flood Resilience
    Industry standard guidance for PFR measures (resistance and resilience categories); specification standards for flood doors, barriers, sump/pump systems. Referenced in risk mitigation module.
    ciria.org — C790 Guidance
  • EST
    RICS — Valuation & Building Surveys
    RICS guidance on rebuild cost assessment, listed building valuation, and RICS Level 3 Building Survey methodology. Source for professional valuation thresholds referenced in underwriting and risk mitigation modules.
    rics.org

Regulatory / Government (Non-Insurance)

  • VER
    MHCLG — UK Housing Statistics (Home Ownership Rates)
    Ministry of Housing, Communities & Local Government English Housing Survey. Source for home ownership rates (30% in 1918; 67% by 1990 cited in history module); 26m dwellings (2024 estimate); 5.2m properties at flood risk (EA estimate).
    gov.uk/english-housing-survey
  • VER
    Met Office — UK Storm Records & Extreme Weather Events
    Met Office official records for Great Storm 1987 (122 mph winds, 18 million trees); Burns Day 1990 storm; summer 2022 temperature record (40.3°C). Used to verify event characterisation in history and timeline modules.
    metoffice.gov.uk/weather/learn-about/weather/case-studies

4. Known Data Gaps

The following are material data gaps — areas where figures or analysis would benefit from primary commercial data that is not publicly available.

GAP Lloyd's HP Rate Bands — Commercially Sensitive

Exact rate bands, technical premium calculations, and portfolio combined ratios for Lloyd's HP syndicates are commercially sensitive and not published. The rate and ROE data in this hub are constructed from public ABI, Swiss Re, and FCA data and represent directional market estimates only. Lloyd's syndicates operating in the HP class should use internal actuarial data.

GAP Net Zero Retrofit Loss Statistics — Too Early

Heat pump fires, solar panel faults, and EV charging incidents are emerging loss categories with insufficient credible statistical data as of early 2026. References to these risks in the underwriting and risk mitigation modules are based on early specialist insurer loss experience reports and engineering studies, not actuarial loss data. This area should be monitored closely as the retrofit install base grows.

GAP 2023–2024 Storm Event Final Settled Losses

Storms Babet (October 2023) and Ciaran (November 2023) loss estimates used in the timeline and database modules are preliminary ABI/Swiss Re estimates. Final settled insured losses may differ materially, particularly for Babet which involved significant commercial and agricultural exposure in Scotland and the east of England. Update when ABI publishes final settled figures.

GAP Flood Re Council Tax Band Cession Rates — Verify Current Year

The Flood Re ceded premium bands quoted in the underwriting module are estimates based on the scheme's published levy structure and 2024/25 approximate band levels. Flood Re revises these periodically. Always verify current cession rates directly with Flood Re or via the managing agent's Flood Re reporting portal before using in pricing.

GAP UHNW Programme Pricing — Not in Public Domain

The worked example premium calculations in the underwriting and global programmes modules are illustrative constructions based on available market data. Actual HNW policy pricing by Hiscox, Chubb, AIG Private Client, and similar insurers is not publicly disclosed. Actual programme prices will vary significantly by risk quality, claims history, and current market cycle conditions.
