1. Why HNW Household Requires Programme Thinking

Mass-market household insurance is a single-property, single-jurisdiction product. At the HNW level — and particularly for the ultra-high-net-worth (UHNW) clients served by Lloyd's HP syndicates — the risk portfolio is typically multi-property, multi-jurisdiction, and multi-line. A typical UHNW client may own a primary UK estate, a London townhouse, a villa in Tuscany or the South of France, a ski chalet in Switzerland, a New York apartment, and a Caribbean property. Each of these exposures sits in a different regulatory jurisdiction, has different local insurer requirements, and creates aggregate accumulation that a single policy cannot address efficiently. EST

Lloyd's HP syndicates — particularly through the major specialist HNW insurers (Hiscox, Chubb, AIG Private Client, Beazley) — have developed programme structures that provide:

  • Single master policy with coordinated local policies (admitted/surplus lines) in each jurisdiction
  • Blanket contents and fine art cover across all properties under one valuation framework
  • Aggregate deductible structures and claims handling coordination via a single lead underwriter
  • Consolidated renewal and premium financing across the portfolio

2. Illustrative HNW Multi-Property Programme Structure

Ultra-HNW Umbrella / Catastrophic Loss Layer

Excess of £10m — Aggregate catastrophic loss (e.g. estate fire, multiple-location nat cat)

Specialist market capacity; Lloyd's XL slip. Rarely triggered — provides UHNW peace of mind for total estate loss.

Excess Layer 2 — All Properties

£5m xs £2m per occurrence

Covers major structural losses; large fine art / jewellery single losses. Lloyd's surplus lines capacity.

Excess Layer 1 — All Properties

£1m xs £1m per occurrence

Second-tier excess; activated on major house fires, significant flood events, large contents losses.

Primary Master Policy — UK Properties

£1m per occurrence buildings + contents; fine art / jewellery scheduled

Lead Lloyd's syndicate; covers UK primary residence and all UK properties. Flood Re eligible properties ceded where applicable. HNW agreed value basis.

Local Admitted Policies — Overseas Properties

Locally admitted cover per jurisdiction; difference-in-conditions (DIC) gap-filled by master

France, Italy, Switzerland, USA, Caribbean as applicable. Local regulation requires admitted insurer. Master policy provides DIC for gaps between local policy and master policy scope.

Self-Insured Retention / High Excess

£2,500 – £25,000 per claim depending on peril and property

UHNW clients often prefer significant self-insurance to reduce premium and avoid small-claims reporting. Aggregate deductible structures available for high-frequency, low-severity perils (escape of water, minor theft).

3. Jurisdiction-Specific Considerations

🇬🇧 United Kingdom (Primary Market)

  • FCA regulated; Lloyd's HP syndicates operate under Lloyd's franchise authorisation VER
  • Flood Re scheme available for pre-2009 eligible properties
  • 12% Insurance Premium Tax (IPT) on most household premiums VER
  • FCA GIPP rules (January 2022): renewal pricing constraints apply
  • High-net-worth: specialist broker intermediation (Marsh Private Client, Aon Private Risk Management, broker networks)

🇫🇷 France

  • Insurance of immovable property (buildings) must typically be placed with an admitted insurer; EEA/UK post-Brexit position changed post-2021 — Lloyd's operates via Lloyd's Brussels for EEA business VER
  • Natural catastrophe (Cat Nat) scheme: compulsory supplementary cover for nat cat losses (flood, earthquake, subsidence) funded by a levy on property policies VER
  • Taxe d'Assurance applies; rates vary by cover type
  • French holiday villas: significant exposure to Var/Côte d'Azur wildfire risk (rising with climate change)
ACPRLloyd's BrusselsCat Nat Scheme

🇮🇹 Italy

  • Buildings insurance is NOT compulsory in Italy (unlike France, where mortgage requires it) — low market penetration historically
  • IVASS (Istituto per la Vigilanza sulle Assicurazioni) regulates; local admitted insurer required for property
  • Major earthquake risk — Italy has the highest seismic risk in Western Europe; most standard property policies exclude or heavily restrict earthquake EST
  • Tuscany/Umbria villas: stone construction typical; often unregistered improvements present
IVASSDIC Available

🇨🇭 Switzerland

  • Cantonal monopoly system: most Swiss cantons have a state-monopoly building insurer (Kantonale Gebäudeversicherung — KGV) that is compulsory for buildings. Contents only placed with private insurers. VER
  • Geneva and Wallis are the only cantons where private building insurance is permitted
  • KGV cover is compulsory and cannot be replaced by a Lloyd's master policy for Swiss buildings
  • Primary challenge: contents cover and liability layer where private market access is available
FINMAKGV Mandatory

🇺🇸 United States

  • State-regulated market; surplus lines admitted in most states for non-standard HNW risks. Lloyd's surplus lines market well-established.
  • National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP): federally-backed flood insurance; private market alternatives growing. VER
  • Wildfire risk: California and Western states have experienced dramatic premium increases and insurer withdrawals from 2023 onwards. EST
  • US vacation properties owned by UK UHNW: typically require local admitted or surplus lines placement; DIC from Lloyd's master policy possible
State-regulatedSurplus LinesNFIP

🌴 Caribbean & Overseas Territories

  • Hurricane/tropical cyclone is the dominant peril; PML modelling essential
  • Some UK Overseas Territories (Cayman, BVI, Bermuda) have established sophisticated (re)insurance markets and Lloyd's approved fronting carriers
  • Demand surge post-hurricane (Irma 2017, Maria 2017) dramatically increased reconstruction costs and claims timelines VER
  • Contents theft risk elevated in unoccupied/holiday-only properties; seasonal occupancy limits must be clear in policy wording

5. Captive Structures for Ultra-HNW

For UHNW individuals with property portfolios exceeding £50–100m in total insured value, captive insurance arrangements may be cost-effective. A personal lines captive is unusual but not uncommon for family offices with diversified asset portfolios. EST

StructureMechanismThreshold / Viability
Segregated Account / Protected CellThe UHNW individual funds a cell within a Protected Cell Company (PCC) in an approved jurisdiction (Guernsey, BVI, Cayman). The cell acts as the first-loss layer; Lloyd's or reinsurance market provides XL above the cell retention.Viable from ~£500k–£1m annual premium. Requires family office infrastructure and ongoing captive management.
Single-Parent CaptiveDedicated insurance entity owned by the UHNW individual or family trust. Writes all personal lines risks (homes, fine art, yachts, vehicles) and buys external reinsurance for catastrophic exposure.Viable from ~£1m–£2m annual premium. Full captive management cost (domicile, audit, actuarial) ~£80k–£150k/year. EST
Family Office Risk PoolSeveral UHNW individuals (e.g. through a private bank or wealth manager) share a captive structure to achieve scale. Risk pooling improves actuarial credibility.Emerging model; requires trust between participants and carefully drafted loss-sharing agreement.

⚠️ Captive Household Insurance: Tax and Regulatory Considerations

  • HMRC scrutiny: Personal lines captives are subject to HMRC review to ensure premiums are arm's-length and the captive has genuine risk transfer. Premiums must not be treated as tax-sheltered personal expenditure.
  • FCA notification: A captive writing UK personal lines must be authorised or exempt from authorisation under FSMA 2000. Offshore captives writing UK risk must comply with Solvency II / Overseas Insurer requirements post-Brexit.
  • Claims service: Captive structures can complicate claims service — UHNW clients expecting concierge-level service should ensure captive arrangements do not create delays in loss settlement.

7. Difference-in-Conditions (DIC) Structuring

DIC policies are critical in international household programme structures. Where local admitted policies are required (as in France, Italy, Switzerland) but provide narrower cover than the Lloyd's master policy standard, a DIC layer fills the gap. EST

DIC ApplicationWhat DIC FillsStructuring Note
France — Cat Nat GapsFrench Cat Nat scheme covers nat cat at standard indemnity. DIC fills: agreed value uplift, contents extensions, fine art cover beyond French policy limits, higher limits for overseas guests' belongingsLloyd's DIC must specify exactly what gaps it fills; claims cooperation clause with French carrier essential
Italy — Earthquake ExclusionIf Italian admitted policy excludes earthquake (common), DIC can provide earthquake cover for the structure; SASE (specific all-risk seismic extension) format usedPML modelling for Italian seismic risk essential; Lloyd's ICA data
US — Differences in DeductibleUS surplus lines policy may have hurricane/named-storm deductible of 2–5% of insured value. DIC can provide the deductible layer above the UK master policy level.Careful coordination needed — US insurer must confirm acceptance of DIC arrangement
Worldwide Fine Art / Jewellery BlanketLocal policies rarely cover fine art and jewellery adequately. Master policy provides blanket worldwide fine art cover that sits above or alongside local policies in each jurisdictionScheduling and valuation management is the key challenge; consistent valuation methodology across jurisdictions

8. Lloyd's Programme Advantages for HP

AdvantageDetail
Single Lead UnderwriterOne Lloyd's syndicate leads the entire programme; consistent approach to coverage, claims, and risk management across all properties and jurisdictions
Claims CoordinationLloyd's Claims Protocol ensures coordinated claims handling; for multi-property losses (e.g. simultaneous storm damage to UK estate and French chateau), a single claims handler manages the process
Agreed Value (No Under-Insurance Risk)HNW Lloyd's policies typically provide agreed value basis — insurer, not insured, carries the risk of under-valuation. Post-BCIS inflation this is a significant advantage over standard policies.
Bespoke Wording FlexibilityLloyd's HP wordings can be individually negotiated for UHNW clients — covering specific exclusions, extending to unusual risks (K&R extension, nuclear liability exclusion override), or providing novel covers (EV charging liability, smart home cyber)
Financial SecurityLloyd's Central Fund (mutual guarantee) provides policyholders with security beyond individual syndicate solvency. Rated A (Excellent) by AM Best. VER
