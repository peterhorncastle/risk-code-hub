Lloyd's Risk Code

CodeClassScopeLloyd's Market Role
HP Household Property (UK) Buildings and contents insurance for UK residential properties. Covers standard and non-standard construction, all occupancy types, HNW homes, listed buildings, and flood-risk properties. Peril set: fire, storm, flood, subsidence, escape of water, theft, malicious damage, accidental damage (extension). Personal possessions all-risks extension available. Lloyd's syndicates (principally Hiscox, Brit, Beazley) occupy the specialist and HNW segment. The mass-market (Aviva, Direct Line, LV=, Admiral) is outside Lloyd's. HP code at Lloyd's is predominantly: HNW (rebuild value £1m+), non-standard construction (listed, thatched, timber frame), flood zone properties, let and holiday homes, unoccupied properties.

Coverage Structure

Buildings Insurance

Physical damage to the structure, fixtures, and fittings. Reinstatement (rebuild) cost basis. Perils: FLEXA (Fire, Lightning, Explosion, Aircraft), storm, flood, subsidence/heave/landslip, escape of water, malicious damage, theft. Architects' and surveyors' fees, debris removal, temporary accommodation included.

Contents Insurance

Household goods, furniture, furnishings, clothing, electronics. New-for-old or indemnity basis. Same peril set as buildings. Single article limits apply. High-value items (jewellery, art, watches, tech) typically require specification above threshold. Accidental damage extension available.

High Net Worth (HNW)

Blanket or scheduled cover for properties with high rebuild values and valuable contents. Agreed value basis for specified items — no average/under-insurance argument. Fine art, jewellery, wine collections, antiques. Personal possessions worldwide. Staff/domestic workers extension. Loss of rent. Alternative accommodation — no sublimit.

Flood Risk Properties

Properties in EA Flood Zone 2 or 3 (medium/high flood risk). Standard market may decline or price prohibitively — Flood Re or specialist Lloyd's cover required. Cover for flood damage to buildings and contents, temporary accommodation, alternative premises. Drying out costs. Section 6 Flood Re eligible risks: pre-2009 build, non-commercial.

Non-Standard Construction

Listed buildings (Grade I, II*, II), thatched properties, timber frame, concrete-framed (Airey/Reema/Cornish houses), steel-framed. Standard market typically declines. Lloyd's specialist market provides cover with reinstatement conditions (like-for-like rebuild for listed). Higher rates reflect rebuilding cost uncertainty and specialist contractor requirements.

Legal Expenses & Home Emergency

Commonly bundled with HP: legal expenses for property disputes, employment, personal injury (£50,000–£100,000 limit). Home emergency: boiler/heating failure, plumbing, pest infestation, lost keys (typically £500–£1,500 per call-out). These ancillary covers are significant premium contributors in the mass market.

Flood Re — The UK's Flood Cross-Subsidy Scheme

What Is Flood Re?

Flood Re is a reinsurance scheme launched in April 2016, jointly established by the UK Government and insurance industry (ABI members). It allows insurers to cede the flood element of high-risk household policies to a central pool (Flood Re Ltd) at fixed ceded premiums based on council tax band — irrespective of actual flood risk. The scheme is cross-subsidised by a levy on all UK household insurers (~£180m per year).

Eligibility: Standard household policies for properties built before 1 January 2009. Excludes: properties built post-2009 (moral hazard avoidance), commercial properties, leasehold flats where buildings are managed by freeholder, purpose-built blocks of flats.

Impact on Lloyd's HP: Lloyd's syndicates writing HP may participate in Flood Re for eligible risks. The scheme has expanded the insurable market for flood-risk properties but creates a cliff-edge for post-2009 properties. Transition plan: Flood Re is designed to wind down by 2039, with premiums gradually rising to reflect actual risk — intended to incentivise property-level flood resilience over the 20-year period.

Lloyd's Market Position — Who Writes HP?

The Two-Tier UK Household Market

Mass market (outside Lloyd's): Aviva (~17% market share), Direct Line Group (Churchill, Green Flag, Privilege, ~15%), LV=, Zurich, AXA, Admiral, NFU Mutual, Ageas. Price-comparison (PCW) dominated. Vanilla risks, standard construction, high-volume low-premium business. FCA GIPP reform (Jan 2022) heavily impacted this segment by eliminating loyalty pricing differentials.

Specialist / Lloyd's market: Hiscox (market leader — HNW and non-standard), Brit, Beazley, MAPFRE, and several specialist MGA/coverholder operations. Focus: rebuild values £500k+, HNW contents, listed buildings, flood zone 3, let properties, unoccupied properties, holiday homes. Typically placed through specialist brokers (Aon Private Client, Marsh Private Client, Howden, Schroders Personal Wealth).

Lloyd's HP GWP: Not published separately. Estimated at £200m–£400m within Lloyd's total property account. EST — based on Hiscox UK household reported revenues and Lloyd's Syndicate Business Forecasts.
