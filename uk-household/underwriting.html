1. Buildings Rating — Core Methodology

UK household buildings rating at Lloyd's is driven primarily by rebuild cost (not market value), location-specific peril loadings, and construction type. The BCIS (Building Cost Information Service) Rebuild Cost database is the industry standard for estimating the cost of reinstating a property to the same specification. VER Sum insured should reflect full rebuild cost inclusive of demolition, professional fees (typically 10–15% uplift), and VAT.

Buildings Premium Formula
Annual Premium = Rebuild Cost (£) × Base Rate (per £1,000) × Flood Loading Factor × Subsidence Loading Factor × Construction Factor × Occupancy Factor
+ Fixed Perils Add-ons (accidental damage, legal expenses) if included

Base Rate (Standard Construction)

Masonry/brick detached house, low-risk postcode, owner-occupied. Base rate drives standard-risk pricing before any loadings.

£0.90 – £1.40 per £1,000 SI EST

Occupancy Factor

Owner-occupied is base 1.0. Let property (buy-to-let/HMO) typically loads 20–40%. Holiday let or short-term furnished holiday letting (FHL) loads 30–60%. Unoccupied >30 days loads 50–200% depending on duration.

Factor: 1.0 – 3.0 EST

EA Flood Zone Loading — Zone 2

Environment Agency Flood Zone 2 (medium probability flood plain, 1-in-100 to 1-in-1,000 year return). Material loading applied. Flood Re eligibility assessed.

Loading: +25% – +60% EST

EA Flood Zone Loading — Zone 3

Environment Agency Flood Zone 3 (high probability, >1% annual chance fluvial flood or >0.5% tidal). Significant loading or referral to Flood Re. Zone 3b functional floodplain may be uninsurable in standard market.

Loading: +60% – +200%+ EST

Subsidence Loading — High Shrink-Swell

BGS National Shrink-Swell Susceptibility Dataset Zones H3/H4 (high/very high). Covers central/south London, Essex, Kent, East Anglia. Loading applied to buildings premium only.

Loading: +15% – +50% EST

Tree Proximity Factor

High-water-demand trees (oak, willow, poplar, elm) within 5m of foundations in a high shrink-swell zone are a material underwriting consideration. Survey may be required. Some risks declined in standard market.

Surcharge: +10% – +30% EST

3. Contents Rating

Contents rating at Lloyd's specialist level moves beyond the standard bedroom-count thumb rules used by mass-market insurers. For HNW policies, contents sum insured is individually declared or blanket-assessed. Key rating factors:

Rating FactorDetail
Sum Insured BasisNew-for-old (standard) vs indemnity (older/rental policies). New-for-old is market standard at Lloyd's for HNW; indemnity basis materially reduces premium but must be clearly disclosed.
Single Article LimitStandard policies apply an unspecified single article limit (typically £1,500–£2,500). Items above this limit must be scheduled and specified. Jewellery, watches, fine art, and wine collections are major scheduling categories for HNW risks.
High-Value ItemsJewellery, watches, fine art, antiques, wine/spirits, musical instruments. Typically rated at 0.5%–2.0% of declared value per annum depending on storage (in-home vs vault) and security. Professional valuation certificates required for items >£10,000–£25,000.
Home Office / Business EquipmentPost-COVID working-from-home has increased average laptop/equipment values in-situ. Many standard policies limit business equipment at home to £2,000–£5,000. Lloyd's policies may extend to full replacement value.
All-Risks Extension (Away from Home)Extends contents cover to items temporarily removed from the home (clothing, jewellery, laptops while travelling). Rated at approximately 0.2%–0.5% of all-risks value per annum.

4. HNW Methodology — Agreed Value & Blanket Cover

Lloyd's HNW household underwriting departs significantly from mass-market methodology. The core distinction is the move from sum-insured indemnity or new-for-old to agreed value and comprehensive blanket coverage — most prominently adopted by Hiscox, Chubb, and AIG Private Client Group. 5. Flood Re Interaction

🌊 Flood Re — Underwriting Mechanics

Flood Re is a reinsurance scheme operating behind the direct insurer. The underwriter issues the policy as normal; the insurer then cedes the flood risk element to Flood Re at a fixed premium set by council tax band. The insurer retains the non-flood perils (fire, storm, subsidence, theft, escape of water). VER

Eligibility criteria (all must apply):

  • UK domestic property only (commercial risks excluded) VER
  • Property must have been built before 1 January 2009 — post-2009 properties are NOT eligible VER
  • Purpose-built blocks of flats (leasehold): individual flats eligible only if the building itself is eligible
  • Eligibility applies to both buildings and contents policies separately

Excess: Flood Re sets a standard flood excess of £250 per claim, which the insurer must apply. Insurers can charge a higher excess if they wish. VER

Transition to 2039: Flood Re's mandate requires it to transition to risk-reflective pricing by 2039 (as per Water Act 2014). From 2039, flood risk in high-flood-zone properties will increasingly be priced at actuarial cost, potentially making some properties uninsurable at affordable premiums. The Property Flood Resilience (PFR) programme is the government's primary mitigation strategy. VER

⚠️ Post-2009 Properties: No Flood Re Eligibility

Properties built after 1 January 2009 are ineligible for Flood Re by statute. This means that new-build properties in flood zones must be rated at full actuarial flood cost. Developers have historically built in Flood Zone 2/3 areas post-2009 — particularly urban waterside developments — which can result in high flood loadings or market exclusion. Underwriters should verify build date from the Land Registry or planning records before assuming Flood Re eligibility.
