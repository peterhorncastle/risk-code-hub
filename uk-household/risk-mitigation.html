1. Flood Resilience — Property Flood Resilience (PFR)

Property Flood Resilience (PFR) is the framework for measures that either prevent floodwater entering a property (resistance) or reduce the damage caused when water does enter (resilience). Flood Re actively promotes PFR adoption and the government's 2020 National Flood and Coastal Erosion Risk Management Strategy commits to 50% of eligible properties having PFR measures in place. VER

🌊 PFR Measure Categories

Resistance measures — prevent water entry:

  • Flood doors and barriers: Purpose-built flood doors (to CIRIA/BSI standards) rated to withstand a defined hydrostatic head of water; temporary barriers across doorways (demountable or deployable). Can resist water up to ~0.6–0.9m depth if properly installed and sealed. VER
  • Air brick covers: Snap-on or permanent covers over ventilation bricks — a primary entry route for groundwater and shallow surface flooding. Inexpensive (~£20–£80 per brick) and highly effective for low-depth events.
  • Non-return valves (sewage backflow): Prevent sewer water backflow through drains into the property — critical in urban flash flood events where drainage systems surcharge.
  • Sump and pump systems: Automatic pumping of groundwater/seepage before it reaches floor level; requires maintained power supply and regular testing.
  • Raised electrical sockets and consumer units: Moving electrical infrastructure above likely flood water level to prevent both immediate danger and post-flood re-occupation delay.

Resilience measures — limit damage when water enters:

  • Flood-resilient flooring: Ceramic tiles, concrete, or flood-resilient engineered wood rather than standard timber/carpet — can be cleaned and dried rather than replaced. Reduces reinstatement cost significantly.
  • Lime plaster and lime render: Breathable plaster systems that dry out post-flooding without the need for full wall strip-out; standard plasterboard requires complete removal and replacement after wetting.
  • Raised white goods and kitchen units: Dishwashers, washing machines, and kitchen cabinet carcasses raised off flood-risk floor level; stainless steel or composite rather than chipboard cabinets.
  • Ground floor wet room design: Full tile and drainage system enabling quick clean-down post-flooding.

Underwriting relevance: Flood Re's Quinquennial Review 2022 confirmed intent to offer PFR-positive pricing discounts from the mid-2020s. VER Lloyd's HP underwriters should document PFR measures in the risk file — properties with demonstrable resistance and resilience measures warrant materially lower flood loadings than equivalent un-improved Zone 2/3 risks.

3. Subsidence Management

Subsidence risk mitigation is primarily about managing clay-soil moisture levels and tree proximity. Once subsidence has occurred, the claims management process is complex and long-duration (typically 2–4 years). Underwriters should look for evidence that the risk owner has proactively addressed the known drivers. VER

🌳 Tree Management

  • Arborist survey identifying high-water-demand species within 5–10m radius
  • Root barrier installation between significant trees and foundations
  • Managed crown reduction or removal of highest-risk trees
  • Long-term monitoring plan — annual arborist inspection
Premium Benefit: −10% to −25% on subsidence loading where proactive tree management documented

💧 Drainage & Moisture Management

  • French drains / soakaways to prevent surface water pooling adjacent to foundations
  • Leaking drains survey and repair (drain camera survey) — leaking drains in clay soils concentrate moisture
  • Soil moisture monitoring sensors (available for residential use) for high-risk properties
  • Guttering and downpipe maintenance — overflow adjacent to foundations is a minor but cumulative risk factor
Premium Benefit: Drain survey certificate reduces adverse selection signal

🏗️ Foundation Investigation & Underpinning

  • Structural engineers report confirming foundation depth and condition
  • Underpinning (conventional or mini-piled) for properties with historic movement
  • Crack monitoring programme (tell-tales, crack gauges) — evidence of stable monitoring rather than ignored movement
  • Ground investigation borehole data for clay type and shrink-swell potential
Note: Underpinned property is complex — some syndicates decline; others accept with survey confirmation of completed works

🔍 Pre-Inception Survey

  • Independent building survey (RICS Level 3 / full structural survey) completed within 3 years
  • Specialist subsidence survey where prior movement disclosed
  • Thermal imaging survey for moisture ingress detection
  • Clear disclosure obligation: all known movement, prior claims, and works must be declared at inception
Survey within 3 years supports acceptance and accurate premium; undisclosed prior movement is a major coverage risk

4. Fire Risk Controls

🔥 Standard Fire Controls

  • Interlinked smoke detection on all floors (BS 5839-6 Grade D standard for residential) VER
  • Heat detector in kitchen (smoke detectors typically removed from kitchens due to false alarm rate)
  • Carbon monoxide detectors adjacent to all combustion appliances
  • Annual boiler and gas appliance service (Gas Safe registered engineer)
  • Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR) — recommended every 10 years for owner-occupied
Compliant smoke detection: standard requirement. Non-compliant: potential claims defensibility issue

🏚️ Thatched Property Fire Controls

  • Specialist spark arrestor on chimney — prevents chimney spark ignition of thatch (primary cause of thatch fires)
  • Fire retardant treatment of thatch — regular re-treatment every 5–10 years
  • Thermal imaging of chimney stack to detect cracks/heat penetration
  • Dedicated hose reel and external water supply (some insurers require)
  • Lightning conductor on thatched properties — standard requirement from specialist insurers
  • Minimum 1 hour fire separation between main house and thatched outbuildings
Spark arrestor is non-negotiable for most Lloyd's thatched property underwriters

🏛️ Listed Building Fire Controls

  • Grade I/II listed buildings: often limited in upgrades permitted without conservation consent
  • Automatic fire suppression (sprinkler system) — increasingly required by specialist insurers for large listed houses, though conservation consent required
  • Fire risk assessment by specialist consultant for Grade I properties
  • Separation of historic structural elements from modern heating systems
  • Heritage timber (old growth oak beams) — specific fire spread risk; documented in survey
Sprinkler system in a large listed property is a material premium reduction but requires specialist installation and conservation officer approval

7. Net Zero Retrofit — Emerging Risk Controls

⚡ Net Zero Retrofit: Emerging Loss Categories & Controls

The UK government's net zero agenda is creating new risk categories that are insufficiently priced or addressed in most standard household policies. Three primary emerging risks for Lloyd's HP underwriters: EST

  • Heat Pump Fires: Air source and ground source heat pumps contain refrigerants and compressors. Early loss experience from specialist insurers suggests compressor fires and refrigerant leaks are emerging as a small but growing sub-peril. Controls: correct installation to MCS certification standard, annual service by F-Gas registered engineer, ventilated installation space.
  • Solar Panel Damage & Fire: Storm and hail damage to PV panels is increasingly frequent as install base grows. More significantly, DC arc faults in PV wiring (a specific electrical fire risk not well-addressed by standard fire detection) are an emerging category. Controls: arc fault detection devices (AFDDs), annual panel and inverter inspection, lightning protection where panels are on high-risk structures.
  • EV Charging Point Liability & Fire: Domestic EV charging (primarily overnight slow-charging via 7kW home wallbox) creates fire risk from electrical fault. Major risk: battery thermal runaway in adjacent garage. Controls: OLEV/OZEV-compliant charger installation (required for grant funding), smart charging (reduced load-shedding risk), garage fire detection and suppression, periodic EICR review of charging circuit.
  • External Wall Insulation (EWI) & Cladding: Post-Grenfell awareness of combustible cladding risk extends to residential EWI retrofits. Some EWI products use expanded polystyrene (EPS) which has fire-spread characteristics if incorrectly specified or installed. Controls: EWI specification to BR 135 fire performance requirements, professional installation with BBA/BBA certificate, building control sign-off.

Lloyd's underwriting action: The HP renewal questionnaire should include a specific section on retrofit installations (heat pump Y/N, solar PV Y/N, EV charger Y/N, EWI Y/N) to enable appropriate rating rather than relying on the existing scope of standard policy cover. Most standard policies were written before these risks were material.

8. Top 10 Risk Mitigation Actions for HP Insureds

  • 01Flood Zone 2/3: Register with EA Flood Warnings Direct. Install air brick covers. Commission a professional flood survey. Consider permanent flood door/barrier installation.
  • 02High shrink-swell zone: Commission an arborist survey for trees within 10m. Remove or crown-reduce high-water-demand trees. Install root barriers where removal isn't possible.
  • 03Thatched property: Install spark arrestor if not already fitted. Keep thatch clear of chimney spark trajectory. Maintain 6m separation from LPG tanks. Annual inspection by Master Thatcher.
  • 04Listed building: Commission RICS Level 3 structural survey and keep current (max 3 years old). Commission specialist rebuild cost valuation by RICS-qualified building surveyor — not BCIS automated estimate.
  • 05All properties: Ensure sum insured reflects current BCIS rebuild cost. With post-2021 inflation, a property not revalued since 2019–2020 is likely under-insured by 20–30%. Request an insurer rebuild cost review.
  • 06Security (high-value contents): Install NSI/SSAIB-certificated monitored alarm. Ensure jewellery and watches are stored in a cash-rated, anchor-bolted safe when not in use. Maintain individual valuations.
  • 07Escape of water (all properties): Fit lever-operated isolation valves on all water supply pipes. Know where the mains stopcock is. Consider automatic leak detection (Leakbot or equivalent) for premium reduction.
  • 08Unoccupied property: Notify insurer before property becomes unoccupied >30 days. Inspect and report to insurer. Drain down water system in winter. Arrange regular inspection visits.
  • 09Net zero retrofit: Ensure all retrofit installations (heat pump, solar, EV charger) are professionally installed to relevant certification standards. Notify insurer of material changes to property as required by policy conditions.
  • 10Fine art and jewellery: Maintain current professional valuations (max 3–5 years old). Photography and serial numbers on record. Keep a secure digital copy of all valuation certificates off-site or in cloud storage.
