CY Standalone Cyber — Coverage Structure

A standalone CY policy is divided into two broad buckets: first-party (the insured's own losses) and third-party (liability to others). The split matters for underwriting — first-party is dominated by ransomware and BI; third-party by privacy class actions and regulatory defence. Most large accounts have both; smaller SME packages (CG) may have combined sub-limits rather than distinct towers.

First-Party Coverages

Cyber Business InterruptionNet profit loss and continuing expenses during a network outage caused by a covered cyber event. Waiting period typically 8–12 hours (vs. 72 hrs in older forms). Often the single largest loss component.
System Damage & RestorationCosts to restore, repair or replace hardware, software and data corrupted or destroyed by a cyber attack. Includes specialist forensic IT vendors.
Ransomware / ExtortionRansom payment (where legally permissible), ransom negotiation specialist fees, decryption key costs. Many policies now require pre-approval before payment.
Breach Response CostsForensic investigation, legal advice, notification of affected individuals, credit/identity monitoring services, crisis PR management. Beazley BBR panel is market benchmark.
Cyber Crime / BECFunds transfer fraud, invoice redirection fraud, telephonic social engineering. Typically sublimited (10–25% of limit); separate crime policy advisable above sub.
Contingent BI (Dependent Systems)BI from outage at a named or unnamed third-party supplier (cloud provider, SaaS platform). Unnamed contingent BI often restricted post-CrowdStrike.

Third-Party Coverages

Network Security LiabilityThird-party claims for transmission of malware or virus, DDoS attacks launched from insured's network, or failure to prevent unauthorized access to third-party systems.
Privacy LiabilityClaims from individuals whose personal data was compromised. Includes GDPR data subject claims, US state privacy class actions, Canadian PIPEDA breaches.
Regulatory Defence & FinesLegal costs defending regulatory investigations (ICO, FTC, HHS/OCR, state AGs). Fines/penalties covered where insurable by applicable law — varies significantly by jurisdiction.
Media Liability (Cyber)Defamation, IP infringement in digital media content. Often included in cyber form for tech companies; excluded for traditional publishers who carry standalone media PI.
PCI-DSS Fines & AssessmentsCard brand penalties and forensic audit costs following a payment card data breach. Coverage for PCI fines is contested — many policies now exclude or sublimit.
Tech E&O (CZ only)Claims arising from failure of technology product or service to perform as intended. Third-party financial loss from software errors, IT service failures, cloud downtime.

Code Variant Structures: CY / CZ / CG / CH

CY

Standalone Cyber

  • Full first + third-party structure
  • Limit: £500k to £500m+
  • MFA mandatory condition for most accounts
  • LMA 5564/5565 exclusion must attach
  • Separate sublimits for BEC, contingent BI
  • Suitable: mid-market to large enterprise across all sectors
CZ

Technology PI / E&O + Cyber

  • Tech E&O tower: errors/failures causing client loss
  • Cyber coverage blended into same policy
  • IP infringement, professional negligence included
  • Limit: £1m to £50m typical
  • Retroactive date critical — "claims made" basis
  • Suitable: SaaS, MSPs, IT consultants, software firms
CG

Commercial Lines Cyber

  • Packaged or standalone for SMEs
  • Combined sublimits rather than separate towers
  • Limit: £25k to £2m typical
  • Simplified application (15–25 questions)
  • Often sold as endorsement to property/liability
  • Suitable: small businesses, sole traders, micro-firms
CH

Healthcare / Critical Infrastructure

  • Clinical disruption BI (patient diversion costs)
  • OT/ICS/SCADA attack vectors included
  • HIPAA/HITECH regulatory section
  • Medical device compromise coverage
  • Limit: £10m to £500m+
  • Suitable: NHS trusts, hospital groups, CNI operators

Rating Factors & Underwriting Drivers

🔐

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Presence of MFA on email, remote access (VPN/RDP) and privileged accounts. Post-Colonial Pipeline (2021), MFA is a binary condition for most underwriters. No MFA = no cover or significant premium loading.

High Impact
🛡️

Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR/XDR)

Active EDR/XDR deployment across ≥90% of endpoints. EDR is now considered table stakes; absence is a major risk indicator. XDR integration (network + cloud telemetry) commands a rate credit.

High Impact
💾

Backup Architecture

3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, 2 different media, 1 offsite. Immutable or air-gapped backups significantly reduce ransomware BI exposure. Backups tested quarterly receive credit; untested backups are discounted.

High Impact
🔧

Patch Management

Time-to-patch for critical vulnerabilities; presence of unpatched internet-facing systems. CVSS 9.0+ patches should be applied within 72 hours. Outstanding critical patches = hard loading or exclusion.

High Impact
🏭

Industry Sector

Healthcare, financial services and critical infrastructure attract highest base rates. Ransomware groups specifically target sectors with low downtime tolerance and high willingness-to-pay.

High Impact
📊

Revenue / Limit Ratio

Cyber limit as % of revenue drives adequacy assessment. A £500m revenue company with a £1m limit is over-exposed from an insurer perspective; insurers prefer limits ≥1% of annual revenue for larger risks.

Medium Impact
🗄️

Data Volume & Sensitivity

PII records count, presence of payment card data, health records, financial data. Higher sensitivity data = greater breach response cost, regulatory exposure and class action risk.

Medium Impact
🌐

Internet-Facing Attack Surface

Number of internet-exposed ports/services, RDP exposure, legacy VPN use, open SMTP relays. External attack surface scanning tools (Bitsight, SecurityScorecard) used as proxy for this.

Medium Impact
📋

Incident Response Planning

Presence of a tested IR plan, named IR retainer, tabletop exercise within 12 months. Accounts with a pre-approved IR firm on retainer typically claim faster and with lower ultimate loss.

Medium Impact
🔒

Privileged Access Management

PAM solutions controlling admin account usage, just-in-time privilege, session recording. Absence of PAM in large enterprises is a material risk factor for lateral movement post-initial compromise.

Medium Impact
📡

Network Segmentation

Degree of network segmentation limiting lateral movement. Flat networks dramatically increase ransomware blast radius — assessed via architecture diagrams or network questionnaire.

Medium Impact
📜

Prior Losses / Claims History

5-year cyber claims history including near-misses. Repeated ransomware claims indicate systemic security gaps. One prior claim with remediation evidence treated differently to recurring losses.

Lower Impact

Rate-on-Limit (ROL) Methodology

Cyber is rated on a rate-on-line (ROL) basis — the premium expressed as a percentage of the policy limit — unlike property (which uses rate per £100 TIV) or liability (rate per £1m revenue). This reflects the fact that a cyber BI loss can exhaust the full policy limit regardless of asset values. ROL is then adjusted by sector, security score, limit adequacy and market conditions.

Cyber Premium Calculation

Base ROL = f( Sector Risk Class, Revenue Band, Limit Size )
Adjusted ROL = Base ROL × Security Score Modifier × Limit Adequacy Factor × Market Cycle Factor
Annual Premium = Policy Limit × Adjusted ROL
Example: £10m limit × 1.80% ROL = £180,000 annual premium

The Security Score Modifier ranges from approximately 0.65× (excellent controls, all Green) to 2.5× (poor controls, multiple Red flags). A typical mid-market risk with mixed controls sits at 1.0–1.2×. The Limit Adequacy Factor penalises accounts where the limit is very low relative to revenue, or rewards accounts that purchase meaningful limits relative to their cyber BI exposure.

⚠️ Underwriting Alert: Aggregation & Contingent BI Post-CrowdStrike (July 2024)

The CrowdStrike Falcon sensor update outage of July 2024 triggered contingent BI claims across thousands of policies simultaneously — airlines, hospitals, banks and logistics all claimed under unnamed dependent systems coverage from the same root cause event.

Following CrowdStrike, many Lloyd's syndicates have materially tightened unnamed contingent BI: sub-limits reduced from 20–25% of policy limit to 5–10%, or excluded entirely with named-only cover available by endorsement. Underwriters now actively assess the insured's exposure to systemic third-party dependencies (AWS, Azure, Microsoft 365, Salesforce, CrowdStrike itself) as part of the application process.

The "Single Risk Minimum Security Standards" — Lloyd's Market Requirements

Since 2022, Lloyd's has required managing agents to implement minimum security control standards before binding CY coverage above certain thresholds. The practical effect is that market-standard application forms now include binding warranties on MFA, EDR and backup quality for accounts above £/$/€1m in limit.

A warranty is not merely a rating factor — it is a condition precedent to cover. If MFA was warranted as "deployed" but was in fact absent at the time of a ransomware attack, the insurer may have grounds to decline the claim entirely. Brokers and insureds must treat underwriting questionnaire responses as legal representations, not aspirational statements.

The Beazley Breach Response (BBR) panel approach represents the gold standard in claims management: pre-approved forensic firms, legal counsel, and notification vendors are engaged immediately on a claim, keeping response costs efficient and reducing notification timeline liability. Policyholders who deviate from panel vendors typically face higher costs that may not be fully reimbursed.
