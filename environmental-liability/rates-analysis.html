EIL Market Rate Index (1986–2026)

Index Base: 1986 = 100 (creation of APE, post absolute pollution exclusion). Chart shows cyclical hardening and softening driven by major events (CERCLA enforcement peaks, Deepwater Horizon, PFAS settlements) and capacity dynamics.

Market Cycle Phases (40 Years)

📍 1986–1995: Formative Era

Post absolute pollution exclusion; first standalone EIL policies emerge. Hard market, limited competition, rates peak at 140 in 1990 during CERCLA enforcement peak. EST

📍 1995–2007: Expansion & Softening

New entrants, standardised products, brownfield boom drives demand. Competitive pricing softens rates to 100 baseline. Market capacity surplus enables growth. EST

📍 2007–2011: Moderate Hardening

Deepwater Horizon awareness, energy sector scrutiny, capacity discipline. Rates harden modestly. Selective withdrawal from petroleum exposure. EST

📍 2012–2018: Soft Cycle

Strong competition, new entrants (Insure & Go, others), capacity surplus, rate pressure 85–95. Arbitrage opportunities for brokers. EST

📍 2019–2022: PFAS-Driven Turn

Systemic PFAS exposure recognised; carriers add exclusions, reunderwrite portfolios. Selective capacity withdrawal. Rate hardening to 118 by 2022. EST

📍 2022–Present: Hard Selective Market

PFAS losses crystallising; climate-driven contamination emerging. Capacity constrained for heavy industrial. Retail/light commercial remain competitive. Index 132 (2026). EST

ROE Scenarios on $500m Global Written Premium

The following three scenarios illustrate return on equity (ROE) outcomes under different combined ratio assumptions, holding capital at 60% of GWP ($300m). ILLUS

Bull Scenario: CR 92% (Disciplined Underwriting)

Premium Income: $500m

Incurred Losses (92% × $500m): $460m

Operating Expenses (27% × $500m): $135m

Investment Income (2.5% × $300m capital): $7.5m

Net Underwriting Profit: $500m – $460m – $135m = -$95m + $7.5m = -$87.5m

Wait — recalculating: Profit from underwriting = Premiums – Losses – Expenses = $500 – $460 – $135 = -$95m (combined ratio includes expenses). Let me correct:

Underwriting Profit (Pre-investment): $500m – ($460m + $135m) = -$95m

Investment Income (2.5% × $300m): $7.5m

Correction: CR 92% means combined claims & expense = 92%, so profit = $500m × (1 – 0.92) = $40m

Total Profit (underwriting + investment): $40m + $7.5m = $47.5m

ROE (Profit / Capital): $47.5m / $300m = ~15.8% ROE ILLUS

Base Scenario: CR 105% (Current Market Reality)

Premium Income: $500m

Combined Ratio: 105%

Underwriting Loss (5% of premium): -$25m

Investment Income (2.5% × $300m): $7.5m

Net Profit: -$25m + $7.5m = -$17.5m

ROE: -$17.5m / $300m = ~-5.8% ROE ILLUS

Bear Scenario: CR 125% (PFAS Mass Tort Year)

Premium Income: $500m

Combined Ratio: 125%

Underwriting Loss (25% of premium): -$125m

Investment Income (2.5% × $300m): $7.5m

Net Profit: -$125m + $7.5m = -$117.5m

ROE: -$117.5m / $300m = ~-39.2% ROE ILLUS

Key Insight: EIL combined ratios ≥ 100% are not unusual given PFAS tail exposure. Carriers justify participation through portfolio diversification and reliance on investment income in soft-market years. ROE volatility is high. EST
