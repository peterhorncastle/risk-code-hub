Lloyd's Code EP — Environmental Impairment Liability

Environmental Impairment Liability (EIL) — also referred to as Pollution Legal Liability (PLL) in the US market — provides coverage for third-party bodily injury and property damage, on-site and third-party cleanup costs, regulatory defence, and natural resource damages arising from pollution conditions. It is a specialist class, distinct from standard General Liability which typically includes an absolute pollution exclusion covering most contamination scenarios. VER

Principal Coverage Types

🏭 Pollution Legal Liability (PLL)

The core EIL product. Covers site owners, operators and lessees for pollution conditions at a scheduled site. Includes on-site cleanup, third-party BI/PD, off-site migration claims and regulatory defence.

🔧 Contractors Pollution Liability (CPL)

Covers contractors and subcontractors for pollution incidents arising from their operations. Especially relevant for environmental remediation contractors, general contractors disturbing contaminated soil, and utilities contractors.

🌊 Natural Resource Damages (NRD)

Covers liability to government trustees for injury to natural resources — water bodies, wildlife, wetlands, groundwater. NRD is separate from cleanup costs and can be very large (e.g. Deepwater Horizon NRD = $8.8bn). VER

☠️ Remediation Cost Cap (RCC)

Covers cost overruns on pre-agreed environmental remediation projects. Insured retains a deductible/retention; policy pays costs exceeding the estimated cleanup figure up to a cap. Used in brownfield transactions and voluntary cleanups.

🔬 Environmental Professional Liability

E&O cover for environmental consultants, engineers and remediation contractors for professional negligence in conducting Phase I/II ESAs, remediation design or regulatory compliance advice.

🚛 Transportation Pollution

Covers spills and releases during transportation of hazardous materials — chemicals, waste, petroleum products. Extension to standard PLL or standalone product. Particularly relevant for hazmat haulers and waste contractors.

Key Regulatory Frameworks

🇺🇸 CERCLA / Superfund (1980)

  • Comprehensive Environmental Response, Compensation and Liability Act VER
  • Strict, joint and several, retroactive liability VER
  • EPA can compel cleanup by Potentially Responsible Parties (PRPs)
  • ~1,300 sites on National Priorities List (NPL) EST
  • Average NPL site cleanup cost: $140m+ EST

🇪🇺 EU Environmental Liability Directive (2004/35/CE)

  • Polluter-pays principle; prevention and remediation of environmental damage VER
  • Covers: water damage, land damage, biodiversity damage VER
  • No third-party civil liability — purely regulatory/administrative VER
  • Implemented in UK via Environmental Damage Regulations 2009 VER
  • Operators of certain "dangerous activities" face strict liability VER

🇺🇸 RCRA — Resource Conservation and Recovery Act

  • Governs generation, transportation and disposal of hazardous waste VER
  • Corrective Action: EPA can require cleanup of historical releases at permitted facilities
  • Subtitle C: hazardous waste; Subtitle D: solid (municipal) waste; Subtitle I: underground storage tanks (USTs)
  • USTs: ~550,000 regulated USTs in USA EST; leaking USTs a major EIL exposure

🛢️ Oil Pollution Act 1990 (OPA)

  • Enacted in response to Exxon Valdez spill VER
  • Strict liability for oil spills into US navigable waters
  • Responsible Party (RP) liable for cleanup and natural resource damages
  • Financial responsibility requirements (COFR) for vessels and facilities

⚠️ PFAS — The Defining Emerging Liability

Per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS) — "forever chemicals" — are a group of ~12,000 synthetic compounds used in firefighting foam (AFFF), non-stick coatings, food packaging and industrial processes. VER They do not biodegrade and accumulate in groundwater, soil and human tissue. Landmark settlements:

  • 3M settled PFAS water supply claims for ~$10.3bn (June 2023) VER
  • DuPont/Chemours/Corteva settled for ~$1.185bn (June 2023) VER
  • AFFF (aqueous film-forming foam) litigation: military bases, airports — thousands of sites
  • EPA MCL for PFOA/PFOS set at 4 parts per trillion (April 2024) VER
  • Industry-wide PFAS liability estimated at $400bn+ over 20 years EST

Market Overview

Environmental liability insurance is a specialist market dominated by a small number of experienced carriers. The US market is the largest, driven by CERCLA liability and brownfield redevelopment activity. The European market is growing following ELD implementation. EST
