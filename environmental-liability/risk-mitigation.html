Phase I ESA Controls

A robust Phase I ESA — compliant with ASTM E1527-21 — is the foundation of risk assessment. Underwriting quality depends on thoroughness of historical research, regulatory database searches and site inspection.

Key Phase I Control Elements

  • ASTM E1527-21 compliant: Follows 2021 standard, includes all required documentation
  • Qualified Environmental Professional (EP): Licensed engineer or environmental consultant with EIL underwriting experience
  • Historical research 100+ years: Sanborn maps, aerial photography, property deeds, prior industrial uses
  • Regulatory database search: EPA CERCLIS, state environmental agency files, RCRA SQG/LQG registries, UST listings, vapor intrusion databases
  • Site interviews: Current operators, environmental staff, maintenance personnel regarding spills, storage, past operations
  • Aerial photograph review: Multi-date series showing site evolution, nearby facilities, vegetation patterns indicating possible impacts
  • REC identification: Recognised Environmental Conditions documented with risk assessment of contamination likelihood

Phase II ESA Controls

Phase II is triggered whenever a REC is identified in Phase I, or planned redevelopment/excavation creates disturbance risk. Proper sampling and analysis design directly mitigates later loss surprises.

🔬 Sampling Design

Systematic soil boring locations targeting areas of concern. Monitoring well installation for groundwater assessment. Vapor intrusion sampling if building planned. Chain of custody maintained.

🧪 Laboratory Analysis

Standard analyte suites: VOCs (chlorinated solvents, BTEX), SVOCs (PAHs, pesticides), metals (lead, chromium, arsenic). PFAS analysis if suspected (AFFF use, food processing, industrial discharge).

📊 Risk Assessment

Comparison to EPA residential/commercial/industrial standards (RSLs). Exposure pathway analysis: direct contact, ingestion, inhalation. Remedial Action Levels (RALs) determined if cleanup required.

Environmental Management System (ISO 14001:2015)

ISO 14001 certification demonstrates systematic commitment to environmental compliance and risk management. Reduces EIL rating factors by 10–20%.

📋 ISO 14001 Certification

Third-party audit against ISO 14001:2015 standard. Environmental aspects and impacts register. Legal compliance documentation. Objectives & targets setting. Regular internal audits. Annual management review.

⚖️ Legal Compliance Register

Maintained register of all applicable environmental laws & regulations (federal, state, local). Updates tracked as regulations change. Audit programme to verify ongoing compliance (e.g. annual facility inspections).

🚨 Emergency Response Plan

Documented procedures for spill response, hazmat release, notification protocols. Trained emergency response team. Regular drills. Contact procedures for regulators, spill response contractors.

💧 Spill Prevention & Response Kit

Strategic placement of spill kits at high-risk areas (storage tanks, loading docks). Absorbent materials, containment booms, disposal procedures documented. Quarterly inventory checks.

Spill Prevention Controls (SPCC)

US EPA Spill Prevention, Control and Countermeasure (SPCC) Rule — 40 CFR Part 112

Applicability: Required for any facility with above-ground oil storage capacity ≥ 1,320 gallons (e.g. heating oil, lube oil, process oils). VER

  • Written SPCC Plan: Prepared & certified by Qualified Spill Professional. Includes facility schematic, storage locations, containment design.
  • Secondary Containment: 110% of the largest single tank capacity. Impermeable dike, vault or berm. Regularly inspected for cracks/degradation.
  • Regular Inspections: Weekly visual inspections for leaks, drips. Annual tank integrity certification. Records maintained 3+ years.
  • Spill Kit Readiness: Absorbent materials, booms, sorbent pads staged near high-risk areas. Staff training annual.
  • Regulatory Reporting: Any discharge > reportable quantity (RQ) to waterway triggers 24-hour federal notification. State agency notification within 5 days.

Underground Storage Tanks (USTs)

EPA Subtitle I (40 CFR Part 280) — UST Compliance

Coverage: ~550,000 regulated USTs in USA, predominantly at gas stations & fuel distributors. Strict compliance reduces EIL exposure. EST

  • Tank Integrity: Double-wall tanks (fiberglass or steel with secondary wall). Cathodic protection systems for steel tanks to prevent corrosion.
  • Overfill Prevention: Automated shut-off valves or ball-float systems on tank openings.
  • Leak Detection: Monthly inventory reconciliation. Annual tank tightness testing if no secondary containment. Monthly groundwater monitoring wells if installed.
  • 30-Day Regulatory Reporting: Any suspected leak must be reported to state within 30 days. Large spills (≥25 gallons) require immediate notification.
  • Financial Assurance: Evidence of ability to pay cleanup costs ($1–10m) via insurance, surety bond, or state fund contribution. VER
  • Closure Requirements: When UST decommissioned, tank & surrounding soil must be sampled. If contaminated, becomes remedial site.

PFAS Control Strategies

PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances) exclusions in EIL policies are now standard. Proactive PFAS reduction demonstrates risk mitigation and may preserve some coverage or improve terms.

🧯 AFFF Replacement Programmes

Aqueous Film-Forming Foam (AFFF) used at airports, military bases, firefighter training grounds contains PFOA/PFOS. Replacement with fluorine-free foam alternatives eliminates new PFAS input. Urgent priority for facilities with legacy AFFF use.

💨 Discharge Monitoring & Control

Enhanced wastewater sampling for PFAS. Treatment upgrade (GAC, ion exchange) for high-use industrial discharges. Documentation of PFAS levels & reduction trends demonstrates due diligence.

🏭 Product Reformulation

Food packaging manufacturers, non-stick coating producers. Transition to PFAS-free alternatives (ceramic coatings, wax blends). Supply chain PFAS mapping to identify upstream sources.

♻️ Supply Chain PFAS Mapping & Take-Back

Identify suppliers of PFAS-containing materials. Vendor agreements mandating PFAS-free alternatives. Product take-back schemes for old non-stick cookware, textiles containing PFAS.

⚠️ PFAS Exclusions in EIL Market (2024–2026)

Most Lloyd's and admitted carriers now exclude PFAS contamination entirely, or sublimit to $1–5m per occurrence with significant rate loading (5–15%+). Proactive PFAS reduction may help preserve some third-party liability coverage if exposure is remote (e.g., manufacturing facility with minimal AFFF use). VER

Contaminated Site Controls

🏛️ Institutional Controls (ICs)

Deed restrictions limiting land use (e.g., "industrial/commercial only — no residential development"). Groundwater Use Restrictions (GURs) prohibiting potable groundwater extraction. Recorded on property deed; binds all future owners.

🛡️ Engineering Controls

Site caps (asphalt, concrete over contaminated soil). Vertical/horizontal barriers limiting contaminant migration. Pump-and-treat systems actively removing contamination from groundwater. Vapor intrusion mitigation (sub-slab depressurization).

🌱 Monitored Natural Attenuation (MNA)

Passive remedy allowing natural degradation of contaminants (some VOCs, PAHs biodegrade over time). Quarterly/annual groundwater monitoring to verify contaminant decline. Regulatory approval required; typically 10–30 year timeframe.

✅ Remediation Performance Benchmarks

Quantified cleanup targets (e.g., "soil PCE < 10 mg/kg within 2 years"). Regular progress monitoring. Milestone achievements documented for regulatory closure & insurance reporting.

Top 10 Operator Risk Actions

Conduct Phase I ESA (ASTM E1527-21) — If no Phase I completed in past 5 years, commission one immediately. Critical for insurance placement and underwriting terms.
Phase II Investigation if REC Identified — Do not delay. Known contamination discovered post-claim is a major coverage denial trigger.
Achieve ISO 14001:2015 Certification — Third-party audit typically costs $10–30k. Reduces EIL premiums 10–20% through demonstrated compliance framework.
SPCC Plan Preparation & Certification — Mandatory for facilities with ≥1,320 gallons above-ground oil. Annual review & update. Budget $5–15k for professional preparation.
Replace Legacy AFFF with Fluorine-Free Alternatives — Urgent for airports, fire training grounds, military. Eliminate PFAS input and reduce policy exclusion risk. Coordinate with fire safety protocols.
UST Compliance Audit — If facility has USTs, verify double-wall construction, cathodic protection active, leak detection operational. Non-compliance can void EIL coverage.
Implement Groundwater Monitoring Programme — Quarterly/annual sampling downgradient of potential contamination sources. Early warning system for emerging issues.
Document Spill Response Procedures & Train Staff — Written procedures, spill kit locations, emergency contact list. Annual training for ops staff. Demonstrate readiness to insurers.
Maintain Regulatory Compliance Register — Track all applicable environmental permits, RCRA waste classification, PCB inventory, asbestos/lead surveys. Annual updates.
Review & Update Insurance Programme Annually — EIL market is fast-moving (PFAS exclusions, capacity constraints). Annual market check with broker ensures adequate coverage & competitive rates.
