Coverage Trigger Framework

Environmental Liability insurance is written on a claims-made basis, meaning coverage is triggered when a claim is made and reported to the insurer during the policy period or within the discovery period following policy expiration. VER

🎯 Claims-Made Trigger

Coverage applies only if the claim is first made against the insured during the policy period or reported within a discovery period (typically 3–5 years). Not dependent on when the loss occurred.

📋 Pollution Condition Definition

Presence of pollutants (CERCLA hazardous substances, petroleum, PFAS, etc.) in soil, groundwater, surface water or air. Includes past, present or threatened releases.

🔄 Discovery vs Occurrence

Unlike occurrence policies, EIL covers claims made during the policy period even if the pollution occurred years earlier. "Discovery" of the condition is key — not the date of release.

⏳ Retroactive Date Importance

The policy retroactive date (typically back 15–30+ years for renewal business) establishes the earliest pollution event eligible for coverage. Excludes pre-retroactive-date conditions.

Phase I, II and III ESA Framework

Environmental Site Assessments (ESAs) follow a phased approach, with Phase I and Phase II data heavily influencing underwriting and rating decisions.

Phase I ESA — Records & Records Search

ASTM E1527-21 Standard (2021 Update) VER

Scope: Non-intrusive investigation combining records search, site inspection and interviews.

  • Historical records: Prior uses (Sanborn maps, aerial photos, property deeds) back 100+ years
  • Regulatory database search: EPA CERCLIS, state environmental files, RCRA facilities, UST registries
  • Site inspection: Visual inspection for staining, storage tanks, potential contamination indicators
  • Interviews: Current/former operators, environmental personnel, tenants regarding operations and spills
  • No sampling: Phase I is entirely desktop and walkover — no soil or water sampling
  • REC identification: Recognises Environmental Conditions (RECs) — past/present activities suggesting contamination risk

Phase II ESA — Intrusive Investigation

When Phase II is Triggered

Any REC identified in Phase I, combined with planned disturbance or redevelopment, typically warrants Phase II investigation.

  • Sampling design: Soil boring, monitoring wells, vapor intrusion sampling, surface water sampling
  • Laboratory analysis: Volatile organic compounds (VOCs), semi-volatile organics (SVOCs), metals, PFAS if suspected
  • Risk assessment: Quantifies contamination levels vs regulatory standards; assesses exposure pathways
  • Remedial action levels: Determines if cleanup is required and what method

Phase III ESA — Remediation Design and Execution

Remedial Engineering & Implementation

Conditional on Phase II findings. Designs and implements cleanup (excavation, soil vapor extraction, pump-and-treat, capping, etc.). Typically outside the direct scope of underwriting but critical for Claims History factor. EST

PLL Rating Formula

Annual Premium = Site Value × Base Rate × Industry Loading × Site History Factor × Coverage Scope Factor × Claims History Factor

Each factor ranges from ~0.8 (favorable) to ~2.0+ (adverse). Terrorism loading and regulatory status adjustments applied separately.

Key Rating Factors (6 Cards)

🏢 SIC/Industry Code

Very High Risk: Petroleum refineries, chemical manufacturing, metal smelters, electroplating. High Risk: Dry cleaning, auto repair, printing, gas stations. Moderate: Food processing, manufacturing. Low Risk: Retail, offices, schools.

📖 Site History

Clean Phase II result (1.0×) vs Phase I only (1.2×) vs known contamination with active remediation (1.4–1.8×) vs legacy industrial with unknown conditions (2.0+×).

🌊 Proximity to Sensitive Receptors

Drinking water wells, schools, rivers, wetlands nearby increase risk. >500m favorable (1.0×); 100–500m moderate (1.2×); <100m from well/school adverse (1.6×).

⚖️ Regulatory Status

No regulatory action (1.0×) vs open state file (1.1×) vs proposed for NPL listing (1.3×) vs current NPL site (1.5–2.0×).

📏 Coverage Scope

Third-party only (1.0×) vs full on-site cleanup included (1.1–1.3×) vs NRD extension (1.2–1.5×). Broader scope = higher exposure.

📋 Prior Claims & Known Conditions

Zero claims/no known conditions (1.0×) vs disclosed conditions with resolution (1.1×) vs active remediation in place (1.3×) vs unresolved open claims (1.8–2.5×).

Worked Examples

Example A: Urban Brownfield PLL ILLUS

Scenario: Former Dry Cleaner Site, Urban Redevelopment

Site Profile: 1.2 acres, downtown urban location, former dry cleaning operation (1985–2010). PCE (perchloroethylene) soil and groundwater contamination known from prior Phase II. Open state remedial action file. Proposed retail redevelopment.

Inputs:

• Site Value: $8,000,000 (estimated retail development value)

• Base Rate: 1.8% (PLL baseline)

• Industry Loading: 1.3× (dry cleaner — Class 2 High Risk)

• Site History Factor: 1.4× (known contamination requiring remediation)

• Regulatory Status Factor: 1.1× (open state file, not NPL)

• Coverage Scope Factor: 1.0× (third-party only, on-site cleanup via separate RCC policy)

• Claims History Factor: 1.0× (no prior EIL claims disclosed)

Calculation: $8m × 1.8% × 1.3 × 1.4 × 1.1 × 1.0 × 1.0 = $28,512 + terrorism loading (~3–5%) = ~$29,400–$29,900

Additional: Separate Remediation Cost Cap policy on estimated $2.5m cleanup = ~$100k (4% of cap).

Example B: CPL for Environmental Contractor ILLUS

Scenario: Soil Remediation Contractor

Insured Profile: Speciality contractor performing excavation and off-site disposal of contaminated soil. Annual revenue $15 million, 50 employees, operations across 5 states. Five-year claims history clean.

Inputs:

• Basis: Annual revenue

• Rate: 1.2% of revenue (CPL standard)

• Revenue: $15,000,000

• Adjustment Factors: 1.0× (no adverse claims history, ISO 14001 in place, SPCC plan compliant)

Calculation: $15m × 1.2% × 1.0 = $180,000

Structure: $500k SIR, $15m limit per occurrence. Single limit applies to third-party BI/PD and regulatory costs.

Key Exclusions in EIL Policies

ExclusionRationale
Pollution Conditions Known at Inception Insurer cannot cover existing, disclosed contamination. Separate RCC policy or Phase II warranty required.
Asbestos & Lead Paint Generally excluded unless explicitly included within coverage scope. Specialized asbestos liability market exists. EST
PFAS (Per- and Polyfluoroalkyl Substances) Increasingly excluded or heavily sublimited in market. PFAS exclusions now standard as of 2024. VER
War, Terrorism, Civil Unrest Excluded under standard wordings. Terrorism loading available for additional premium.
Workers Compensation & Employer's Liability These are separate classes; not covered under EIL policies.
Contractual Liability (absent insured peril) Assumed liabilities under contracts excluded unless arising from EIL-covered pollution.
Gradual Property Damage to Insured's Own Property EIL covers third-party and regulatory liability, not insured's own property depreciation from contamination. EST
