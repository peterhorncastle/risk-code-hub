1976

RCRA Enacted — Cradle-to-Grave Hazardous Waste Regulation

Resource Conservation and Recovery Act; first federal framework regulating hazardous waste generation, transport, treatment and disposal. Created permitting regime for treatment, storage and disposal facilities (TSDFs). VER

1978

Love Canal Federal Emergency — Hooker Chemical, Niagara Falls NY

Hooker Chemical had buried 21,000 tons of chemical waste beneath a residential neighbourhood. President Carter declared a federal emergency; 800 families relocated. The defining event that drove Congress to enact CERCLA. VER

~$270m+ cleanup costs
1980

CERCLA Enacted — The Superfund Revolution

Comprehensive Environmental Response, Compensation and Liability Act signed 11 December 1980. Created strict, joint-and-several, retroactive liability for contaminated site cleanup. Established the Hazardous Substance Superfund. VER

1982

Times Beach, Missouri — Dioxin Contamination

Dioxin-contaminated waste oil sprayed on roads to control dust. EPA purchased the entire town of 2,200 residents for $36.7m — the only US town fully bought out by the federal government for environmental reasons. VER

~$200m+ total remediation
1984

Bhopal Disaster — Union Carbide India

Methyl isocyanate gas release from Union Carbide India pesticide plant, Bhopal. Approximately 3,787 official deaths; estimates of total casualties reach 16,000+. Largest industrial accident in history. $470m settlement agreed 1989. VER

$470m settlement / 3,787+ deaths
1986

ISO Absolute Pollution Exclusion & SARA — Creating the EIL Market

ISO introduced the absolute pollution exclusion into the standard CGL form, removing pollution coverage from general liability. Simultaneously SARA (Superfund Amendments) strengthened CERCLA. Together these created the structural gap filled by standalone EIL policies. VER

1986

Sandoz Rhine Chemical Spill — Basel, Switzerland

Fire at Sandoz warehouse; 30 tonnes of pesticides and herbicides washed into the Rhine. Fish died for 400 km; drinking water intake in cities downstream shut. Directly triggered European environmental legislative reform. VER

~$100m+ costs / 400km fishery loss
1989

Exxon Valdez Oil Spill — Prince William Sound, Alaska

Tanker grounded on Bligh Reef; 11 million gallons of crude oil spilled — at the time the largest US oil spill. Exxon ultimately paid $3.4bn cleanup costs and $507m punitive damages (confirmed US Supreme Court 2008). VER

~$3.4bn cleanup + $507m punitive
1990

Oil Pollution Act 1990 — Post-Exxon Valdez Reform

OPA 90 enacted in direct response to Exxon Valdez; strict liability for oil spills to US navigable waters; financial responsibility (COFR) requirements for vessels and facilities; Oil Spill Liability Trust Fund expanded. VER

1996

PG&E Hinkley Settlement — Chromium-6 Groundwater, California

Pacific Gas & Electric paid $333m to settle claims from 333 Hinkley, CA residents for Chromium-6 contamination of groundwater. Made famous by Erin Brockovich. Largest direct action settlement at time. VER

$333m settlement
2004

EU Environmental Liability Directive 2004/35/CE

Adopted 21 April 2004. Polluter-pays principle; operators of dangerous activities strictly liable for environmental damage (water, land, biodiversity). Transposition deadline: 30 April 2007. Implemented in UK by Environmental Damage Regulations 2009. VER

2010

Deepwater Horizon — Macondo Well Blowout, Gulf of Mexico

Explosion 20 April 2010 killed 11 workers. 4.9 million barrels released over 87 days — largest accidental marine oil spill in history. BP total costs exceeded $65bn including $8.8bn NRD and $5.5bn Clean Water Act penalties. VER

$65bn+ total BP costs (EST)
2010

Enbridge Line 6B Michigan Oil Spill — Kalamazoo River

Largest inland oil spill in US Midwest history — 843,000 gallons of diluted bitumen into Talmadge Creek and Kalamazoo River. Enbridge cleanup costs exceeded $1.2bn; settlement $177m (2016). VER

$1.2bn cleanup + $177m settlement
2014

Flint, Michigan — Lead Water Crisis

Switch to Flint River as water source without corrosion control caused lead leaching from pipes. 6,000–12,000 children exposed to elevated lead levels. State of Michigan settled for $600m (2021). VER

$600m settlement (2021)
2015

Volkswagen Emissions Scandal — NOx Defeat Device

VW installed defeat device software to cheat emissions tests across ~11 million diesel vehicles globally. Total costs exceeded $33bn including US EPA settlement $14.7bn (2016), European fines, and civil litigation. Created "environmental fraud" as a liability theory. VER

$33bn+ total costs
2015

Aliso Canyon Gas Leak — Porter Ranch, California

Largest methane release in US history — 109,000 tonnes CH₄ released over 4 months from SoCal Gas underground storage facility. 11,000 families temporarily relocated. SoCal Gas total costs ~$1.6bn+. VER

~$1.6bn total SoCal Gas costs (EST)
2017

GenX / Chemours PFAS Contamination — Cape Fear River, NC

Chemours (DuPont spinoff) found discharging GenX (next-generation PFAS) into Cape Fear River. Drinking water for ~250,000 residents contaminated. Triggered major regulatory action against PFAS compounds nationally. VER

2022

Camp Lejeune Justice Act — PFAS/TCE Contamination Claims

Enabled US veterans and families to sue for PFAS, trichloroethylene and other contamination at Marine Corps Base Camp Lejeune (1953–1987). Estimated 900,000+ potential claimants; liability estimated $6bn–$21bn. VER

$6–21bn estimated liability (EST)
2023

3M PFAS Settlement — $10.3bn

3M agreed to pay approximately $10.3bn over 13 years to resolve claims from US public water suppliers contaminated by PFAS (June 2023). Described as one of the largest environmental settlements in US history. VER

$10.3bn settlement
2023

DuPont / Chemours / Corteva PFAS Settlement

~$1.185bn settlement to resolve PFAS contamination claims from US public water systems. Note: leaves substantial private party, NRD and state/federal regulatory claims unresolved. VER

$1.185bn settlement
2023

East Palestine, Ohio Train Derailment — Norfolk Southern

Norfolk Southern freight train derailed 3 February 2023; vinyl chloride in 5 tank cars deliberately vented and burned; widespread soil and water contamination. Norfolk Southern agreed $600m+ settlement (2024). VER

$600m+ settlement
2024

EPA PFAS MCL — 4 Parts Per Trillion Drinking Water Standard

EPA finalised the first-ever national drinking water Maximum Contaminant Levels for PFOA and PFOS at 4 parts per trillion (April 2024). Water utilities face estimated $1.5bn+ annual compliance costs. Triggers massive liability for PFAS polluters. VER
