Knowledge Cutoff & Scope Notes

Hub Knowledge Cutoff: May 2025

This Environmental Liability (EP) hub aggregates data and analysis through May 2025. The following categories move rapidly and may be out of date:

  • PFAS Settlements & Litigation: New claims filed, settlement amounts, class action expansions occur monthly. Camp Lejeune claims tracking changes weekly. June 2023 major settlements (3M, DuPont) are last verified major events.
  • EPA Regulatory Developments: PFAS MCL set April 2024. Future EPA rulemakings (GenX, ITRC guidance updates, state-level PFAS laws) may supersede hub content.
  • State Cleanup Programme Updates: Each state maintains environmental site registries & cleanup timelines. Hub references typical state practices; individual state regimes vary significantly.
  • EIL Market Rate Data: Rates & combined ratios derived from broker market commentary & analyst reports. Syndicate-by-syndicate pricing is proprietary; hub reflects aggregate market trends only.
  • Lloyd's Syndicate-Specific Exclusions: Individual syndicates may have unique PFAS exclusion language. No Lloyd's bulletin mandating specific wording exists as of May 2025; consult individual market terms.

Six Data Gap Disclosures

GAP 01: Lloyd's Environmental Liability GWP by Code GAP

Issue: Lloyd's does not publish syndicate-level premium data by Lloyd's code. Environmental Liability (EP) written premium is not separately disclosed in public reports.

Hub Estimate: Global EIL market ~$3bn GWP (VER from broker commentary). Lloyd's estimated 10–15% share = ~$300–450m. EST

Implication: Rate discussions & market cycle analysis rely on indirect market commentary rather than official Lloyd's data.

GAP 02: Total PFAS Liability — Industry Estimates & Range GAP

Issue: Total industry PFAS liability is highly uncertain. Estimates range from $100bn to $1 trn+ depending on scope (groundwater only vs soil vs all affected industries).

Hub Estimate: $400bn+ over 20 years is analyst consensus midpoint. EST

Sensitivity: Camp Lejeune alone could drive $6–21bn of that. 3M settlement ($10.3bn) & DuPont ($1.185bn) are verified; future settlements unknown.

Implication: PFAS tail risk is material but unquantifiable. Exclusions in EIL policies are justified by this uncertainty.

GAP 03: EIL Rate Benchmarks by Segment & Carrier GAP

Issue: Individual carrier & syndicate rates are commercially sensitive. Lloyd's does not publish rate schedules; rates vary by broker relationships, risk profile & individual insured track record.

Hub Estimates: Rate grids in Module 06 derived from secondary sources (broker reports, analyst commentary). EST

Implication: Actual quotes will vary ±20–30% from hub benchmarks. Use for educational context only; broker placement required for current pricing.

GAP 04: EU ELD Claim Data & Enforcement Quantification GAP

Issue: The EU Environmental Liability Directive is admin/regulatory only (no private third-party liability under ELD itself). European Commission publishes limited enforcement data. Individual member state claim experience varies widely.

Hub Estimate: EU ELD losses estimated at 5–10% of US CERCLA severity (directional only). EST

Implication: European EIL is developing market; claims experience data sparse. Lloyd's is expanding coverage but without deep historical loss runs.

GAP 05: Camp Lejeune AFFF Litigation Final Liability GAP

Issue: 900,000+ AFFF claims filed at Camp Lejejeune (military base, North Carolina). PFAS settlement range $6–21bn reflects extreme uncertainty in ultimate exposure (claim invalidity rates, settlement dynamics, regulatory cost allocation).

Status (May 2025): Litigation ongoing. Settlements partial; final liability unknown. EST

Implication: Single largest PFAS exposure in market; outcome will reshape EIL pricing & capacity for military/base-adjacent exposures.

GAP 06: Lloyd's Syndicate-Specific PFAS Exclusion Scope GAP

Issue: PFAS exclusions are now standard in Lloyd's market (2024–2026). However, specific wording varies by syndicate. No Lloyd's bulletin mandates unified language; each syndicate maintains proprietary exclusion wording.

Status (May 2025): Hub notes general trend (PFAS excluded or sublimited); specific policy language review required at underwriting. EST

Implication: Brokers must review individual syndicate wordings. "PFAS exclusion" does not mean identical coverage across market.
