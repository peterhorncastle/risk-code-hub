What is Upstream / Offshore Energy Insurance?

Upstream energy insurance covers the exploration, development and production of oil and gas resources — primarily in offshore environments. It is one of the most technically complex classes in the Lloyd's market, requiring deep understanding of engineering, geophysics, environmental exposure and international regulatory regimes.

The class encompasses physical damage to platforms, FPSOs, drilling rigs, subsea infrastructure and marine support vessels; operators extra expense and control of well coverage for blowout events; third party liability arising from offshore operations; and construction all-risks for new offshore projects.

Lloyd's has been the primary global market for offshore energy insurance since the North Sea opened for commercial exploration in the 1960s, and remains the market of choice for complex, high-value risks that cannot be placed elsewhere.

⛽ Physical Damage (Property)

Covers direct physical loss or damage to offshore structures including fixed platforms, FPSOs, jack-up and semi-submersible rigs, drillships, subsea equipment, pipelines and umbilicals.

🔥 Operators Extra Expense (OEE) / Control of Well

Covers the cost of regaining control of a well following a blowout — well control costs, re-drill, seepage and pollution, and restoration of the well site. The most volatile coverage in the class.

⚖️ Offshore Liability

Third party bodily injury and property damage liability arising from offshore operations, including pollution liability, wreck removal, crane liability and mob/demob risks.

🏗️ Energy Construction (Offshore)

Contractors all risks (CAR) / marine all risks for the construction, installation and commissioning of offshore energy assets — platforms, FPSOs, subsea systems and pipelines.

🌊 Wreck Removal & Pollution

Covers the compulsory removal of wrecked or stranded offshore structures and associated pollution cleanup, increasingly driven by regulatory requirements in the North Sea and GoM.

⚙️ Loss of Production Income (LOPI)

Business interruption cover for loss of production revenue following physical damage — typically triggered by the underlying property section and subject to a deductible period.
