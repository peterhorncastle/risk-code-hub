Quality Management Controls (for Insured Firms)

File/Matter Management

  • Regular file reviews: 6-monthly minimum
  • Limitation diary: Critical dates calendar for ALL matters
  • COLP/COFA oversight: Dedicated compliance oversight
  • Engagement letters: On every matter — scope of work defined
  • Conflict checks: Before every new matter — automated system preferred
  • Client care letters: Costs and complaints procedure documented

Outcome: files without engagement letters and missed limitation dates are #1 cause of PI claims.

Supervision Structure

  • Documented supervision policy
  • Peer review: For complex/high-value matters
  • Escalation protocol: When to seek partner sign-off
  • Mentoring: For NQs/junior associates
  • CPD records: Maintained and monitored
  • Regular team meetings: With case review
  • Incident near-miss reporting: Culture of continuous improvement
  • Risk partner/manager: For larger firms

Financial Controls

  • Client account reconciliation: Daily for larger firms, weekly minimum
  • Unexplained entries: Immediate escalation protocol
  • SRA Accounts Rules audit: Quarterly internal compliance checks (2019 rules)
  • MLCO (Money Laundering Compliance Officer): Active governance
  • Client identification records (AML): Retained 5 years

Benefit: robust financial controls reduce client account shortfall risk — primary SRA intervention trigger.

Technical Standards

  • Precedent library: Up-to-date templates
  • Research protocols: Westlaw/LexisNexis vs AI tools (currently high risk)
  • Matter risk assessment: At intake
  • Specialist sign-off: For out-of-area matters
  • Annual CPD: SRA/ICAEW minimum + firm-specific
  • Regulatory update briefings: Monthly for relevant practice areas

E4/E5 Specific Controls

Audit Quality (E4)

  • EQCR/ECQR: Engagement Quality Control Review for all PIE audits
  • Going concern procedures: ISA 570 revised — specific documentation required
  • Substantive sampling: Move away from pure controls reliance
  • Third-party confirmation: Bank letters, debtor circularisation
  • AML checks on clients: ISQC 1 requirements

Key lesson from Wirecard: physical verification of cash balances; do not rely on trustee/bank attestation alone.

Tax Advice Documentation

  • Advice letter: For all material tax advice — not just email
  • Counsel opinion: Obtained where advice is complex or borderline
  • DOTAS registration: Disclose all applicable Tax Avoidance Schemes
  • Scope limitation: Document explicitly what is OUT of scope
  • Follow-up checklist: Confirm client has implemented advice correctly
  • MTD compliance: Making Tax Digital — VAT and income tax digital records

Financial Adviser Suitability (E5)

  • Risk profile assessment: Documented, reviewed annually
  • Suitability report: For every product recommendation
  • Pension transfer: QPTS (Qualified Pension Transfer Specialist) sign-off mandatory for DB→DC
  • TVAS: Transfer Value Analysis — 3-month validity
  • DB transfer assessment: Client affordability + vulnerability assessment
  • Record retention: 10 years minimum for pension advice records (FCA PS21/3)

Complaints Handling

  • Timely acknowledgement: Within 5 business days (FCA/SRA requirement)
  • Root cause analysis (RCA): On all complaints — learning culture
  • PI insurer notification: Within policy period; check notification triggers
  • FOS/SRA referral: Manage carefully — early resolution usually better
  • Claims co-operation: Preserve all files immediately on complaint — do not edit or delete

Top 10 Risk Mitigation Actions

  1. Maintain a critical dates diary — Missed limitation periods are the single most preventable cause of legal PI claims
  2. Issue engagement letters with defined scope on EVERY matter — Courts have consistently held that scope ambiguity benefits the claimant
  3. Conduct 6-monthly file reviews for all live matters — Identify problems early when remediation is still possible
  4. For DB pension transfer advice: ensure QPTS sign-off on every case — Retain full suitability report for 10 years
  5. For statutory auditors: implement physical verification of material cash/asset balances — Do not rely solely on third-party attestation
  6. Complete AML/KYC checks before accepting any new client matter — Record source of funds for property transactions
  7. Notify PI insurer immediately on reasonable belief of a claim — Late notification is the most common policy breach
  8. Conduct annual PI programme review: Check retroactive date continuity; verify limit adequacy vs current caseload/transaction values
  9. Implement peer review for all high-value or complex matters — Supervision structure is the strongest PI underwriting signal
  10. Maintain contemporaneous DOTAS/CIOT documentation for all tax advice — Register applicable schemes promptly
