Professional Indemnity Insurance 1932–2024

Twenty major events defining the evolution of legal, accounting and financial adviser professional indemnity.

1932 Donoghue v Stevenson
House of Lords establishes "neighbour principle" and modern tort of negligence — foundational for all professional liability litigation. VER
1964 Hedley Byrne & Co v Heller [1964]
House of Lords: negligent misstatement by professionals can give rise to liability — pivotal for solicitors and accountants. Establishes "assumption of responsibility" doctrine. VER
1976 SRA Mandatory PI Introduced
Law Society (SRA predecessor) introduces mandatory professional indemnity insurance for all practising solicitors — first mandatory professional PI scheme in UK. Initial minimum £50,000. VER
1986 Big Bang & Financial Services Act
City of London deregulation opens financial services market. FSA 1986 creates regulatory framework. IFA market grows rapidly — seeds future pension mis-selling PI exposure. VER
1990 Caparo Industries v Dickman [1990]
House of Lords: auditors owe duty of care to company as a whole, NOT to individual shareholders or third-party investors. Three-part test: foreseeability, proximity, fairness. Limits audit liability scope significantly. VER
1992 Lloyd's of London Reconstruction
Lloyd's financial crisis triggers reconstruction (R&R). PI capacity contracts sharply; many Names' syndicates withdraw from professional lines. Hard market follows — PI premiums rise 40–60%. VER
1999 SRA Assigned Risks Pool (ARP)
Law Society introduces ARP as safety net for solicitors unable to obtain PI in the open market. Insurers of last resort at above-standard premium. Later reformed 2013 to reduce moral hazard. VER
2000 Hall v Simons [2000]
House of Lords abolishes barrister immunity from negligence suits for court work. All barristers can now be sued for advocacy errors. PI demand for barristers increases significantly. VER
2001 Enron & Arthur Andersen Collapse
Enron bankruptcy (Dec 2001); Arthur Andersen convicted of obstruction. Firm collapses globally. Triggers Sarbanes-Oxley 2002. Global PI rates harden 30–60% in 2002–2003. VER
2001 Financial Ombudsman Service
FOS established under Financial Services and Markets Act 2000. Consumer claims route against IFAs and financial advisers formalised. Opens floodgates for endowment mortgage mis-selling claims. VER
2007 Legal Services Act — ABS
Legal Services Act 2007 introduces Alternative Business Structures — non-lawyers can own law firms. ABS entities require SRA PI under qualifying insurer scheme. VER
2013 Jackson Reforms — PI Market Impact
Legal Aid, Sentencing and Punishment of Offenders Act 2012 (effective 2013). Abolishes referral fees; changes CFAs. Short-term reduction in claims volume. Solicitors PI premiums soften 2013–2016. VER
2016 BHS Collapse — KPMG/PwC PI Exposure
BHS collapses with £571m pension deficit. PwC fined £6.5m (record at time) for auditing Taveta group. KPMG separately implicated in BHS-related advisory. Creates template for regulatory/PI overlap. VER
2017 BSPS Pension Transfer Scandal
British Steel Pension Scheme (BSPS) restructuring — ~10,000 members advised to transfer to DC arrangements. FCA investigation: 1,600+ firms examined; estimated £2–3bn industry compensation liability. Largest systemic E5 (financial adviser PI) event in UK history. VER
2018 Carillion Collapse — KPMG PI
Carillion (UK's 2nd largest construction firm) collapses Jan 2018; £7bn debt. KPMG auditor for 19 years — signed off accounts showing "going concern". FRC investigation; KPMG fined £14.4m (2023). Significant E4 PI claims exposure. VER
2019 SRA Minimum PI Limit Increase
SRA increases minimum indemnity: £2m (sole traders/partnerships) → £3m (ABSs/LLPs). Qualifying Insurers Scheme terms updated. Run-off obligation: 6 years minimum confirmed. VER
2020 EY/Wirecard Audit Failure
Wirecard AG collapse June 2020 — €1.9bn "cash" missing. EY as auditor for 11 years; failed to detect fraud. €700m+ claims filed against EY by investors and Wirecard's insolvency administrator. Largest auditor liability case in German history. VER
2021 Manchester Building Society v Grant Thornton
Supreme Court clarifies auditor liability scope in UK — SAAMCO principle applied. GT advise hedge accounting for certain loans; accounts incorrect. £50m+ loss claimed. Court refines "scope of duty" test limiting auditor liability. VER
2022 FCA BSPS Enforcement Wave
FCA publishes final guidance; multiple firms receive enforcement notices for unsuitable BSPS pension transfer advice. PI insurers face wave of E5 claims. Some small IFA firms unable to pay compensation — FSCS compensates eligible consumers. VER
2024 ARGA/Audit Reform — FRC Replacement
UK government introduces Audit Reform Bill — FRC to be replaced by Audit, Reporting and Governance Authority (ARGA) with stronger enforcement powers. Increased auditor accountability expected to widen E4 PI exposure. PIE audit firm operational separation phased in. VER
