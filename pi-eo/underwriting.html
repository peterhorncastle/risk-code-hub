Claims-Made Basis

All PI products are written on a claims-made basis. The policy responds when a claim is first MADE against the insured during the policy period — not when the negligent act occurred. Critical: retroactive date (or "retroactive cover") determines how far back past acts are covered. Full prior acts coverage is standard for established firms. Run-off (tail) cover protects retired or dissolved firms for claims arising after policy expiry. Continuity of cover is essential — a gap in PI cover creates uninsured exposure for the gap period AND the future discovery period.

E2/E3 Legal PI Underwriting

Rating Formula

Legal PI Premium = Annual Fee Income × Base Rate × Risk Factor × Cover Adjustments

Base rate range: 0.4–3.0% of fee income (varies enormously by firm type and claims history)

Six Rating Factors

1. Work Type Mix

  • Conveyancing: 1.5–2.5× (highest risk)
  • Commercial property: 1.2–1.8×
  • Personal injury: 1.3–2.0×
  • Corporate M&A: 0.8–1.2×
  • Litigation: 1.0–1.5×
  • Private client/wills: 0.6–1.0×
  • Family law: 0.8–1.2×

Conveyancing is #1 claims generator — highest transaction volume, binary outcomes, mandatory SRA reporting.

2. Firm Size & Revenue

  • Sole trader: 1.5–2.5×
  • 2–5 partners: 1.0–1.5×
  • 6–20 partners: 0.8–1.2×
  • 21–50 partners: 0.7–1.0×
  • 51+ / Top 100: 0.6–0.9×

Larger firms have supervision structures, specialist teams and quality management.

3. Claims History

  • Clean (5+ years): 0.8–0.9×
  • 1 minor claim (<£50k): 1.0–1.15×
  • 1 significant (>£100k): 1.2–1.5×
  • Multiple claims: 1.5–2.5×
  • Adverse trend: possible declination or ARP

Severity rather than frequency is primary underwriting driver for large firms.

4. SRA Compliance Record

  • No record: 1.0×
  • Minor SRA investigation (resolved): 1.1–1.3×
  • LSPO/COFA concerns: 1.2–1.5×
  • Active SRA investigation: 1.5–2.0× or declination

COLP and COFA quality is key underwriting signal.

5. AML/Fraud Controls

  • Robust procedures: 0.95–1.0×
  • Standard procedures: 1.0×
  • Property/client money concerns: 1.2–1.5×
  • Past account issues: possible declination

SRA Accounts Rules compliance is critical — shortfalls are major PI trigger.

6. Excess Layer Loading

  • Primary (0–£2m): 100% rate
  • First excess (£2–10m): 45–65%
  • Second excess (£10–25m): 30–45%
  • Third excess (£25–50m): 20–35%
  • Upper excess (£50m+): 15–25%

Excess layers benefit from underlying retention and lower loss frequency.

E4/E5 Accountants & Financial Adviser PI Underwriting

E4 Accountant Rating

Accountants PI Premium = Annual Fee Income × Base Rate × Practice Area Factor × Audit Factor

Audit uplift: Statutory audit work adds 0.5–2.0× loading depending on: (a) PIE audits (FTSE 100/350, banks) — major loading; (b) non-PIE audits — standard loading; (c) no audit — lower base rate.

E5 Financial Adviser Rating

IFA PI Premium = AUM × Rate + Fee Income × Rate × Product Risk Factor

Highest risk: DB pension transfer advice (3–8% of turnover), structured products, with-profits. Lower risk: General investment management (ISA/GIA), protection insurance, mortgage advice.

Practice Area Risk Classification

Class Description E4 Base Rate (% fee income) E5 Base Rate (% revenue)
Class I Tax compliance only, management accounts, no audit 0.3–0.6% —
Class II Mixed practice: tax, audit (non-PIE), accounts, advisory 0.6–1.2% 0.5–1.0% (standard IFA)
Class III PIE/listed company audit, financial due diligence, insolvency 1.2–2.5% 1.5–3.5% (DB transfers, structured products)
Class IV Big 4/Top 6 excess layers; catastrophic audit liability Negotiated 3.0–8.0% (active DB transfer, SIPP)

Underwriting Process for Large Firms

Step Activity Timeline
1 Submission: proposal form + 5-year claims history + financials 4–6 weeks before renewal
2 Practice profile review: work type breakdown, staffing, supervision Week 1–2 of review
3 Claims analysis: frequency, severity, open reserves, trends Week 1–3
4 SRA/ICAEW/FRC compliance review: disciplinary history, regulatory actions Week 2–3
5 Referral to reinsurance (large programmes £25m+) Week 2–4
6 Pricing meeting: underwriter + claims + actuarial sign-off Week 3–5
7 Indicative terms issued Week 4–6
8 Final terms binding (confirmation of no material changes) Renewal date

Run-off Calculation (Legal Firms)

SRA-mandated 6-year run-off for solicitors closing practice. Run-off premium calculation:

Run-off premium = Last year's PI premium × Run-off multiplier

Run-off multiplier typically: Year 1 of tail = 200–250%; Years 1–6 aggregate = 300–400% of last annual premium.

Key drivers: work type tail (conveyancing has long tail — title defects emerge years later); average claims notification lag for legal PI = 18–36 months; limitation period for negligence = 6 years (Limitation Act 1980).

Worked Examples

Example 1 — 8-Partner London Law Firm (E2 Primary + Excess)

Firm profile: 8 equity partners; £3.5m annual fee income; 45% conveyancing, 35% commercial, 20% private client; clean claims history 7 years; SRA compliant

Primary layer (£0–£3m SRA minimum):

  • Fee income × base rate = £3.5m × 1.2% = £42,000
  • Work type adjustment: 45% conveyancing → ×1.35
  • Clean history: ×0.9
  • Premium primary layer: £42,000 × 1.35 × 0.9 = £51,030 ILLUS

Excess layer (£3m xs £3m): £42,000 × 50% = £21,000 × 1.35 × 0.9 = ~£25,500 ILLUS

Total programme: ~£76,500 ILLUS

Example 2 — Regional Top 20 Accountancy Firm (E4)

Firm profile: £12m annual fee income; 30% statutory audit (mix of PIE and non-PIE), 40% tax, 30% advisory; 2 PIE audits (FTSE SmallCap); 1 historic claim (settled £85k, 4 years ago)

Premium calculation:

  • Base rate: Class III (PIE audit) = 1.8% of fee income
  • Claims loading: 1 historic claim (×1.15)
  • Audit uplift: 2 PIE audits (×1.25)
  • Premium: £12m × 1.8% × 1.15 × 1.25 = £310,500 ILLUS

Excess layers (£10m xs £15m, Lloyd's): £25,000 ILLUS

Total: ~£335,500 ILLUS
