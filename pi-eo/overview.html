Core Coverage Types

Six primary coverage categories across the professional indemnity landscape.

Solicitors PI — E2

Covers negligence, breach of duty, errors and omissions by solicitors and law firms in providing legal advice and services. Primary perils: conveyancing errors (wrong title, missing searches, completion failures), negligent wills/probate, negligent commercial advice, missed limitation periods, undertaking breaches, dishonesty of employees (subject to fraud sublimit). Mandatory: all SRA-authorised firms must hold Qualifying Insurer-approved PI.

Barristers PI — E3

Professional indemnity for barristers and chambers. Post-Hall v Simons [2000] — barristers can be sued for negligence in court work. Coverage: negligent advice (written and oral opinions), advocacy errors (though proving causation is difficult), drafting errors (pleadings, contracts), direct access work. Bar Standards Board minimum: £500k per claim. Most chambers buy significantly higher limits. Direct access bar (no solicitor intermediary) increases exposure.

Accountants PI — E4

Covers negligent audit opinions, financial statement errors, insolvency practitioner mistakes, business advisory negligence. Key perils: negligent audit (failure to detect fraud/misstatement — Caparo; Manchester Building Society v Grant Thornton [2021]); management accounts errors; bookkeeping errors; due diligence reports. Statutory auditors (PIE auditors under FRC oversight) carry heaviest exposure. Big 4 primarily self-insure via captives.

Tax Adviser PI — E5

Professional indemnity for tax advisers, CIOT/ATT members, and FCA-regulated financial advisers. Tax advice perils: negligent tax planning, incorrect returns leading to penalties, failure to advise on reliefs, VAT errors. Financial adviser perils: pension transfer advice (BSPS, BHS — major class action exposure), unsuitable investment recommendations, drawdown advice errors, SIPP miss-selling.

Run-off Coverage

When a legal or accounting firm closes, partners/directors remain personally liable for past work for 6 years (solicitors: SRA requires 6 years minimum run-off). Run-off premium: typically 200–350% of last year's premium for 6-year tail. Lloyd's is a major run-off market — particularly for larger firms and mergers/acquisitions where legacy firms require long-tail coverage.

Excess Layer PI

Lloyd's market primarily writes excess layer PI above admitted paper (Travelers, QBE, AIG primary). Large law firms (Magic Circle, Silver Circle) and Big 4/Top 10 accountancy firms require limits of £50–500m+. Excess layers: £5m xs £10m → £10m xs £15m → £25m xs £25m → £50m xs £50m. Individual excess layer premium: 30–60% of primary rate per layer.

Regulatory Framework & Oversight

Four major regulatory bodies govern professional indemnity insurance across UK professions.

SRA — Solicitors Regulation Authority

Mandatory PI for all SRA-authorised firms. Qualifying Insurers Scheme (QI): only approved insurers can provide primary cover. Assigned Risks Pool (ARP): safety net for firms that cannot obtain cover — FSCS-funded at premium above standard rates. SRA Indemnity Rules 2013 (as amended): specify minimum terms including civil liability, dishonesty of employees, defence costs included in or additional to limit. SRA intervention: if firm closes/partner disappears, SRA intervenes and claims handled centrally.

Bar Standards Board

Barristers must hold PI meeting BSB Handbook requirements. Minimum: £500k each and every claim. Most chambers arrange group policy for all members. Direct Access barristers (no solicitor required) carry higher exposure — client-facing work without solicitor safety net. Self-employed barristers arrange own cover; chambers often buy group facility. BSB recognises Lloyd's market as qualifying insurer.

FRC / ICAEW

Statutory auditors regulated by Financial Reporting Council (FRC) — "Public Interest Entity" auditors (FTSE 100/350, banks, insurers) carry unlimited liability under Companies Act 2006. Liability Limitation Agreements (LLAs): auditors may agree proportionate liability caps with company audit committees. ICAEW monitors PI adequacy for member firms. Big 4 all self-insure at primary layer; Lloyd's market provides excess layers for Tier 2 and Tier 3 firms.

FCA

Financial advisers, IFAs and wealth managers regulated by FCA require PI as condition of authorisation. FCA COBS 6.1A: adviser charges and product recommendations must be appropriate (suitability obligation). FSCS covers eligible claims up to £85,000 if firm fails. Pension transfer advice liability: DB-to-DC transfers require qualified pension transfer specialist (QPTS); BSPS/BHS scandal created systemic exposure — over 1,600 firms investigated by FCA for unsuitable pension transfer advice 2018–2022.
