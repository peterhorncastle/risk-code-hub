PI Programme Tower Structure

Professional Indemnity excess-of-loss towers are designed to match the risk appetite and regulatory requirements of different professional classes. The primary layer sets conditions; all excess layers follow the primary policy's wording and settlement decisions.

Qualifying Insurer Principle: The SRA Qualifying Insurer Scheme mandates that solicitors' primary PI must be written on approved policy terms. Lloyd's syndicates are approved qualifying insurers. The primary policy sets conditions for all excess layers — excess insurers "follow the settlements" of the primary policy.

UK SRA Qualifying Insurer Scheme

Qualifying Insurer Status

Only SRA-approved insurers can provide primary PI cover for SRA-authorised law firms. Lloyd's is a QI. Approved Minimum Terms and Conditions (AMTC): prescribed wording covering scope, minimum limit, claims notification, dishonesty of employees, defence costs. All derogations from AMTC require SRA approval. Qualifying Insurer renewal list published annually on SRA website.

Minimum Terms & Conditions

AMTC cover must include: (a) each and every claim basis; (b) all civil liability claims arising from private legal practice; (c) all partners, principals and employees; (d) former practice run-off; (e) dishonesty of principals and employees (up to minimum limit); (f) defence costs additional to limit (or included but minimum limits increased); (g) no policyholder excess on primary layer.

Assigned Risks Pool (ARP)

Safety net for firms that cannot obtain QI cover in open market. ARP premium: typically 3–5× standard market rate. ARP cover: maximum 12 months; firm must close or obtain open-market cover. ARP managed by: SRA. Post-2013 reform: ARP firms limited — cannot take on new clients; must notify existing clients. Cost: FSCS-funded with surcharge on all QI premiums.

Run-off Requirements

All SRA-authorised firms must maintain 6-year run-off when closing. Run-off = claims-made policy continuing post-practice-closure. Run-off premium: paid from firm's assets at closure; partners jointly and severally liable for unpaid run-off premium. SRA obligation: if run-off not obtained, SRA intervenes and pursues partners individually.

Firm Type Structures

Magic Circle / US Big Law

Limits: £50–500m (total programme).

Structure: Primary admitted (UK QI or NY-admitted); Lloyd's excess layers at £25m, £50m, £100m+ intervals.

Annual premium: £1–5m+ total programme.

Key: Solicitor advocate immunity fully abolished; international offices require local admitted paper. Captive fronts increasingly used for primary retention.

Mid-Tier National Firms

Limits: £10–50m.

Primary: Travelers, Zurich, QBE, or Lloyd's facility.

Lloyd's excess: £5m+ layers.

SRA compliance: COLP/COFA reporting; annual compliance return.

Claims: Conveyancing and commercial disputes dominate.

Premium: £100k–£800k total programme.

Top 6 Accountancy Firms

Coverage: Big 4 (PwC, Deloitte, EY, KPMG) and Grant Thornton / BDO primarily self-insure at primary — captive or group fund.

Lloyd's role: High excess layers (£50–500m+); specialist PIE audit run-off; M&A transaction advisory PI.

Pricing: Highly negotiated; loss reserves disclosed to select markets under NDA.

Key: Audit liability unlimited under Companies Act 2006 unless LLA agreed.

IFA Networks / Financial Adviser Groups

Regulation: FCA-regulated; PI as condition of authorisation.

Network PI: Group policy covering appointed representatives.

Direct authorisation: Individual firm buys own PI.

BSPS era: Many insurers exclude DB pension transfer advice for new policies; run-off tail for BSPS era still running.

Premium: £5k–£200k depending on DB transfer exposure and AUM.

Illustrative Case Study ILLUS

Top-50 UK Law Firm — 30 Partners

Firm profile: 30 equity partners; £18m annual fee income; 40% commercial/corporate, 30% commercial property, 20% employment/disputes, 10% private client; 1 minor claim settled £85k (4 years ago); no SRA concerns; COLP/COFA in place.

Third excess (£10m xs £30m): Lloyd's top excess £47,600 ILLUS
Total limit: £40m per claim and in aggregate ILLUS
Total estimated premium ~£379,300 ILLUS

Lloyd's Advantages in This Structure

  1. Approved qualifying insurer: Lloyd's has QI status — can write SRA-compliant primary layer without additional approval process.
  2. Market depth for large excess layers: Unique capacity for £50m–500m+ excess layers not available from single admitted insurer.
  3. Claims expertise: Specialist financial lines and PI claims practitioners; average legal PI claim notification to resolution ~24–36 months.
  4. Run-off expertise: Long-tail run-off portfolio management — Lloyd's Society Fund backs closed syndicates' run-off obligations.
  5. Cross-border follow: Lloyd's excess policy follows primary terms for international offices, reducing programme complexity.
