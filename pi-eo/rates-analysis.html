Market Cycles

Early Lloyd's Market (1990–1992)

Lloyd's PI syndicates develop dedicated capacity. Market still relatively small. Rates moderate — 1–2% of fee income for legal PI. Limited claims data.

Lloyd's R&R Hardening (1992–1996)

Lloyd's crisis triggers PI capacity withdrawal. Mortgage fraud wave from 1990-95 drives claims. Premium rises 40–60%. ARP established. Some firms uninsurable.

Soft Market Competition (1996–2001)

New entrants (Zurich, AIG, QBE) compete aggressively for PI market. Premium falls to historical lows — 0.5–1.5% of fee income. Conveyancing claims building but not yet visible in pricing.

Post-Enron Hard Market (2001–2006)

Enron/Andersen collapse hardens E4 PI dramatically (+50–100%). WorldCom, Parmalat add to claims. Legal PI also hardens on conveyancing fraud claims. Sarbanes-Oxley creates US audit PI demand.

Jackson/Regulation Soft Phase (2007–2018)

Jackson Reforms reduce legal claims volume 2013–2016. BSPS exposed but not yet priced in. Market competes for PI premium — rates fall to near-record lows by 2017. E4 softens as Enron-era claims resolved.

BSPS/COVID Hardening (2019–2023)

BSPS pension transfer liability creates systemic E5 hardening (25–50%+). COVID conveyancing boom/bust creates E2 claims spike. EY/Wirecard hardens E4. Market rate rises 20–35% peak-to-trough.
